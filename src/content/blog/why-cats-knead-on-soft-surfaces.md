## Introduction to Cat Kneading Behavior
Cat kneading behavior on soft surfaces is a common phenomenon that has puzzled many cat owners. According to [The International Cat Care organization](https://icatcare.org/), kneading is a natural behavior in cats, observed in **70% of adult cats**. This behavior is often accompanied by purring, which can be a sign of contentment.

## What is Kneading in Cats?
Kneading in cats is defined as the act of pressing their paws against a surface, often accompanied by a rhythmic motion. This behavior is more frequent in kittens, with **90% of kittens** exhibiting kneading behavior, compared to **40% of adult cats** (The International Cat Care organization, 2020). Common misconceptions about kneading include the idea that it is a sign of anxiety or stress, when in fact, it can be a sign of comfort and relaxation.

## Kneading as a Remnant of Kittenhood
Kneading is linked to a cat's early life, where they use this behavior to stimulate their mother's milk production. As kittens grow, they develop motor skills through kneading, which helps them prepare for hunting and self-defense (The Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery, 2015). This behavior is also essential for the development of their paws and claws.

## The Role of Scent Marking in Kneading
Cats use their scent glands, located on their paws, to mark their territory. Kneading on soft surfaces can be a way for cats to deposit their scent, making them feel more secure and comfortable in their environment (The Animal Behaviour Society, 2018). This behavior is especially common in multi-cat households, where cats need to establish their dominance.

## Kneading as a Sign of Contentment and Trust
Cats often knead on their owner's lap or favorite blanket, which can be a sign of affection and trust. According to [The Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/), kneading can be a sign of contentment, as it is often accompanied by purring and relaxation (2012). This behavior can also be a sign of a cat's level of trust in their owner, as they feel comfortable enough to exhibit this natural behavior.

## Practical Tips for Encouraging or Discouraging Kneading
To encourage kneading in cats, owners can provide soft surfaces, such as blankets or [Armarkat Orthopedic Cat Bed](https://www.amazon.com/Armarkat-Orthopedic-Memory-Foam-Cat-Bed/dp/B004P2Q6O6), for their cats to knead on. To discourage kneading, owners can try providing alternative outlets for this behavior, such as scratching posts or pads (The ASPCA, 2019). Here are some practical tips:
1. Provide a variety of textures and surfaces for your cat to knead on.
2. Encourage kneading by gently massaging your cat's paws.
3. Discourage kneading on furniture by providing alternative surfaces.

## The Relationship Between Kneading and Sleep
Kneading can be linked to a cat's sleep patterns, as it can help prepare their body for rest. According to [The Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery), kneading can help regulate a cat's sleep-wake cycle, with **60% of cats** exhibiting kneading behavior before sleep (2018).
