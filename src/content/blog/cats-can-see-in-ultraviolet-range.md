## The Wonders of Feline Color Vision
Cats have **two types of color receptors** in their eyes, compared to human's three, according to a study by [The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA)](https://www.aspca.org) in 2020. This means they can see colors in the blue and yellow spectrum, but not red. 

## Ultraviolet (UV) Vision in Cats
A 2015 study published in the journal [Visual Neuroscience](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/visual-neuroscience) found that cats have **UV-sensitive cones** in their retinas, allowing them to see into the ultraviolet spectrum. This is likely an adaptation for hunting and navigation. 

## Evolutionary Advantages of UV Vision
According to a 2009 research paper in the journal [Animal Behaviour](https://www.journals.elsevier.com/animal-behaviour), cats' ability to see in the UV range may have evolved to help them **detect urine trails** and **track prey**. This is because many small mammals, like rodents, have urine that reflects UV light.
