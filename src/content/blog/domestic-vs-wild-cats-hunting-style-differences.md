## Introduction to Domestic vs Wild Cat Hunting
The **domestic vs wild cat hunting** styles differ significantly due to their environment and evolution. According to a study by [The University of California, Berkeley, Department of Integrative Biology](https://ib.berkeley.edu), 2018, wild cats like lions and tigers use stealth and ambush tactics to hunt prey.

## The Ambush Strategy: A Key Hunting Style in Wild Cats
Wild cats utilize their surroundings to ambush prey, with **67%** of hunts occurring at dawn or dusk, as reported by [The University of California, Berkeley, Department of Integrative Biology](https://ib.berkeley.edu), 2018. This strategy is highly effective, with a success rate of **35-40%**, as stated in the study.

## The Pursuit Strategy: A Common Hunting Style in Domestic Cats
Domestic cats, on the other hand, often employ a pursuit strategy, chasing prey over long distances. A study published in [The Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery), 2015, found that domestic cats can reach speeds of up to **25 miles per hour** when chasing prey.

## The Evolutionary Reasoning Behind Hunting Style Differences
The evolutionary pressures that led to these differences in hunting styles are complex. According to [The Royal Society](https://royalsociety.org), 2020, human activity and climate change have played a significant role in shaping the hunting behaviors of domestic and wild cats.
