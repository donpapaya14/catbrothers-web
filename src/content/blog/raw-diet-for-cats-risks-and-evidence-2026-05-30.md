## What is a Raw Diet for Cats?
A **raw diet for cats** refers to a type of diet that consists of uncooked, unprocessed foods, such as meat, bones, and organs. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/), there are several types of raw diets, including prey model diets, frankenprey diets, and commercial raw diets. ## Risks Associated with Raw Diets for Cats
Raw diets for cats can pose several risks, including **bacterial contamination**, **nutritional deficiencies**, and **increased risk of disease transmission**. A study by the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov/) in 2019 found that **44% of raw cat food samples** were contaminated with **Salmonella**. Additionally, a study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association (JAVMA)](https://avmajournals.avma.org/) in 2018 found that **25% of cats fed a raw diet** had **nutritional deficiencies**.

## Evidence Supporting the Benefits of Raw Diets for Cats
Despite the risks, some studies suggest that raw diets can have benefits for cats, including **improved skin and coat health** and **reduced inflammation**. A study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) in 2015 found that cats fed a raw diet had **improved skin and coat health** compared to those fed a cooked diet. Another study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19393135) in 2017 found that cats fed a raw diet had **reduced inflammation** compared to those fed a cooked diet.

## Vet Consensus on Raw Diets for Cats
The [American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)](https://www.avma.org/) recommends that cat owners **consult with their veterinarian** before feeding a raw diet to their cat. The AVMA also recommends that cat owners **handle raw meat and bones safely** to reduce the risk of bacterial contamination.

## Transitioning to a Raw Diet: Tips and Precautions
If you're considering transitioning your cat to a raw diet, here are some tips and precautions to keep in mind:
1. **Gradually introduce the new diet** over a period of 7-10 days to prevent digestive upset.
2. **Choose a high-quality raw food** that is made from fresh, wholesome ingredients.
3. **Handle raw meat and bones safely** to reduce the risk of bacterial contamination.
4. **Monitor your cat's health** closely and consult with your veterinarian if you notice any signs of illness or digestive upset.
