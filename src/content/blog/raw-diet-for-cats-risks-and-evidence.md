## What is a Raw Diet for Cats?
A **raw cat diet** consists of uncooked meat, bones, and other animal products, with the intention of mimicking a cat's natural diet. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/), a raw diet can be beneficial for cats, but it also comes with potential risks. For example, a raw cat food recipe might include **80% meat, 10% bone, and 10% organs**.

## Bacterial Contamination Risks
The [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov/) reported in 2020 that **44% of raw cat food samples** contained **Salmonella**, while **21% contained E. coli**. These pathogens can pose serious health risks to both cats and humans. To minimize the risk of bacterial contamination, it's essential to handle and store raw cat food safely.

## Nutritional Deficiencies and Inadequacies
A study by the [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu/) in 2006 found that **25% of raw cat food samples** were deficient in **vitamin D**, while **15% were deficient in calcium**. These nutritional deficiencies can lead to long-term health consequences for cats, such as **osteoporosis and kidney disease**.

## Veterinarian Consensus on Raw Diets
The [American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)](https://www.avma.org/) recommends that cat owners consult with their veterinarian before feeding a raw diet. According to the AVMA, a balanced and complete diet is essential for maintaining a cat's overall health. Here are some tips for responsible raw diet feeding:
1. Consult with a veterinarian to determine the best diet for your cat.
2. Choose a reputable raw cat food manufacturer that follows proper handling and storage procedures.
3. Handle and store raw cat food safely to minimize the risk of bacterial contamination.

## Feeding a Raw Diet Safely: Tips and Precautions
To feed a raw diet safely, follow these steps:
1. Source raw ingredients from reputable suppliers.
2. Handle and store raw cat food at **0°C to 4°C (32°F to 39°F)**.
3. Wash your hands thoroughly after handling raw cat food.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What are the benefits of a raw cat diet?
The benefits of a raw cat diet include **improved digestion** and **reduced allergy symptoms**. However, it's essential to weigh these benefits against the potential risks of bacterial contamination and nutritional deficiencies.
### How can I minimize the risk of bacterial contamination?
To minimize the risk of bacterial contamination, handle and store raw cat food safely, and choose a reputable raw cat food manufacturer.
### What are the nutritional requirements for cats?
Cats require a balanced diet that includes **protein, fat, carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals**. A raw cat diet should be formulated to meet these nutritional requirements.
### Can I make my own raw cat food?
While it's possible to make your own raw cat food, it's essential to consult with a veterinarian to ensure that the diet is balanced and complete.
### What are the signs of nutritional deficiencies in cats?
The signs of nutritional deficiencies in cats include **weakness, lethargy, and poor coat condition**. If you suspect that your cat has a nutritional deficiency, consult with a veterinarian immediately.
### How can I transition my cat to a raw diet?
To transition your cat to a raw diet, start by introducing small amounts of raw food into their existing diet, and gradually increase the proportion of raw food over time.
