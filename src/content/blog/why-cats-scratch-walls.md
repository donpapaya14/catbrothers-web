## Introduction to Cat Scratching
Cats scratching walls is a common issue many cat owners face, with **70% of cat owners** reporting that their cats engage in this behavior, according to [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/), 2020. The instinctual reasons behind cat scratching include marking territory and exercising, with cats having **scratch marks** on trees and other surfaces to communicate with other cats.

## Types of Surfaces Cats Prefer to Scratch
Cats prefer to scratch surfaces with certain characteristics, such as **texture and height**, with a study by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/), 2018, finding that cats prefer surfaces with a **rough texture** and a **height of at least 3 feet**. Here are some examples of surfaces that cats like to scratch:
* Vertical surfaces like walls and doors
* Textured surfaces like carpet and sisal rope
* Surfaces with a **height of at least 3 feet**

## Health Implications of Excessive Scratching
Excessive scratching can have health implications for cats, including **injury and infection**, with the [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org/), 2019, reporting that cats who scratch excessively are at a higher risk of **developing arthritis**. Here are some health risks associated with excessive scratching:
| Health Risk | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Injury | Cats can injure themselves while scratching, especially if they scratch **broken glass or sharp objects**.
| Infection | Cats can develop infections from scratching, especially if they scratch **dirty or contaminated surfaces**.
| Arthritis | Excessive scratching can lead to **arthritis** in cats, especially if they scratch **repeatedly**.

## Training Cats to Scratch Appropriately
Training cats to scratch appropriately can be done by redirecting them to scratch pads or posts, with the [Certified Animal Behaviorist](https://www.certifiedanimalbehaviorist.com/), 2022, recommending the following steps:
1. Place the scratch pad or post in a **visible location**.
2. Encourage your cat to scratch the pad or post by **sprinkling catnip** on it.
3. Reward your cat with **treats** when they scratch the pad or post.

## Deterrents for Cats Scratching Walls
There are several deterrents that can be used to prevent cats from scratching walls, including **sprays and tapes**, with [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com/), 2021, recommending the following:
* Spray a **deterrent spray** on the wall to discourage your cat from scratching.
* Place **double-sided tape** on the wall to make it uncomfortable for your cat to scratch.
