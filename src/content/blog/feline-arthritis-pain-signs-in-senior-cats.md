## Recognizing the Early Signs of Feline Arthritis
Feline arthritis pain signs in senior cats can be subtle, but **stiffness**, **limping**, and **decreased mobility** are common symptoms, according to [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/), 2018. Less obvious signs include changes in **appetite** and **grooming habits**.

## Pain Assessment Tools for Veterinarians and Owners
The [International Association for the Study of Pain](https://www.iasp-pain.org/) (2019) recommends using standardized pain assessment tools like the **Feline Grimace Scale** and the **Canine and Feline Pain Scale** to identify pain levels in cats. These tools help veterinarians and owners assess pain more accurately.

## Non-Pharmacological Interventions for Managing Arthritis Pain
**Physical therapy**, **weight management**, and **nutritional supplements** like **omega-3 fatty acids** and **glucosamine** can help alleviate arthritis pain in senior cats, as per the [American College of Veterinary Surgeons](https://www.acvs.org/), 2020. Here are some practical steps:
1. Provide a **warm and comfortable environment** for your cat.
2. Encourage **gentle exercise**, such as short walks or playtime.
3. Feed a **balanced diet** rich in omega-3 fatty acids and glucosamine.

## Pharmacological Options for Managing Feline Arthritis Pain
The [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org/) (2019) discusses commonly prescribed pain medications for cats, including their potential **side effects** and **contraindications**. Alternative treatments like **meloxicam** and **robenacoxib** may also be considered.

## Lifestyle Modifications to Support Senior Cat Comfort
Creating a **senior cat-friendly home environment** can help alleviate arthritis pain and improve overall comfort, as suggested by [Hill's Pet Nutrition](https://www.hillspet.com/), 2020. Here are some practical tips:
1. Provide **ramps** or **stairways** for easy access to high places.
2. Use **elevated feeding stations** to reduce strain on joints.
3. Offer **orthopedic bedding** for comfortable rest.

## Consulting with Your Veterinarian
Working with a veterinarian is crucial to develop a comprehensive **pain management plan** for your senior cat, including regular **check-ups**, **pain assessments**, and adjustments to the treatment plan as needed, according to the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org/), 2018.
