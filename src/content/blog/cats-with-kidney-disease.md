## Introduction to Kidney Disease in Cats

Kidney disease affects **15-30% of cats over age 10**, making it one of the most common feline health issues (International Cat Care, 2020). Chronic kidney disease (CKD) occurs when kidneys gradually lose function, impairing waste filtration and electrolyte balance. Diet plays a critical role in managing **feline kidney disease**, as proper nutrition can slow progression and improve quality of life.

Primary causes include aging, genetics, and conditions like hypertension. Early symptoms—increased thirst, weight loss, and poor coat quality—often go unnoticed. A **cat kidney disease diet** focuses on reducing phosphorus, moderating protein, and increasing hydration to ease kidney workload.

## Nutritional Requirements for Cats with Kidney Disease

Cats with CKD need diets with:
1. **Reduced phosphorus** (0.2-0.5% dry matter): High levels accelerate kidney damage (Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine, 2019).
2. **Moderate high-quality protein** (28-35% dry matter): Limits nitrogen waste without causing muscle loss.
3. **Added omega-3 fatty acids**: EPA/DHA (0.4-1g per 1000 kcal) reduce inflammation.
4. **Increased moisture**: Wet food or broth supplements combat dehydration.
5. **Controlled sodium**: <0.3% dry matter to manage blood pressure.

## Home Cooking for Cats with Kidney Disease

For homemade **cat kidney failure diets**, follow these steps (Academy of Veterinary Nutrition, 2021):
1. **Protein base**: 100g cooked chicken breast or 80g lean beef (provides 10g protein).
2. **Binders**: 500mg calcium carbonate per 100g meat to balance phosphorus.
3. **Supplements**: Renal support blends like [Renal Essentials for Cats en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Renal+Essentials+for+Cats&tag=vds96-20)).
4. **Hydration**: Add 50ml low-sodium broth per serving.

## Monitoring and Adjusting the Diet

Regular vet checks are crucial:
1. **Every 3 months**: Blood tests for creatinine (<2mg/dL ideal) and BUN (<30mg/dL).
2. **Urine tests**: Specific gravity >1.035 indicates adequate hydration.
3. **Weight checks**: Monthly to prevent muscle wasting.

Adjust protein/phosphorus if values worsen (American Veterinary Medical Association, 2020). Cats in late-stage CKD may need further protein reduction to 25% dry matter.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the first signs of kidney disease in cats?
Increased thirst and urination are the earliest symptoms. A University of Edinburgh study (2021) found 78% of CKD cats drank >100ml water/kg/day versus 40ml in healthy cats.

### How long can a cat live with kidney disease?
With proper **cat kidney disease diet** and care, median survival is 2-4 years after diagnosis (International Renal Interest Society, 2022). Stage 1 cats often live 3+ years.

### Is tuna bad for cats with kidney disease?
Yes, tuna is high in phosphorus (0.4g per 100g) and lacks balanced nutrients. Use only as occasional treats in <5g portions.

### Can kidney disease in cats be reversed?
No, but progression can be slowed. A Cornell University trial (2020) showed renal diets reduce disease progression by 50% compared to regular food.

### How often should I feed a cat with kidney failure?
Split meals into 4-5 small portions daily to reduce nitrogen load. Ideal feeding times: 7am, 12pm, 5pm, 10pm.

## Practical Summary

- **Test early**: Annual bloodwork for cats over 7
- **Choose wet food**: 80% moisture content minimum
- **Limit phosphorus**: <0.5% in dry matter
- **Supplement wisely**: Omega-3s (EPA/DHA 300mg daily)
- **Monitor weight**: Weigh weekly; 1-2% monthly loss is acceptable
- **Hydrate creatively**: Add water to food (1:1 ratio)
- **Vet visits**: Quarterly for stage 2-4 CKD
- **Emergency plan**: Keep unflavored Pedialyte for dehydration crises
