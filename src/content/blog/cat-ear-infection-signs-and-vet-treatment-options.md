## Subtle Signs Your Cat Has an Ear Infection
If you suspect your cat has an ear infection, look out for **excessive head rubbing**, **tilted head posture**, or **reluctance to chew hard food**. According to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), these symptoms can mimic behavioral issues, making diagnosis challenging. In 2022, they reported that **70% of cats** with ear infections exhibited these subtle signs.

## Ear Mites vs. Bacterial Infections: How to Tell
To differentiate between ear mites and bacterial infections, examine the discharge. **Black crumbs** often indicate ear mites, while **yellow pus** suggests a bacterial infection. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/) in 2021 found that **85% of cats** with ear mites had black discharge.

| Infection Type | Discharge Color/Texture |
| --- | --- |
| Ear Mites | Black crumbs |
| Bacterial | Yellow pus |

## Prescription Treatments Compared
Common medications for ear infections include **antibiotics** and **antifungals**. According to the [Veterinary Information Network](https://www.vin.com/), the cost of antibiotics can range from **$20 to $50** per treatment. **Never use OTC human drops**, as they can be toxic to cats.

## When It's an Emergency (Not Just an Infection)
If your cat exhibits **Horner's syndrome** or **loss of balance**, seek immediate veterinary attention. The [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/) organization reports that these symptoms can indicate deeper issues, such as **middle ear infections** or **brain damage**.

### Red Flags
* Horner's syndrome
* Loss of balance
* **Severe pain**
