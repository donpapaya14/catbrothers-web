## Introduction to Raw Diets
A **raw diet for cats** is a type of feeding that involves providing cats with uncooked, unprocessed foods such as meat, bones, and vegetables. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (2020)](https://www.aaha.org/), the history of raw diets dates back to the early 20th century. Current trends in feline nutrition show a growing interest in raw diets, with different types such as **prey model** and **frankenprey**.

## Nutritional Benefits and Risks
The nutritional benefits of a raw diet for cats include **high protein** and **low carbohydrate** content. However, there are also risks associated with raw diets, such as **bacterial contamination** and **nutrient deficiencies**. A study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association (2019)](https://avmajournals.avma.org/) found that 30% of raw meat samples contained **Salmonella**.

## Veterinary Consensus and Recommendations
The current veterinary consensus on raw diets for cats is that they can be a viable option, but owners should be aware of the potential risks. The [American Veterinary Medical Association (2022)](https://www.avma.org/) recommends that owners consult with a veterinarian before switching to a raw diet.

## Common Mistakes in Raw Feeding
To avoid common mistakes when feeding a raw diet to cats, owners should:
1. **Provide a balanced diet** that includes a variety of foods.
2. **Handle and store food properly** to prevent contamination.
3. **Monitor their cat's health** regularly.

## Monitoring Health and Adjusting Diets
To monitor a cat's health while on a raw diet, owners should:
1. **Track their cat's weight** and adjust the diet accordingly.
2. **Monitor their cat's stool quality** and adjust the diet if necessary.
3. **Consult with a veterinarian** regularly.

The [National Research Council (2006)](https://www.nap.edu/) recommends that cats receive a minimum of **30% protein** and **20% fat** in their diet.

## Conclusion and Future Directions
In conclusion, a raw diet for cats can be a viable option, but owners should be aware of the potential risks and take steps to mitigate them. Future research directions include studying the long-term effects of raw feeding on cat health.
