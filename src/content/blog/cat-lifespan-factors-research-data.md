## Introduction to Factors Affecting Cat Lifespan
The **factors affecting cat lifespan** are multifaceted, including genetics, diet, environment, health conditions, and age-related changes. According to the [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.aafp.org/), the average lifespan of a domestic cat is around **12-15 years**. However, with proper care and attention to these factors, many cats can live well into their late teens and even early twenties.

## Genetic Factors and Cat Lifespan
Genetics play a significant role in determining a cat's lifespan. Certain breeds, such as the **Siamese** and **Persian**, are known to have longer lifespans than others. A study by the [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.aafp.org/) in 2020 found that the average lifespan of a Siamese cat is around **15-20 years**, while that of a Persian cat is around **12-17 years**.

## Diet and Nutrition's Role in Feline Longevity
Diet and nutrition are crucial factors in determining a cat's lifespan. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) in 2018 found that **obesity** can reduce a cat's lifespan by up to **2-4 years**. On the other hand, a diet rich in **omega-3 fatty acids** and **antioxidants** can help to promote healthy aging and increase lifespan.

## Environmental Factors Affecting Cat Lifespan
Environmental factors, such as **indoor vs. outdoor living** and **exposure to pollutants**, can also impact a cat's lifespan. A study by the [National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/) in 2019 found that outdoor cats are at a higher risk of **injury and disease** than indoor cats, which can reduce their lifespan by up to **5-7 years**.

## Health Conditions and Cat Lifespan
Common health conditions, such as **kidney disease**, **diabetes**, and **hyperthyroidism**, can also affect a cat's lifespan. According to [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/), these conditions can reduce a cat's lifespan by up to **2-5 years** if left untreated.

## Age-Related Changes and Cat Lifespan
As cats age, they undergo a range of physical and cognitive changes that can affect their lifespan. A study published in [Geriatric Medicine](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4308955/) in 2015 found that **cognitive decline** can occur in cats as early as **10-12 years** of age, which can impact their quality of life and lifespan.

## Improving Your Cat's Lifespan Through Preventive Care
To improve your cat's lifespan, it's essential to provide regular **check-ups**, **vaccinations**, and **parasite control**. According to the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org/), regular check-ups can help to detect health problems early, which can improve treatment outcomes and increase lifespan. Here are some practical steps you can take:
1. Schedule regular check-ups with your veterinarian every **6-12 months**.
2. Keep your cat up-to-date on all **vaccinations** and **parasite control** measures.
3. Provide a **balanced diet** rich in **omega-3 fatty acids** and **antioxidants**.
