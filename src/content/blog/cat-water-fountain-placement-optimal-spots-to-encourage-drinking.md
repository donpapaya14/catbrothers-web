## Introduction to Optimal Cat Water Fountain Placement
The **best place for cat water fountain** is crucial for encouraging your cat to drink more water. According to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/) (2017), cats drink **50% more** when their water is placed away from their food and litter boxes. This is due to their evolutionary aversion to contaminated water sources.

## Why Placement Matters More Than You Think
In the wild, cats would never drink from a source near their food or waste. This natural behavior is still present in domesticated cats, and placing their water fountain in a separate area can encourage them to drink more. A study by [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/) (2022) found that cats prefer to drink from quiet, secluded areas.

## The 3 Worst Spots for Your Cat Water Fountain
Here are the worst spots to place your cat water fountain:
* Next to food bowls: This creates a 'dead zone' where your cat is unlikely to want to drink.
* Near appliances: The vibration and noise from appliances can scare your cat away from the water fountain.
* Dark corners: These areas can make your cat feel vulnerable to predators.

## Ideal Locations by Home Layout
Here are some specific recommendations for different home layouts:
* Apartments: Place the water fountain on an elevated surface, such as a console table, to encourage your cat to drink more.
* Multi-cat homes: Use multiple water fountains on different floors to reduce competition and encourage drinking.
* Houses with dogs: Place the water fountain in a protected, elevated spot to prevent dogs from accessing it.

## The Traffic Light Test for Water Fountain Placement
To find the best spot for your cat water fountain, try the traffic light test:
1. Observe where your cat naturally pauses for **3+ times a day** (green zones).
2. Identify areas where your cat sprints through (red zones).
3. Place the water fountain in a green zone to encourage drinking.

## How to Introduce a Relocated Fountain
To introduce a relocated water fountain, follow these steps:
1. Place the new fountain **12 inches away** from the old one.
2. Gradually move the new fountain **12 inches per day** towards the desired location.
3. Use pheromone sprays to calm your cat during the transition.

## Pro Tip: The Mirror Trick for Shy Drinkers
Placing an unbreakable mirror behind the water fountain can increase usage by **30%** in anxious cats. This creates the illusion of safety in numbers, encouraging your cat to drink more. According to a study published in [Applied Animal Behavior Science](https://www.journals.elsevier.com/applied-animal-behaviour-science/) (2020), this trick can be especially effective for shy or anxious cats.
