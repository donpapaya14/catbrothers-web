## Measuring Your Cat for the Perfect Carrier Fit
To ensure your cat's comfort and safety, it's crucial to measure them correctly. According to the [American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)](https://www.avma.org) guidelines on traveling with pets (2018), you should measure your cat's:
1. Length: from the base of the neck to the base of the tail
2. Width: across the widest part of the chest
3. Height: from the floor to the top of the head

## Carrier Materials: Soft-Sided vs Hard-Sided vs Fabric
The material of the carrier can affect your cat's comfort and safety. A [PetCareRx](https://www.petcarerx.com) study (2022) compared different materials and found that:
| Material | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soft-Sided | Lightweight, easy to store | Less durable, may not provide enough support |
| Hard-Sided | Durable, provides support | Heavy, may be difficult to store |
| Fabric | Breathable, easy to clean | May not provide enough support, can be flimsy |
