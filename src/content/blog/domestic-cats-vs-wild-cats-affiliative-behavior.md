## Introduction to Domestic Cats vs Wild Cats Affiliative Behavior
The **domestic cats vs wild cats behavior** comparison is a fascinating topic, with research suggesting that domestic cats have undergone significant changes in their behavior due to artificial selection and genetic changes. According to Scott & Fuller (1965), a study on domestic animal behavior found that selection for friendliness in companion dogs can also be applied to domestic cats, resulting in increased affection towards humans.

## Why Domestic Cats are More Affectionate than Wild Cats
Domestic cats have evolved to be more affectionate towards humans due to thousands of years of domestication. This is evident in a study by [University of California](https://www.uc.edu), which found that domestic cats have a **23%** increase in oxytocin receptors, the hormone responsible for social bonding, compared to their wild counterparts. As noted by [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int), the domestication of animals has led to significant changes in their behavior and physiology.

## The Role of Sensory Deprivation in Domestic Cat Affection
Sensory deprivation, such as reduced natural light and limited access to wild prey, may contribute to domestic cats' increased dependence on human interaction. A study by Bekoff (2001) found that domestic cats that are deprived of natural light and prey exhibit **increased stress levels** and **decreased socialization**. In contrast, domestic cats that are provided with ample natural light and stimulation exhibit **reduced stress levels** and **increased socialization**.

## Differences in Socialization and Imprinting
The socialization and imprinting processes in domestic and wild cats differ significantly, leading to distinct personalities and attachment styles. A study by Lloyd (1983) found that domestic cats that are socialized to humans from an early age exhibit **increased attachment** and **affection** towards their owners. In contrast, wild cats that are not socialized to humans exhibit **decreased attachment** and **increased aggression**.

## The Impact of Human Interaction on Domestic Cat Development
Human interaction has a significant impact on the development of domestic cats, including changes in brain structure and behavior. A study by Bekoff (2002) found that domestic cats that are provided with **consistent and positive human interaction** exhibit **increased cognitive development** and **improved social skills**. In contrast, domestic cats that are deprived of human interaction exhibit **decreased cognitive development** and **impaired social skills**.
