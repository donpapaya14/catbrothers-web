## Introduction to Feline Vision
Cats' eyes are structured differently from humans, with a **tapetum lucidum** that reflects light back into the eye, enhancing their ability to see in low light conditions, as explained by the [American College of Veterinary Ophthalmologists](https://www.acvo.org). This, combined with a wider field of vision, makes their visual experience unique.

## Visual Acuity Comparison
When comparing **cat vision vs human vision**, it's clear that cats have lower visual acuity, with 20/100 to 20/200 vision, while humans have 20/20 vision, according to the [National Institutes of Health](https://www.nih.gov). This affects their daily behavior, with cats relying more on their other senses.

## Color Vision Differences
Cats see **blues and violets** more clearly but have limited **red vision**, as noted in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com). This impacts their hunting and interaction with their environment, with specific examples including their ability to see prey in certain lighting conditions.

## Hearing Range Comparison
Cats can hear sounds up to **79 kHz**, while humans can only hear up to **20 kHz**, as stated in [The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America](https://asa.scitation.org). This affects their reaction to high-pitched noises, with cats being more sensitive to certain frequencies.

## Smell Sensitivity Comparison
Cats have **200 million odor sensors** in their noses, compared to only **6 million** in humans, according to [Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com). This affects their ability to detect pheromones and scents, with cats being more sensitive to certain smells.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the difference between cat vision and human vision?
The main difference is the structure of the eye, with cats having a tapetum lucidum that enhances their low-light vision. According to the [American College of Veterinary Ophthalmologists](https://www.acvo.org), this unique structure allows cats to see better in low-light conditions.
### How do cats' senses compare to humans?
Cats have a wider field of vision, can hear higher frequencies, and have a more sensitive sense of smell, with **200 million odor sensors** compared to humans' **6 million**, as noted in [Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com).

It's recommended to play with your cat for at least **15-20 minutes** per day, using a variety of toys and activities to keep them stimulated.
