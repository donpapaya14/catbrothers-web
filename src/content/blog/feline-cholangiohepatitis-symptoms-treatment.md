## What is Feline Cholangiohepatitis?
Feline cholangiohepatitis symptoms treatment is a crucial aspect of managing **feline liver disease**. According to the American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA) in 2015, feline cholangiohepatitis affects approximately **1.4%** of the domestic cat population. The disease is more prevalent in **middle-aged to older cats**, with a median age of **10.4 years**.

## Symptoms of Feline Cholangiohepatitis
Common symptoms of feline cholangiohepatitis include **vomiting**, **diarrhea**, and **loss of appetite**. More specific indicators include **icterus (jaundice)** and **lethargy**, as reported in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery in 2018. A study published in the same journal found that **63%** of affected cats exhibited vomiting, while **45%** showed signs of diarrhea.

## Causes and Risk Factors of Feline Cholangiohepatitis
Underlying causes of feline cholangiohepatitis include **bacterial or viral infections**, as well as risk factors like **age**, **sex**, and **breed**. Research by the University of California, Davis in 2020 found that **Siamese and Abyssinian breeds** are more susceptible to the disease. Additionally, **male cats** are more likely to be affected than females, with a **male-to-female ratio** of **1.3:1**.

## Diagnosis and Testing for Feline Cholangiohepatitis
Diagnostic procedures for feline cholangiohepatitis include **blood tests**, **imaging studies**, and **liver biopsies**. The Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine in 2017 emphasized the importance of ruling out other conditions that may mimic the disease. A study published in the same journal found that **ultrasound** is a useful diagnostic tool, with a **sensitivity** of **85%** and a **specificity** of **90%**.

## Treatment and Management of Feline Cholangiohepatitis
Available treatment options for feline cholangiohepatitis include **antibiotics**, **anti-inflammatory medications**, and **supportive care**. The European Society of Veterinary Internal Medicine in 2019 recommended a **combination therapy** approach, which includes:
1. **Antibiotics** to manage bacterial infections
2. **Anti-inflammatory medications** to reduce inflammation
3. **Supportive care**, such as fluid therapy and nutritional support

## Prognosis and Prevention of Feline Cholangiohepatitis
The outlook for cats diagnosed with feline cholangiohepatitis depends on various factors, including the severity of the disease and the effectiveness of treatment. According to the Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine in 2016, **early intervention** and **long-term management** are crucial for improving prognosis. Preventative measures include **vaccination** and maintaining a **healthy lifestyle**, with a **balanced diet** and regular **exercise**.
