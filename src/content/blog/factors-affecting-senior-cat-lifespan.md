## Section 1: Factors Affecting Senior Cat Lifespan
The **Senior Cat Lifespan Factors** are crucial in determining the quality and length of life for older felines. According to a study by the [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.aafp.org/), genetics, lifestyle, and health conditions are the top factors influencing a senior cat's lifespan. For instance, a study published in 2020 found that **genetics** account for up to 30% of a cat's lifespan.

## Section 2: Impact of Weight on Senior Cat Lifespan
Weight plays a significant role in determining a senior cat's lifespan. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) in 2018 found that **obese cats** have a shorter lifespan compared to their normal-weight counterparts. The study revealed that obesity can reduce a cat's lifespan by up to 2 years.

## Section 3: Effect of Nutrition on Senior Cat Lifespan
**Nutrition** is essential in maintaining a senior cat's lifespan. A study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) in 2019 found that cats fed a **balanced diet** had a longer lifespan compared to those fed an unbalanced diet. The study recommended that senior cats be fed a diet rich in **protein** and **omega-3 fatty acids**.

## Section 4: Common Health Conditions Affecting Senior Cat Lifespan
Senior cats are prone to various health conditions that can impact their lifespan. According to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) in 2020, the most common health conditions affecting senior cats include **arthritis**, **kidney disease**, and **diabetes**. Regular check-ups with a veterinarian can help identify these conditions early on.

## Section 5: Managing Senior Cat Health to Increase Lifespan
To increase a senior cat's lifespan, it's essential to manage their health effectively. Here are some practical tips:
1. **Regular check-ups**: Schedule regular check-ups with a veterinarian to monitor your cat's health.
2. **Balanced diet**: Feed your cat a balanced diet rich in protein and omega-3 fatty acids.
3. **Regular exercise**: Engage your cat in regular exercise, such as playtime with toys.
4. **Weight management**: Monitor your cat's weight and take steps to prevent obesity.
5. **Stress reduction**: Reduce stress in your cat's life by providing a comfortable and calm environment.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How many years do senior cats live?
Senior cats can live up to 15-20 years, depending on various factors such as genetics, lifestyle, and health conditions. According to a study by the [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.aafp.org/), the average lifespan of a senior cat is around 12-15 years.
### What are the most common health conditions affecting senior cats?
The most common health conditions affecting senior cats include **arthritis**, **kidney disease**, and **diabetes**. Regular check-ups with a veterinarian can help identify these conditions early on.
### How often should I feed my senior cat?
Senior cats should be fed 2-3 times a day, depending on their individual needs and health conditions. It's essential to consult with a veterinarian to determine the best feeding schedule for your cat.
### Can senior cats still play and exercise?
Yes, senior cats can still play and exercise, but their activity level may decrease with age. Engage your cat in gentle play, such as playtime with toys, to keep them active and stimulated.
