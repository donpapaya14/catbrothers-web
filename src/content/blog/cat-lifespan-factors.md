## Genetics and Breed

Genetics play a crucial role in determining a cat's lifespan, with some breeds living significantly longer than others. According to the **American Veterinary Medical Association (2020)**, the average cat lifespan ranges from **13 to 17 years**, but certain breeds like Siamese and Burmese can live **18-20 years or more**. Conversely, breeds like the Maine Coon and Ragdoll have shorter lifespans, averaging **10-13 years** due to genetic predispositions to heart disease and other conditions.

**Key Data:**
- Siamese cats: **18-20 years** (AVMA, 2020)
- Maine Coon: **10-13 years** (AVMA, 2020)
- Mixed-breed cats: **14-16 years** (AVMA, 2020)

**Actionable Advice:**
1. Research breed-specific health risks before adopting.
2. Opt for regular genetic testing if adopting a purebred cat.
3. Monitor for breed-specific conditions (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy in Maine Coons).

## Nutrition and Diet

A cat's diet directly impacts its longevity. The **Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)** found that cats fed high-protein, low-carb diets lived **1.5-2 years longer** than those on carbohydrate-heavy diets. Overfeeding can lead to obesity, which reduces lifespan by **up to 2.5 years**, while underfeeding increases the risk of malnutrition-related diseases.

## Lifestyle and Environment

Indoor cats live **3-5 years longer** than outdoor cats due to reduced exposure to toxins, predators, and accidents (**Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine, 2019**). Stress from unstable environments can also shorten lifespan by **1-2 years**.

**Key Data:**
- Indoor cats: **13-17 years**
- Outdoor cats: **8-10 years** (JVIM, 2019)

**Actionable Advice:**
1. Keep cats indoors or supervise outdoor time.
2. Use **Feliway diffusers** to reduce stress.
3. Avoid toxic plants (e.g., lilies) and household chemicals.

## Health Conditions and Diseases

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) affects **30% of cats over 10 years** and reduces lifespan by **3-5 years** if untreated (**International Cat Care, 2022**). Early detection through bloodwork can extend life expectancy significantly.

**Key Data:**
- CKD prevalence: **30% in senior cats** (ICC, 2022)
- Diabetes reduces lifespan by **2-3 years** (ICC, 2022)

**Actionable Advice:**

## Veterinary Care and Prevention

Regular vet visits increase lifespan by **2-4 years** (**CDC, 2021**). Vaccinations prevent fatal diseases like feline leukemia, which kills **85% of infected cats within 3 years**.

**Key Data:**
- Annual vet visits: **+2-4 years lifespan** (CDC, 2021)
- Feline leukemia mortality: **85% in 3 years** (CDC, 2021)

**Actionable Advice:**
1. Schedule **biannual check-ups for seniors**.
2. Keep vaccinations updated (core vaccines every **1-3 years**).
3. Use flea/tick prevention monthly.

## Aging and Senior Cat Care

Cats over **11 years** need specialized care. The **American Animal Hospital Association (2020)** recommends **bi-annual vet visits**, joint supplements, and soft bedding to manage arthritis, which affects **90% of cats over 12**.

**Key Data:**
- Arthritis prevalence: **90% in seniors** (AAHA, 2020)
- Senior cats need **30% fewer calories** (AAHA, 2020)

**Actionable Advice:**
1. Switch to **senior-formulated food** at age 7.
2. Provide **ramps or low-sided litter boxes**.
3. Monitor for cognitive decline (e.g., nighttime vocalization).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the average lifespan of a house cat?
The average indoor cat lives **13-17 years**, according to the AVMA (2020). Genetics, diet, and veterinary care significantly influence this range.

### Do male or female cats live longer?
Female cats live **1-2 years longer** on average due to lower risks of trauma and urinary blockages (JVIM, 2019).

### How can I make my cat live longer?
1. Keep them at a **healthy weight** (BMI 4-5).
2. Schedule **vet visits 2x/year after age 7**.
3. Feed **high-protein, low-carb diets** (JFMS, 2018).

### What cat breed lives the longest?
Siamese and Burmese cats often live **18-20+ years**, while Maine Coons average **10-13 years** (AVMA, 2020).

### Is wet or dry food better for longevity?
Wet food is superior for **kidney health** (higher moisture), but dry food helps **dental health**. A **mix of both** is ideal (JFMS, 2018).
