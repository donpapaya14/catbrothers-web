## Introduction to Oldest Cat Record
The **oldest cat ever recorded** was **Creme Puff**, who lived an astonishing **38 years and 3 days** (1967–2005), as verified by [Guinness World Records (2019)](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com). Owned by Jake Perry of Austin, Texas, this mixed-breed cat surpassed the previous record holder (Granpa, 34 years) by a significant margin. Perry attributed her longevity to a unique diet including bacon, eggs, broccoli, and coffee with cream—though veterinarians caution this regimen isn't universally advisable.

Key data points:
- **38 years**: Longest confirmed feline lifespan ([Guinness World Records, 2019](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com))
- **34 years**: Previous record holder (Granpa Rex Allen)
- **15–20 years**: Average lifespan of domestic cats ([International Cat Care, 2020](https://icatcare.org))

## Diet and Lifestyle of the Oldest Cat
Creme Puff’s unconventional diet included:
1. **Protein-rich foods**: Eggs, turkey, and bacon
2. **Vegetables**: Steamed broccoli and asparagus
3. **Dairy**: Heavy cream in coffee (occasionally)

While her diet was atypical, research suggests **controlled portions and hydration** are critical. A [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)](https://journals.sagepub.com) study found cats fed **measured meals 2–3x daily** lived 1.8 years longer than free-fed cats. Perry also provided:
- **Structured playtime**: 20-minute daily sessions
- **Stress-free environment**: Quiet spaces and routine vet visits

## Genetics and Longevity in Cats
Genetics account for **30–50%** of lifespan variation, per the [National Center for Biotechnology Information (2020)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov). Key findings:

| Breed | Average Lifespan | Longevity Factors |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|
| Siamese | 15–20 years | Low genetic disease risk |
| Maine Coon | 12–15 years | Prone to hypertrophic cardiomyopathy |
| Mixed Breed | 13–17 years | Higher genetic diversity |

## Healthcare and Veterinary Care for Long-Lived Cats
The [American Animal Hospital Association (2022)](https://www.aaha.org) recommends:
1. **Bi-annual checkups**: For cats over 7 years
2. **Vaccinations**: Core vaccines (e.g., rabies, FVRCP) every 3 years
3. **Dental care**: Annual cleanings reduce systemic inflammation by **60%**

Early detection of chronic kidney disease (affecting **30%** of senior cats) can extend life by **2–4 years** ([Cornell Feline Health Center, 2021](https://www.vet.cornell.edu)).

## Tips for Promoting Longevity in Your Cat
From [Cornell University (2021)](https://www.vet.cornell.edu):
1. **Portion control**: Feed **20–30 calories per pound daily**
2. **Hydration**: Provide **1 oz of water per pound** of body weight
3. **Enrichment**: Rotate toys weekly to prevent boredom
4. **Litter hygiene**: Scoop 2x daily; deep-clean monthly

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the oldest cat alive today?
As of 2023, **Flossie** (born 1995) holds the Guinness record at **27 years**. Her caretakers credit a **high-protein diet** and **stress-minimized environment**.

### Do indoor cats live longer than outdoor cats?
Yes. Indoor cats average **13–17 years**, while outdoor cats live **2–5 years less** due to accidents and disease ([International Cat Care, 2020](https://icatcare.org)).

### What cat breed lives the longest?
**Siamese and Burmese** breeds often reach **18–20 years**, while **mixed breeds** benefit from genetic diversity ([NCBI, 2020](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov)).

### How can I tell if my cat is aging well?
Signs include **consistent weight**, **clear eyes**, and **regular grooming**. Annual blood tests after age 10 detect **80% of age-related issues** early ([AAHA, 2022](https://www.aaha.org)).

### Is wet or dry food better for longevity?
**Wet food** is preferable—it’s **70% water**, reducing kidney strain. Combine with **dental treats** to offset lower abrasion ([Cornell, 2021](https://www.vet.cornell.edu)).
