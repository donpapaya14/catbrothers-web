## Introduction to Wild Cat Hunting
Wild cat hunting behavior is a fascinating topic, with **stalk-and-pounce tactics** and **stealth** playing crucial roles. According to [National Geographic](https://www.nationalgeographic.org), a study in 2019 found that wild cats spend up to **70%** of their time stalking prey. This behavior is essential for their survival, as it allows them to conserve energy and increase their hunting success.

## Domestic Cat Hunting Comparison
Domestic cat hunting behavior differs from that of wild cats, with **motivation** and **technique** being key differences. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com) in 2017 found that domestic cats are more likely to hunt for **recreation** rather than survival. The study also noted that domestic cats use **less stealth** and **more aggression** when hunting.
