## Oldest Cat Guinness Record
### Introduction to the Record Holder

Creme Puff, a domestic shorthair cat, holds the Guinness World Record for the oldest cat ever recorded. Born on August 3, 1967, and passing away on August 6, 2005, at the age of 38 years and 3 days, Creme Puff's longevity is a remarkable achievement. According to the Guinness World Records 2016, Creme Puff's record was verified by her owner, Jake Perry, who provided documentation of her birth and death dates [1].

### Nutrition and Lifestyle of the Longest Living Cat

Creme Puff's diet and living conditions played a significant role in her extraordinary longevity. Her owner, Jake Perry, reported that Creme Puff was fed a diet of dry food and occasionally received treats. A study published in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery in 2018 found that cats fed dry food had a lower risk of obesity and related health issues compared to those fed wet food [2]. Regular veterinary check-ups and preventative care also contributed to Creme Puff's long lifespan.

### Genetic Factors in Feline Longevity

Research suggests that genetics may play a role in feline longevity. A study published in the American Journal of Veterinary Research in 2020 found that certain genetic variants were associated with increased lifespan in cats [3]. However, more research is needed to fully understand the relationship between genetics and feline longevity.

### Healthcare and Veterinary Care of Aging Cats

As cats age, regular veterinary check-ups and preventative care become increasingly important. The American Animal Hospital Association recommends that cats receive regular check-ups, vaccinations, and parasite control measures to maintain their health and well-being [4]. Additionally, cats may require more frequent dental care and may benefit from a senior-specific diet.

### Comparison with Average Feline Lifespan

The average lifespan of a domestic cat is around 12-15 years. However, with proper care and nutrition, many cats can live well into their late teens and even early twenties. A study published in the Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association in 2017 found that cats that received regular veterinary care and were fed a balanced diet had a significantly longer lifespan compared to those that did not [5].
