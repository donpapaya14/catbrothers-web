## Introduction to Domestic Cats Kneading
Domestic cats kneading is a common behavior observed in **domestic cats**, but rarely seen in their wild counterparts. According to a study published in the [International Journal of Comparative Psychology](https://www.comparativepsychology.org/), 2018, kneading is characterized by the rhythmic pushing of paws against soft surfaces.

## What is Kneading and How Do Domestic Cats Do It?
Kneading involves **domestic cats** using their paws to apply pressure on a surface, often accompanied by **purring** and **stretching**. This behavior is most commonly observed in kittens, who use kneading to stimulate milk flow from their mothers. As reported by the [International Journal of Comparative Psychology](https://www.comparativepsychology.org/), 2018, domestic cats continue to exhibit this behavior into adulthood.

## The Evolutionary Purpose of Kneading in Kittens
Kneading originated as a nursing behavior to stimulate milk flow in wildcat ancestors. Studies on felid development, published in the [Journal of Mammalogy](https://academic.oup.com/jmammal), 2020, suggest that this behavior is essential for the survival of kittens. The retention of this behavior in domestic cats can be attributed to **neoteny**, the retention of juvenile traits in adults.

## Why Adult Domestic Cats Retain Kneading Behavior
Adult domestic cats retain kneading behavior due to neoteny, which is a result of domestication. As stated in the [Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences](https://www.pnas.org/), 2019, domestication leads to changes in brain chemistry, including increased levels of **serotonin** and **oxytocin**, which contribute to the retention of juvenile behaviors.

## The Absence of Kneading in Adult Wild Cats
In contrast to domestic cats, adult wild cats lose kneading behavior after weaning. Observations of lions, tigers, and leopards, published in the [African Journal of Ecology](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/1365297x), 2021, show that these animals do not exhibit kneading behavior in adulthood.

## How Domestication Changed Feline Brain Chemistry
Domestication has led to significant changes in feline brain chemistry, including differences in **serotonin** and **oxytocin** levels. Research published in the [Animal Behaviour Journal](https://www.journals.elsevier.com/animal-behaviour), 2022, suggests that these changes contribute to the retention of juvenile behaviors, including kneading.

## Practical Implications for Cat Owners
Cat owners can interpret kneading behavior as a sign of **comfort** and **relaxation**. However, it can also indicate **stress** or **anxiety**. According to the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org/), 2023, owners can respond to kneading behavior by providing a comfortable environment and reducing stressors.

Here are some practical steps for cat owners:
1. Provide a **comfortable** and **quiet** space for your cat to knead.
2. Offer a variety of **textured surfaces** for your cat to knead on.
3. Reduce **stress** and **anxiety** by providing a stable and predictable environment.
