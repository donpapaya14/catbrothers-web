## Understanding AVMA Vaccination Guidelines
The American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA) vaccination guidelines for cats emphasize the importance of **core vaccinations** for all cats, regardless of lifestyle. According to the [AVMA 2022 Vaccination Guidelines for Cats](https://www.avma.org/), core vaccinations include **rabies**, **feline viral rhinotracheitis**, **feline calicivirus**, and **panleukopenia**. These vaccinations are crucial for protecting cats against life-threatening diseases.

## Core Vaccinations for Kittens and Adult Cats
The core vaccinations for kittens and adult cats are administered in a series of doses, with the first dose typically given at **6-8 weeks of age**. The recommended schedule includes:
1. First dose: 6-8 weeks
2. Second dose: 10-12 weeks
3. Third dose: 14-16 weeks
As stated by the [Core Vaccinations for Cats (AVMA, 2022)](https://www.avma.org/), adult cats require **booster shots** every 1-3 years, depending on the vaccine and the cat's lifestyle.

## Non-Core Vaccinations: Risks and Benefits
Non-core vaccinations, such as **feline leukemia virus (FeLV)** and **feline immunodeficiency virus (FIV)**, are recommended for cats at risk of exposure. According to the [Non-Core Vaccinations for Cats (AVMA, 2022)](https://www.avma.org/), the risks and benefits of administering non-core vaccinations should be carefully considered, taking into account factors such as the cat's lifestyle, health status, and potential exposure to diseases.

## Vaccination Schedule for Indoor and Outdoor Cats
The vaccination schedule for indoor and outdoor cats differs, with outdoor cats requiring more frequent vaccinations due to increased exposure to diseases. The [Vaccination Schedule for Indoor and Outdoor Cats (AVMA, 2022)](https://www.avma.org/) recommends the following schedule:
| Cat Type | Vaccination Schedule |
| --- | --- |
| Indoor | Core vaccinations every 1-3 years |
| Outdoor | Core vaccinations every 1 year, non-core vaccinations as needed |

## Common Vaccine Reactions and Side Effects
Common vaccine reactions and side effects in cats include **mild fever**, **lethargy**, and **swelling at the injection site**. According to the [Vaccine Reactions and Side Effects (AVMA, 2022)](https://www.avma.org/), severe reactions are rare, but can include **anaphylaxis** and **seizures**.

## Boosters and Revaccinations: When and Why
Boosters and revaccinations are essential for maintaining immunity against diseases. The [Boosters and Revaccinations for Cats (AVMA, 2022)](https://www.avma.org/) recommends the following schedule:
1. Booster shots every 1-3 years
2. Revaccination every 1-3 years, depending on the vaccine and the cat's lifestyle
