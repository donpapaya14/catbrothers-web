## Introduction to Raw Diets

A **raw diet for cats** consists of uncooked meat, organs, and bones, mimicking their natural prey. The American Animal Hospital Association (2019) categorizes raw diets into two main types: **prey model** (whole animal parts) and **commercial raw foods** (pre-packaged, balanced formulas). While proponents argue it aligns with feline evolutionary needs, veterinary consensus remains divided on safety and nutritional adequacy.

## Nutritional Benefits and Risks

Raw diets provide **high-protein, high-moisture** content, with 70-80% protein in prey-model diets (Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association, 2018). However, risks include:
1. **Bacterial contamination**: 20-35% of raw diets test positive for Salmonella or E. coli (CDC, 2020).
2. **Nutritional imbalances**: Homemade raw diets often lack taurine (critical for heart health) or calcium-phosphorus ratios, leading to 12% higher risk of metabolic bone disease in kittens (Tufts University, 2021).

## Vet Consensus and Recommendations

The Veterinary Information Network (2020) surveyed 500 veterinarians: **67% discourage raw diets** due to contamination risks, while 22% approve **commercial raw foods** with safety certifications. Key guidelines:
1. **Avoid homemade raw diets** unless formulated by a veterinary nutritionist.
2. Choose **high-pressure processed (HPP) commercial options** like [ Stella & Chewy's Raw Frozen Cat Food en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Stella+&+Chewy's+Raw+Frozen+Cat+Food&tag=vds96-20), which reduces pathogens by 99.9%.
3. **Supplement with omega-3s** if using fish-based proteins to offset mercury exposure.

## Raw Diet and Cat Health Outcomes

A National Center for Biotechnology Information review (2022) linked raw diets to:
- **Improved digestion**: 40% fewer hairballs in cats fed raw vs. kibble.
- **Dental benefits**: 15% less periodontal disease due to chewing raw bones.
- **Controversial claims**: No conclusive evidence supports raw diets preventing cancer or diabetes.

## Safe Handling and Preparation

The CDC (2020) recommends:
1. **Freeze raw meat** at -4°F (-20°C) for 72 hours to kill parasites.
2. **Use separate utensils** and disinfect surfaces with 1:32 bleach solution.
3. **Thaw in refrigerator** (not countertop) to slow bacterial growth.
4. **Monitor stool quality**: Diarrhea or vomiting may indicate contamination.

## Conclusion and Future Directions

Current research lacks long-term studies on raw diets’ impact on feline longevity. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a raw diet better for cats than kibble?
Raw diets offer higher protein bioavailability (92% vs. 75% in kibble, per NCBI 2022), but require strict safety measures. Kibble is safer for households with immunocompromised individuals.

### Can kittens eat raw food?
Yes, but only **commercial balanced formulas** like [ Stella & Chewy's Raw Frozen Cat Food en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Stella+&+Chewy's+Raw+Frozen+Cat+Food&tag=vds96-20). Kittens need 30% more taurine than adults (AAFCO, 2021).

### How often should I feed my cat raw food?
Adult cats thrive on **2-3 meals daily**, with portions sized at 2-3% of body weight (e.g., 1.5 oz per meal for a 10 lb cat).

### Do raw diets cause parasites?
Freezing meat as per CDC guidelines eliminates 95% of parasites. Avoid wild-caught prey without freezing.

### Are raw diets expensive?
Commercial raw diets cost **$2-$5/day** vs. $0.50-$2 for kibble. Homemade versions can be cheaper but risk nutritional gaps.
