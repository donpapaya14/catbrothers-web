## Introduction to Feline Arthritis
Feline arthritis is a common condition affecting **20% of cats** over the age of 14, according to the American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA) 2020. It is caused by the wear and tear of joints, leading to **osteoarthritis**, or by an autoimmune response, resulting in **rheumatoid arthritis**. 

## Subtle Signs of Feline Arthritis Pain
Early warning signs of arthritis pain in cats include changes in behavior, appetite, and mobility. A study published in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery 2018 found that **60% of cats** with arthritis showed a reluctance to jump or climb. 

## Physical Examination and Diagnosis
Veterinarians diagnose feline arthritis through physical examination, radiographs, and other diagnostic tests. The Veterinary Information Network (VIN) 2022 recommends a combination of these methods to accurately diagnose arthritis in cats.

## Pain Management Options for Cats
Pain management options for cats with arthritis include non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), corticosteroids, and alternative therapies. According to International Cat Care 2020, **NSAIDs** are the most commonly used treatment for feline arthritis.

## Lifestyle Changes to Help Cats with Arthritis
To create a comfortable environment for cats with arthritis, owners can make lifestyle changes such as weight management, exercise, and providing easy access to food, water, and litter. Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine 2019 recommends the following steps:
1. **Feed a balanced diet** to maintain a healthy weight.
2. **Provide regular exercise**, such as short play sessions.
3. **Create a comfortable living space**, with easy access to necessities.

## Alternative Therapies for Feline Arthritis
Alternative therapies for feline arthritis include acupuncture, physical therapy, and nutritional supplements. A study published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine 2017 found that **acupuncture** can be an effective treatment for feline arthritis.
