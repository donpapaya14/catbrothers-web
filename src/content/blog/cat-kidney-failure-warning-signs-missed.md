## Hidden Warning Signs of Kidney Failure in Cats
**Cat kidney failure warning signs** can be subtle and often resemble other conditions, making them easy to miss. Common symptoms include increased water intake, weight loss, and lethargy. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) (2018) found that **75% of cats with kidney failure** presented with non-specific symptoms.

## The Role of Age and Genetics in Feline Kidney Disease
Age and genetic predisposition play a significant role in **feline kidney disease**. According to the [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu), cats over **10 years old** are at higher risk for kidney disease. A study by Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2015) found that **senior cats** are more likely to develop kidney disease due to age-related changes.

## Blood Tests and Imaging for Accurate Diagnosis
Blood tests and imaging studies are crucial for diagnosing **kidney disease** in cats. The [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org) recommends regular blood tests and imaging studies to monitor kidney function in cats. Owners should ask their vet about the following tests:
1. Complete Blood Count (CBC)
2. Blood Chemistry Profile
3. Urinalysis
4. Imaging studies (e.g., X-rays, ultrasound)

## Nutritional Management of Feline Kidney Disease
Diet plays a vital role in managing **feline kidney disease**. A study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19391355) (2019) found that a **renal-friendly diet** can slow the progression of kidney disease in cats. Key components of a renal-friendly diet include:
* Phosphorus restriction
* Limited protein intake
* Increased omega-3 fatty acids

## Common Medications That Can Harm Kidneys
Certain medications can exacerbate **kidney disease** in cats. The [FDA](https://www.fda.gov) warns that non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) can cause kidney damage in cats. Safer alternatives include:

## When to Seek Veterinary Care for Suspected Kidney Failure
If you suspect **kidney failure** in your cat, seek veterinary care immediately. Look out for the following symptoms:
1. Increased thirst and urination
2. Weight loss
3. Lethargy
4. Vomiting
5. Diarrhea
The [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org) recommends seeking veterinary care if you notice any of these symptoms.
