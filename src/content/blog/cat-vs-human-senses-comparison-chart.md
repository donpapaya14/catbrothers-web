## Introduction to Cat Senses
The **cat senses comparison chart** is a fascinating topic that highlights the unique abilities of our feline friends. When it comes to **cat vision vs human**, cats have a visual acuity of 6/5 in low light conditions, compared to humans who have a visual acuity of 6/6 in normal light conditions (Pirie, 2011). According to the [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int), visual acuity is a crucial aspect of an animal's ability to interact with its environment.

## Visual Acuity: How Cats See the World
Cats have a field of view of almost 200 degrees, compared to humans who have a field of view of around 180 degrees (Pirie, 2011). Their color vision is also limited to blues and violets, whereas humans can see a wide range of colors. In low light conditions, cats can see 6 times better than humans, thanks to their reflective retinas and large pupils (Pirie, 2011).

## Hearing Range: What Frequencies Do Cats Hear?
The **cat hearing range** is between 25 Hz and 64 kHz, which is much wider than the human hearing range of 20 Hz to 20 kHz (Heffner & Heffner, 1985). Cats can also hear sounds that are too faint for humans to detect, thanks to their sensitive ears and brain structure. According to the [National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders](https://www.nidcd.nih.gov), hearing range is an important aspect of an animal's ability to communicate and navigate its environment.

## Smell Sensitivity: How Cats Detect Odors
Cats have 200 million **olfactory receptors** in their noses, compared to only 6 million in humans (Lloyd, 2004). This makes their sense of smell much more sensitive than ours. Cats use their sense of smell to detect food, predators, and potential mates. According to the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org), smell sensitivity is an important aspect of an animal's ability to interact with its environment.
