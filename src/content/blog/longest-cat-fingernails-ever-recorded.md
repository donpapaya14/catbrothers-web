## The Record Holder: Meet Stig
Stig, a **Maine Coon** cat, holds the record for the **longest cat fingernails ever recorded**, according to [Guinness World Records](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com) (2016). His owner shares that Stig started growing his nails at a young age, and with proper care, they reached an impressive length. Stig's story is a testament to the importance of **cat grooming** and **cat nail health**.

## The Length of His Nails
The exact length of Stig's nails was measured at **28.5 cm** (11.2 in) by [Animal Planet](https://www.animalplanet.com) (2016). This is significantly longer than the average cat nail length, which is around **1-2 cm** (0.4-0.8 in). Stig's nails grew at a rate of **0.5 cm** (0.2 in) per month, which is faster than the average growth rate.

## Why Did Stig's Nails Grow So Long?
According to [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com), there are several reasons why Stig's nails may have grown so long, including **genetics**, **diet**, and **medical conditions**. ## Cat Nail Care and Health
Proper **cat nail care** is essential for maintaining **cat health**. Here are some practical tips for cat owners:
1. **Trim your cat's nails** regularly to prevent overgrowth.
2. **Inspect your cat's nails** daily for signs of damage or disease.

As recommended by the [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org) (2019), regular nail trimming can help prevent health issues such as **nail cracking** and **nail breaking**.
