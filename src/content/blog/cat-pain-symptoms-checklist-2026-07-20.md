## Introduction to Feline Pain
Cats exhibit **cat pain symptoms** differently than humans, often hiding signs of distress. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/), in 2020, it was reported that **61% of cat owners** are unable to recognize signs of pain in their cats.

## Subtle Behavioral Changes
Changes in appetite, sleep patterns, and social interaction may indicate **feline pain management** issues. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) in 2019 found that **45% of cats** with chronic pain exhibited decreased appetite.

Here are some subtle behavioral changes to look out for:
1. Decreased appetite
2. Changes in sleep patterns
3. Withdrawal from social interaction

## Physical Signs of Discomfort
Specific postures, facial expressions, and vocalizations can signify **cat pain symptoms**. The [International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants](https://www.iaabc.org/) reported in 2018 that **cats in pain** often exhibit a **20% increase** in vocalization.

Some physical signs of discomfort include:
* Tucked tail
* Flattened ears
* Avoiding eye contact
