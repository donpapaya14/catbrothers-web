## Introduction to Feline Curiosity
Cats knocking things over is a common behavior observed by many pet owners. According to the **American Animal Hospital Association (2020)**, curiosity plays a significant role in feline behavior, and knocking things over can be a manifestation of this curiosity. Cats are naturally inquisitive creatures, and their environment often becomes a playground for exploration.

## Play Instinct and Hunting Behavior
The instinct to hunt and play is deeply ingrained in cats. As noted by the **Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019)**, this instinct can lead cats to view objects as potential prey, prompting them to knock them over. This behavior is especially common in younger cats or those with high energy levels.

## Attention Seeking and Communication
Cats also use knocking things over as a form of **communication and attention-seeking behavior**. As explained by **International Cat Care (2018)**, cats may knock over objects to initiate play or to signal their needs to their owners. This behavior can be particularly noticeable in cats that are not receiving enough attention or stimulation.

## Environmental Factors and Boredom
Environmental factors such as boredom, lack of stimulation, and territorial marking can contribute to cats knocking things over. **Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2022)** suggests that providing cats with a stimulating environment, including toys and scratching posts, can help reduce this behavior. Additionally, ensuring cats have enough space and opportunities for exercise can also play a role in minimizing knocking over of objects.

## Redirecting the Behavior
To redirect this behavior, pet owners can take several steps:
1. **Provide Adequate Stimulation**: Ensure cats have access to a variety of toys and activities to keep them engaged.
2. **Create a Cat-Friendly Environment**: Secure loose items that could be easily knocked over, and provide scratching posts to redirect scratching behavior.
3. **Use Positive Reinforcement**: Reward cats for calm behavior, using treats or praise.
As advised by the **ASPCA (2021)**, consistency and patience are key when trying to change a cat's behavior.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Why do cats knock over plants?
Cats may knock over plants due to curiosity or to use the soil as a litter box. According to **[Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org)**, cats may also be attracted to the texture or smell of the plants.
### How can I stop my cat from knocking over my vase?
To prevent your cat from knocking over a vase, place it in a secure location or use a **Cat Deterrent Spray**. You can also try distracting your cat with toys or treats when they approach the vase.
### What are the signs of boredom in cats?
Signs of boredom in cats include excessive sleeping, lack of interest in toys, and increased knocking over of objects. As noted by **[World Health Organization](https://www.who.int)**, mental stimulation is crucial for pets, just like humans.
### Can cats learn not to knock things over?
Yes, cats can learn not to knock things over through consistent training and positive reinforcement. **[International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org)** provides guidance on how to train your cat effectively.
### Why do cats scratch furniture?
Cats scratch furniture for marking territory, exercising, and sharpening their claws. Using a scratching post, like those found on **[Cat Scratching Post en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Cat+Scratching+Post&tag=vds96-20)(https://www.amazon.com)**, can help redirect this behavior.
### How often should I play with my cat?
You should play with your cat at least twice a day for about **15-20 minutes** each session. This can help reduce boredom and knocking over of objects.
