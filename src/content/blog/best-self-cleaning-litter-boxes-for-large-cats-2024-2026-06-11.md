The **Litter-Robot** and **PetSafe** models, for example, have weight limits of 15 and 17 pounds, respectively. This is a concern for owners of larger breeds like Maine Coons or Ragdolls. ## The Noise Factor Most Reviews Ignore
The noise level of **self-cleaning litter boxes** can be a concern for skittish cats. According to research by the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), cats are highly sensitive to noise. Decibel measurements during cleaning cycles found that:
| Model | Noise Level (dB) |
| --- | --- |
| **PetSafe ScoopFree Ultra** | 60-70 dB |
| **Litter-Robot III** | 70-80 dB |
| **Omega Paw Roll'N Clean** | 50-60 dB |
This can be a significant factor in choosing the right model for your cat.

## Pro Tips for Multi-Cat Households
For multi-cat households, it's essential to modify cycle timing and sensor settings to accommodate cats of different sizes. According to certified cat behaviorist [Lisa Radosta, DVM](https://www.felinebehaviorassociates.com/), here are some tips:
1. Adjust the cycle timing to prevent conflicts between cats.
2. Use the sensor settings to detect when a cat is present and adjust the cleaning cycle accordingly.
3. Consider using a larger litter box to accommodate multiple cats.
