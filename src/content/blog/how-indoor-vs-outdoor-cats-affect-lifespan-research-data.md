## Average lifespan: indoor vs outdoor cats
The average lifespan of **indoor cats** is 12-20 years, compared to **2-5 years** for outdoor cats, according to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) (2015). Key mortality risks for outdoor cats include vehicle accidents, feline leukemia, and predation.

## Top 3 outdoor dangers shortening cat lives
Data from the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org) (2018) shows that **40% of outdoor cat deaths** are due to vehicle accidents, while **15%** are caused by feline leukemia, and **12%** by predation. Regional variations exist, with higher risks in areas with high traffic volume or wildlife populations.

## Indoor cat longevity secrets
Indoor cats can live **5-8 years longer** than outdoor cats due to controlled diets, reduced stress, and vaccination schedules, according to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) (2020). A balanced diet with **2-3% of daily calories from protein** and regular veterinary check-ups can contribute to a longer lifespan.

## The supervised outdoor compromise
Research published in the [International Journal of Environmental Research](https://www.mdpi.com/journal/ijerph) (2021) suggests that **catios**, leash training, and timed outdoor access can reduce risks by **60%** while providing enrichment for cats. ## Breed-specific outdoor risk factors
A study in the [Journal of Applied Animal Welfare Science](https://www.tandfonline.com/toc/haaw20/current) (2019) found that **coat color**, **breed**, and **age** can affect outdoor survival rates. For example, **white cats** are at higher risk of sunburn, while **Siamese** cats are more prone to certain health issues than **Maine Coon** cats.

## Transitioning outdoor cats indoors safely
To transition an outdoor cat to indoor living, follow these steps:
1. **Gradually reduce outdoor access** over a period of 2-3 weeks.
2. **Provide alternative stimulation**, such as toys or scratching posts.
3. **Increase indoor playtime** to **30 minutes** per day.
4. **Monitor behavior** and adjust the transition plan as needed.
5. **Consult a veterinarian** for personalized advice.
