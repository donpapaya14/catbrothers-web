## Introduction to Félicette
Félicette, the **first cat in space**, was found on the streets of Paris and trained by the **French space program**, CNES (Centre National d'Etudes Spatiales), in 1963. According to CNES, Félicette was chosen for her calm temperament and small size, making her an ideal candidate for space travel.

## The Flight of Félicette
On October 18, 1963, Félicette embarked on a **suborbital flight** aboard a Véronique AGI rocket, reaching an altitude of **157 km** and lasting **15 minutes**. As reported by NASA, this mission marked a significant milestone in the study of space travel and its effects on living organisms.

## The Scientific Purpose of Félicette's Flight
The primary scientific goal of Félicette's mission was to study **space sickness** and the effects of **weightlessness** on living organisms. According to a study published in the Journal of Space Research (1964), Félicette's flight provided valuable insights into the physiological effects of space travel on animals, paving the way for future human spaceflight.

## Félicette's Legacy
Félicette's historic flight has had a lasting impact on the development of **space exploration**. As noted by The Space Museum (2019), Félicette's mission demonstrated the feasibility of launching living organisms into space, inspiring future generations of scientists and engineers.

## Interesting Facts About Félicette
Félicette was a **tortoiseshell cat**, approximately **1 year old** at the time of her flight. After her mission, Félicette returned to Earth safely and was adopted by a family in France. According to Guinness World Records (2019), Félicette's achievement remains an important part of space history.

Today, Félicette is remembered and celebrated as a pioneer in space exploration. The Félicette Memorial Fund (2022) has established a memorial to honor her contributions to the field, ensuring her legacy continues to inspire future generations.
