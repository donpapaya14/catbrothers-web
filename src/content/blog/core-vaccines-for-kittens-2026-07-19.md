### Introduction to Kitten Vaccinations

Kitten vaccinations are a crucial aspect of maintaining the health and wellbeing of your feline companion. According to the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA), vaccinations are essential in preventing diseases and protecting the health of kittens (American Veterinary Medical Association, 2022). The AVMA provides guidelines for veterinarians to follow when administering vaccinations to kittens, ensuring that they receive the necessary protection against diseases.

### Core Vaccines Recommended by AVMA

The AVMA recommends the following core vaccines for kittens:

* **Rabies**: a fatal viral disease that affects the nervous system of cats and can be transmitted to humans (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2021).
* **Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis (FVR)**: a highly contagious viral disease that affects the upper respiratory tract of cats (American Animal Hospital Association, 2020).
* **Feline Calicivirus (FCV)**: a viral disease that affects the upper respiratory tract of cats and can cause a range of symptoms from mild to severe (American Animal Hospital Association, 2020).
* **Panleukopenia (FPV)**: a highly contagious viral disease that affects the bone marrow of cats and can cause a range of symptoms from mild to severe (American Animal Hospital Association, 2020).

### Non-Core Vaccines for Kittens

Non-core vaccines, such as **Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV)** and **Feline Immunodeficiency Virus (FIV)**, are recommended for kittens that are at high risk of exposure to these diseases. According to the American Association of Feline Practitioners, FeLV and FIV vaccines are recommended for kittens that are exposed to infected cats or have a history of exposure (American Association of Feline Practitioners, 2019).

### Kitten Vaccination Schedule

The typical vaccination schedule for kittens is as follows:

* **Initial vaccinations**: kittens should receive their first vaccinations at 6-8 weeks of age, with booster shots given every 3-4 weeks until they are 16 weeks old (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2021).
* **Booster shots**: kittens should receive booster shots every 1-2 years to maintain their immunity against diseases (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2021).

### Potential Vaccine Side Effects in Kittens

Vaccines can cause a range of side effects in kittens, from mild to severe. According to the Veterinary Information Network, common side effects of vaccines in kittens include:

* **Mild side effects**: such as soreness at the injection site, fever, and lethargy (Veterinary Information Network, 2022).
* **Severe side effects**: such as anaphylaxis, which is a life-threatening allergic reaction (Veterinary Information Network, 2022).

### Cost and Insurance Considerations

The cost of kitten vaccinations can vary depending on the location and the veterinarian. According to the North American Pet Health Insurance Association, the average cost of a kitten vaccination series is around $100-$200 (North American Pet Health Insurance Association, 2022).
