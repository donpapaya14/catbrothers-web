## Introduction to Cat Food Ingredients to Avoid
When it comes to **cat food ingredients to avoid**, understanding what's in your cat's food is crucial for their health. According to the [American Association of Feed Control Officials (AAFCO)](https://www.aafco.org), **meat by-products** can be a significant concern. These by-products include organs and bones, which can be acceptable or low-quality, depending on the source.

## The Truth About 'Meat By-Products'
Meat by-products are defined by the [AAFCO Official Publication 2023](https://www.aafco.org) as non-rendered, clean parts of carcasses, such as organs and bones. While some by-products are nutritious, others can be low-quality and even harmful. For example, a study by the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) found that **feather meal** can be a low-quality protein source.

## Artificial Preservatives Linked to Health Risks
Certain artificial preservatives, such as **BHA**, **BHT**, and **ethoxyquin**, have been linked to health risks, including cancer and organ damage. According to the [FDA Veterinary Medicine Advisory Committee Report 2021](https://www.fda.gov), these preservatives can cause significant harm to cats. A study by the [National Toxicology Program](https://ntp.niehs.nih.gov) found that **BHA** can cause cancer in animals.

## Grain Fillers vs. Necessary Carbohydrates
Grain fillers, such as **corn gluten meal** and **wheat middlings**, can be harmful to cats, while complex carbohydrates like **brown rice** and **oats** can be beneficial. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery 2022](https://journals.sagepub.com), the glycemic index of these carbohydrates can vary significantly. ## Natural Flavors That Aren't What They Seem
**Natural flavors** can be misleading, as they can include **MSG derivatives** or **animal digest**. According to the [Truth in Pet Food Project 2023 Investigation](https://truthaboutpetfood.com), these ingredients can be harmful to cats. To identify truly natural flavor sources, look for ingredients like **chicken broth** or **salmon oil**.

## The Protein Percentage Deception
Crude protein percentages can be inflated with non-meat sources, such as **feather meal** or **plant proteins**. According to the [Tufts University Clinical Nutrition Service 2023](https://vet.tufts.edu), calculating the true animal protein content is crucial. A study by the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) found that **animal protein** is essential for feline health.

## Red Flag Ingredients for Cats with Sensitivities
Certain ingredients, such as **carrageenan**, **guar gum**, and **artificial colors**, can trigger allergies or digestive issues in cats. According to the [WSAVA Nutritional Guidelines 2022](https://www.wsava.org), these ingredients should be avoided. A list of red flag ingredients is shown below:

1. **Carrageenan**
2. **Guar Gum**
3. **Artificial Colors**
4. **Preservatives**
