## The Meat Source Deception
When it comes to meat sources, **chicken meal** and **chicken by-product meal** are not created equal. According to a study by the University of Illinois Feline Nutrition Study (2021), **chicken meal** has a higher protein bioavailability compared to **chicken by-product meal**. This is because chicken meal is made from **100% chicken**, whereas chicken by-product meal can contain **feathers, beaks, and other low-quality ingredients**.

## Special Cases: Senior Cats vs Kittens
The protein needs of senior cats and kittens differ significantly. According to the WSAVA Global Nutrition Guidelines (2023), senior cats require **30-40% protein**, while kittens require **50-60% protein**. It's essential to adjust the portion sizes based on the age and activity level of your cat. For example, a senior cat may require **1/4 cup of food per 10 pounds of body weight**, while a kitten may require **1/2 cup of food per 10 pounds of body weight**.
