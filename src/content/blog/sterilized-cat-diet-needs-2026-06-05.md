## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Nutrition

Sterilization alters a cat's metabolism, reducing energy requirements by **20-30%** while increasing appetite by **23%**, according to the [American Animal Hospital Association (2022)](https://www.aaha.org). This hormonal shift makes **sterilized cat diet** planning critical to prevent obesity, which affects **60% of spayed/neutered cats** (International Journal of Obesity, 2021). Key adjustments include:

- **Reduced calories**: 20-25% fewer calories than intact cats
- **Higher protein**: Minimum 40% of calories from animal protein
- **Increased fiber**: 3-8% crude fiber to promote satiety

## Calculating Daily Caloric Intake

The [National Research Council (2019)](https://www.nationalacademies.org) recommends this formula for sterilized cats:

1. **Resting Energy Requirement (RER)**: 70 × (body weight in kg)^0.75
2. **Adjust for activity**:
   - Sedentary: RER × 1.2
   - Active: RER × 1.4
3. **Sterilization factor**: Multiply result by 0.8

**Example**: A 4kg sterilized indoor cat needs 70 × (4)^0.75 × 1.2 × 0.8 = **180 kcal/day**.

## Food Options for Sterilized Cats

The [Pet Food Institute (2023)](https://www.petfoodinstitute.org) compares options:

1. **Wet food** (Best)
   - Pros: High moisture (75%), portion control
   - Cons: More expensive
2. **Dry food** (Convenient)
   - Pros: Dental benefits, longer shelf life
   - Cons: Only 10% moisture
3. **Raw diets** (Controversial)
   - Pros: High protein
   - Cons: Bacterial risk, unbalanced without supplements

## Managing Weight in Sterilized Cats

A 3-year [International Journal of Obesity (2021)](https://www.nature.com/ijo) study showed these strategies reduce obesity risk by 47%:

1. **Measure portions**: Use a kitchen scale (grams > cups)
2. **Feed 3-4 small meals**: Mimics natural hunting behavior
3. **Interactive feeders**: Slow eating by 30%
4. **Daily play**: 15 minutes of feather toy chasing burns 20 kcal

## Common Nutritional Mistakes to Avoid

The [Veterinary Information Network (2022)](https://www.vin.com) warns against:

1. **Free-feeding**: Increases calorie intake by 38%
2. **Treat overuse**: Limit to <10% of daily calories
3. **Low-protein diets**: Accelerates muscle loss in aging cats
4. **Ignoring water intake**: Sterilized cats need 60ml/kg/day

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should a sterilized cat eat per day?
A 4kg sterilized cat typically needs **180-200 kcal/day**. Use the formula: 70 × (weight in kg)^0.75 × 1.2 × 0.8. Measure portions with a scale for accuracy.

### What's the best protein source for neutered cats?
Animal-based proteins like chicken, turkey, or fish are ideal, providing **≥40% of calories**. Plant proteins lack essential amino acids like taurine, which cats cannot synthesize.

### Can sterilized cats eat regular cat food?

### How to prevent weight gain after spaying?
Combine **portion control** (measure meals), **wet food** (higher satiety), and **three 5-minute play sessions daily**. A 2021 University of Glasgow study found this maintains weight in 89% of cases.

### Do sterilized cats need special vitamins?
