## Recognizing Early Signs of Feline Dental Issues
Cats are prone to dental issues, with **80% of cats over the age of 3** suffering from some form of dental disease, according to the [American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)](https://www.avma.org). Common symptoms of dental problems in cats include bad breath, yellowing teeth, and changes in eating habits. For instance, a study by the [Journal of Veterinary Dentistry (2019)](https://www.jvdent.com) found that **57% of cats with dental disease** had halitosis.

## Effective Brushing Techniques for Cats
Brushing your cat's teeth can be a challenging task, but with the right technique, it can be a breeze. The [International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants (IAABC)](https://www.iaabc.org) recommends starting slowly, using positive reinforcement, and getting your cat accustomed to the process. Here's a step-by-step guide:
1. Start by letting your cat get used to the toothbrush and toothpaste.
2. Gradually introduce the toothbrush into your cat's mouth, beginning with the front teeth.
3. Brush your cat's teeth for **2-3 minutes**, twice a day.

## Scheduling Regular Veterinary Check-Ups for Cat Dental Care
Regular dental exams are crucial for maintaining your cat's oral health. The [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org) recommends scheduling dental check-ups every **6-12 months**. During a veterinary dental check-up, your cat's teeth will be examined, and any necessary cleaning or extractions will be performed.

## Common Cat Dental Care Mistakes to Avoid
There are several common mistakes cat owners make when it comes to **cat dental care at home**. Using human toothpaste or not brushing frequently enough can lead to dental problems. According to the [Veterinary Information Network (VIN)](https://www.vin.com), **70% of cat owners** do not brush their cat's teeth regularly.
