## Introduction to Cat Vomiting Yellow Bile
If your cat is vomiting yellow bile, it's essential to understand the underlying causes and potential solutions. **Cat vomiting yellow bile** can be a sign of an empty stomach, which occurs when the stomach is not digesting food properly. According to a study published in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2022), about 30% of cats that vomit yellow bile have a serious underlying condition, while 70% have minor stomach irritation.

## Why Bile Turns Vomit Yellow (and When It's NOT Normal)
Bile plays a crucial role in digestion, helping to break down fats and absorb fat-soluble vitamins. When the stomach is empty, bile can accumulate and cause vomiting. The color of the vomit can indicate the presence of blood or toxins, with **red** indicating blood, **green** indicating intestinal contents, and **yellow** indicating bile. A study by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2022) found that 25% of cats with yellow vomit had a gastrointestinal foreign body, while 15% had inflammatory bowel disease.

## The 3 Most Overlooked Triggers Vets Find
According to research by the Cornell Feline Health Center (2023), the following triggers are often overlooked:
1. **Hairball buildup**: Despite no visible hair in the vomit, hairballs can still cause vomiting.
2. **Meal timing gaps**: Exceeding 12 hours between meals can lead to vomiting.
3. **Hidden IBD early signs**: Inflammatory bowel disease can cause vomiting, and early signs may be subtle.
To prevent these triggers, consider the following actionable meal frequency adjustments:
* Feed your cat 3-4 times a day
* Use a timed feeder to ensure consistent meal times
* Monitor your cat's weight and adjust food portions accordingly
