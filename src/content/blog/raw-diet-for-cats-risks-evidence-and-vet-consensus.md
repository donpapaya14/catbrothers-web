## Introduction to Raw Cat Food Risks and Benefits
The debate about **raw cat food risks and benefits** has been ongoing, with some arguing that a raw diet is more natural and healthier for cats, while others point out the potential risks of bacterial contamination. According to the [American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)](https://www.avma.org), the concept of raw cat diets originated from the idea of mimicking the natural diet of wild cats. 

## The History of Raw Cat Diets: From Ancestral Feeding to Modern Trends
The history of raw cat diets dates back to the early 20th century when cat breeders and veterinarians began exploring alternative feeding options for domestic cats. A key milestone in the development of raw cat diets was the publication of a study by [Francis M. Pottenger Jr. (1939)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1643731/) on the effects of diet on feline health. The study found that cats fed a raw diet had improved overall health and well-being compared to those fed a cooked diet.

## Raw Cat Food Risks: Bacterial Contamination and Foodborne Illness
One of the primary concerns with raw cat food is the risk of bacterial contamination, particularly with **Salmonella** and **E. coli**. According to the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov), these bacteria can cause foodborne illness in cats and their owners. A study published in the [Journal of Food Protection (2017)](https://jfoodprotection.org/doi/abs/10.4315/0362-028X.JFP-16-252) found that 28% of raw cat food samples were contaminated with **Salmonella**.

## The Evidence: Studies on Raw Cat Diets and Feline Health
Several studies have investigated the effects of raw cat diets on feline health. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2015)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1098612X15572069) found that cats fed a raw diet had improved kidney function and dental health compared to those fed a commercial diet. However, another study published in the [Journal of Animal Science (2018)](https://academic.oup.com/jas/article/96/10/3735/4994448) found that raw cat diets may be deficient in certain nutrients, including **vitamin E** and **taurine**.

## Veterinarian Consensus: When to Recommend Raw Cat Diets and When to Avoid
Veterinarians generally agree that raw cat diets may be suitable for certain cats, such as those with food sensitivities or allergies. However, they also recommend that cat owners carefully weigh the potential benefits against the risks of bacterial contamination. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org), cat owners should consult with their veterinarian before switching to a raw diet.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What are the benefits of a raw cat diet?
The benefits of a raw cat diet include improved kidney function, dental health, and overall well-being. According to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2015)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1098612X15572069), cats fed a raw diet had improved kidney function and dental health compared to those fed a commercial diet.
### What are the risks of a raw cat diet?
The risks of a raw cat diet include bacterial contamination, particularly with **Salmonella** and **E. coli**, which can cause foodborne illness in cats and their owners. According to the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov), these bacteria can cause serious illness in people, especially those with weakened immune systems.
### How can I minimize the risks of a raw cat diet?
To minimize the risks of a raw cat diet, it's essential to handle the food safely and hygienically. Here are some tips:
* **Wash your hands**: Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after handling raw cat food.
* **Clean and disinfect**: Clean and disinfect all surfaces and utensils that come into contact with raw cat food.
* **Store food safely**: Store raw cat food in a sealed container and keep it refrigerated at a temperature of 40°F (4°C) or below.
