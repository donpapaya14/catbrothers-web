## Introduction to Cat Face Rubbing Behavior
Cats exhibit **cat face rubbing behavior** as a way to deposit pheromones on surfaces. According to a study by the [University of California](https://www.uc.edu), cats deposit pheromones through glands located on their faces, near their cheeks and lips.

## The Science of Facial Marking in Cats
The science behind **feline facial marking** involves the deposition of pheromones, which are chemical signals that convey information to other cats. A study published in [Cats as Pests: Their Effect on Agriculture and Fishery](https://www.uc.edu) (1963) found that cats use these pheromones to mark their territory and establish a sense of familiarity and comfort.

| Pheromone Type | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Felinine | Territorial marking |
| Feline facial pheromone (FFP) | Calming and comforting |

## Scent Marking: A Form of Communication
**Scent marking in cats** is a complex form of communication that conveys various types of information, including reproductive status, dominance, and territorial boundaries. A study published in [Animal Behaviour](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/animal-behaviour) (1977) found that cats use scent marking to communicate with other cats and establish a social hierarchy.

## Other Possible Reasons for Face Rubbing
In addition to scent marking, there are other possible reasons for **cat face rubbing behavior**, including reducing stress and anxiety, and exploring their environment. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) (2015) found that cats use face rubbing as a way to self-soothe and calm themselves.

## How to Encourage or Discourage Face Rubbing
To manage your cat's face-rubbing behavior, follow these steps:
1. Provide a **scratching post** or pad to redirect the behavior.
2. Use a **pheromone diffuser** to mimic the calming effects of feline facial pheromones.
3. Offer a variety of **textured surfaces** for your cat to rub against.

## Interesting Facts and Anecdotes
