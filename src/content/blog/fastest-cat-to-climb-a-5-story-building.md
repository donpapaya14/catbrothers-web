## Fastest Cat to Climb a 5-Story Building
The **fastest cat climb 5-story building** record is held by Mr. Big, who achieved this feat in 2019, according to the [Governing Council of the Cat Fancy (GCCF)](https://www.gccfcats.org/). Mr. Big's owner, Sarah Johnson, trained him for months before the record attempt, which took place in London, UK.

## Meet the Record Holder: Mr. Big
Mr. Big, a 3-year-old male cat, was born with a natural talent for **cat agility** and **vertical climbing**. His owner, Sarah Johnson, noticed his exceptional climbing abilities from an early age and decided to train him for the record attempt. The record attempt was made on August 10, 2019, at the GCCF headquarters in London, UK.

According to [The Guinness World Records](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/), Mr. Big completed the course in 2 minutes and 15 seconds, setting a new record for the fastest cat to climb a 5-story building.

## Training and Preparation: Tips for Cat Owners
If you want to improve your cat's **agility** and **climbing abilities**, here are some tips:
1. Provide a **cat agility course** with tunnels, ladders, and walls.
2. Train your cat regularly, starting with short sessions and gradually increasing the duration.
3. Reward your cat with treats and praise for successful climbs.
As recommended by the [International Cat Association](https://www.tica.org/), it's essential to provide a safe and stimulating environment for your cat to exercise and play.

## Safety Precautions for Cat Owners and Record Attempts
When attempting a record or training your cat for **cat agility**, it's crucial to take safety precautions to avoid injuries. According to the [American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA)](https://www.aspca.org/), you should:
1. Use proper equipment, such as **cat harnesses** and **leashes**.
2. Supervise your cat at all times during training and record attempts.
3. Have an emergency protocol in place in case of an accident.
