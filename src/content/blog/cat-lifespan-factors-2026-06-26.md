## Introduction to Feline Longevity

The average domestic cat lives **12-15 years**, but **cat lifespan factors** like genetics, nutrition, and healthcare can extend this to 20+ years. According to the [American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020)](https://catvets.com), cats receiving **preventative care** live 2-3 years longer than those without. Indoor cats also outlive outdoor cats by **5-7 years** due to reduced injury and disease risks.

Key influences on **feline longevity**:
1. **Genetics**: Breed-specific predispositions
2. **Diet**: Protein quality and calorie control
3. **Environment**: Indoor safety vs. outdoor hazards
4. **Healthcare**: Annual vet visits reduce mortality by **30%** (AVMA, 2022)

## Lifestyle Factors and Health Conditions

Outdoor cats face **3x higher mortality** from trauma/toxins (Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine, 2018). Critical stats:
- **Obese cats** (BMI >30) develop diabetes **4x faster**
- **Dental disease** affects **70%** of cats by age 3, shortening lifespan

**Prevention plan**:
1. Keep cats indoors or use **enclosed catios**
2. Brush teeth 3x/week with veterinary toothpaste
3. Weigh monthly; ideal weight = **5-10 lbs** for most breeds

## Impact of Regular Veterinary Care

Cats with **bi-annual vet visits** have **40% lower** risk of late-stage disease (AVMA, 2022). Essential care:

| Age       | Recommended Care | Frequency |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| Kitten    | Vaccinations     | Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks |
| Adult     | Bloodwork        | Annually  |
| Senior (7+)| Thyroid testing  | Every 6 months |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the longest a domestic cat has lived?
**Creme Puff** holds the record at **38 years** (Guinness World Records). Most longevity records involve indoor cats fed species-appropriate diets and receiving biannual vet checks.

### Do male or female cats live longer?
**Female cats live 1-2 years longer** on average. A University of Georgia study (2016) of 1,200 cats found intact males had the shortest lifespans due to territorial risks.

### Is wet or dry food better for longevity?
**Wet food increases lifespan** by promoting hydration. The WALTHAM Centre (2017) found cats on wet diets had **32% lower** kidney disease rates. Mix both for dental benefits.

### How often should senior cats see the vet?
**Every 6 months** after age 7. Cornell Feline Health Center reports 50% of cats over 10 have undiagnosed hypertension needing early intervention.
