## Introduction to Senior Cat Litter Needs
As cats age, their litter box needs change due to **reduced mobility** and **sensory decline**. According to the [American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020)](https://www.aafp.org), senior cats require easier access to litter boxes. 
This can be achieved with **low-sided** and **large** litter boxes.

## Features of Ideal Litter Boxes for Seniors
The ideal litter box for senior cats should have **low sides**, be **large** enough for the cat to turn around, and have **non-slip surfaces** to reduce strain. A study by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019)](https://www.jfms.com) found that these features improve the litter box experience for senior cats.

## Litter Types Suitable for Senior Cats
Senior cats require **gentle** and **odor-controlling** litter. According to the [Cat Fanciers' Association (2021)](https://cfa.org), **clumping**, **silica gel**, and **recycled paper** litters are suitable options.

## Tips for Transitioning Senior Cats to New Litter Boxes
To transition senior cats to new litter boxes:
1. Place the new litter box next to the old one.
2. Fill the new litter box with the same litter as the old one.
3. Encourage the cat to use the new litter box by placing treats or toys inside.
As advised by [International Cat Care (2020)](https://icatcare.org), this gradual transition minimizes stress and accidents.
