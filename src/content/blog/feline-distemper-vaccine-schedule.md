## What is Feline Distemper and Why is Vaccination Crucial?
Feline distemper, also known as **feline panleukopenia**, is a highly contagious and potentially life-threatening disease affecting cats. According to the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (2020)](https://www.cdc.gov), distemper outbreaks in the US have been reported, highlighting the importance of vaccination. Symptoms include **fever**, **lethargy**, and **loss of appetite**.

## When to Vaccinate Your Cat Against Feline Distemper
The **American Veterinary Medical Association (2022)** recommends a detailed schedule for feline distemper vaccination:
| Age | Vaccination |
| --- | --- |
| 6-8 weeks | First dose |
| 10-12 weeks | Second dose |
| 14-16 weeks | Third dose |
| 1 year | Booster shot |
| Every 3 years | Revaccination |

## Risks and Side Effects of Feline Distemper Vaccination
While generally safe, feline distemper vaccination can have potential risks and side effects. According to the [Food and Drug Administration (2019)](https://www.fda.gov), common side effects include:
* **Pain** or **swelling** at the injection site
* **Fever** or **lethargy**
* **Loss of appetite** or **vomiting**

## Boosters and Revaccination: What You Need to Know
Boosters and revaccination are crucial for maintaining immunity against feline distemper. A study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association (2018)](https://avmajournals.avma.org) found that revaccination is **90% effective** in preventing the disease.

## Feline Distemper Vaccine Types: What's the Difference?
There are two main types of feline distemper vaccines: **live** and **inactivated**. According to [PetMD (2022)](https://www.petmd.com), live vaccines provide **longer-lasting immunity**, while inactivated vaccines are **safer** for cats with weakened immune systems.

## Consulting Your Veterinarian: Tips for a Smooth Vaccination Process
To ensure a smooth vaccination process, follow these tips:
1. **Schedule an appointment** with your veterinarian
2. **Prepare your cat** for the vaccination by withholding food and water for a few hours
3. **Ask questions** about the vaccination process and potential side effects
