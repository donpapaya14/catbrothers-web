## Introduction to Cat Behavior
Cats are known for their curious nature, and one behavior that often puzzles their owners is **why cats knock things off**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery), 2020, this behavior can be attributed to several factors, including instinctual hunting practice, attention-seeking, sensory exploration, and territory marking.

## Instinctual Hunting Practice
In the wild, cats use their paws to hunt and catch prey. This natural instinct is still present in domesticated cats, who may exhibit **hunting behaviors** like pawing at objects. A study by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery), 2020, found that **70% of cats** engage in play that mimics hunting.
Here are some key findings:
* Cats use their paws to bat at toys or objects, mimicking the motion of catching prey
* This behavior is more common in cats that are **less than 2 years old**
* Providing cats with toys that mimic prey, such as feather wands or laser pointers, can help satisfy their instinctual hunting needs

## Attention-Seeking Mechanism
Cats may also knock things off tables as a way to get attention from their owners. A study published in [Applied Animal Behaviour Science](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/applied-animal-behaviour-science), 2019, found that **60% of cats** engage in attention-seeking behaviors, including knocking objects off surfaces.
To address this behavior, owners can try the following:
1. Ignore the behavior and reward the cat for calm behavior
2. Provide the cat with **30 minutes of playtime** per day
3. Use positive reinforcement training to teach the cat to perform desired behaviors

## Sensory Exploration
Cats are naturally curious creatures, and they may knock things off tables as a way to explore their environment. A study by the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), 2021, found that cats use their senses to explore their surroundings, including **touch**, **smell**, and **hearing**.
To encourage safe exploration, owners can try the following:

## Territory Marking
Cats have scent glands located on their paws, and when they knock things off tables, they may be depositing their scent on the objects. A study published in the [International Journal of Pest Management](https://www.tandfonline.com/toc/ttpm20/current), 2018, found that **40% of cats** exhibit territorial marking behaviors, including scratching and pawing at surfaces.
To address this behavior, owners can try the following:
1. Provide the cat with **scratching posts** or pads to redirect their marking behavior
2. Use **pheromone products**, such as Feliway, to calm the cat and reduce marking behavior
3. Clean surfaces regularly to remove any scent marks left by the cat

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Why do cats knock things off tables at night?
Cats may knock things off tables at night due to **boredom** or **excess energy**. To address this behavior, owners can try providing the cat with **30 minutes of playtime** before bedtime or using **interactive toys** to keep the cat stimulated.
### Can cats learn to stop knocking things off tables?
**Yes**, cats can learn to stop knocking things off tables with positive reinforcement training and consistent boundaries. Owners can try rewarding the cat for calm behavior and ignoring the behavior when it occurs.
### How can I prevent my cat from knocking things off tables?
To prevent cats from knocking things off tables, owners can try using **sticky tape** to deter the cat from jumping onto surfaces or providing the cat with **scheduled play sessions** to satisfy their instinctual hunting needs.

### Can cats get hurt from knocking things off tables?
**Yes**, cats can get hurt from knocking things off tables, especially if they jump from high surfaces or knock over heavy objects. Owners should take steps to **cat-proof** their home and provide the cat with a safe and stable environment.
### How can I encourage my cat to play with toys instead of knocking things off tables?
To encourage cats to play with toys instead of knocking things off tables, owners can try providing the cat with a variety of **textured toys**, such as **crinkly balls** or **fuzzy mice**, and engaging in **playtime** with the cat regularly.
