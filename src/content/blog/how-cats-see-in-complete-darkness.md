## Introduction to Cat Low-Light Vision
Cats are known for their exceptional **cat low-light vision**, which enables them to navigate and hunt in conditions with minimal illumination. According to a study by the [National Eye Institute](https://www.nei.nih.gov), 2020, this ability is largely due to a reflective layer in the cat's eye called the **tapetum lucidum**. This layer reflects light back onto the retina, enhancing the cat's ability to see in low-light conditions.

## The Role of Tapetum Lucidum
The **tapetum lucidum** is a critical component of a cat's eye, responsible for their **cat night vision** capabilities. As noted by the [National Eye Institute](https://www.nei.nih.gov), 2020, this layer is made up of tiny crystals that reflect light, allowing the cat to see more effectively in low-light environments. The structure and function of the **tapetum lucidum** are unique to cats and are a key factor in their exceptional **feline visual acuity**.

## The Effect of Rods vs Cones
The cat's retina contains two types of photoreceptors: **rods** and **cones**. According to a study published in the [Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences](https://www.pnas.org), 2013, **rods** are more sensitive to light and are responsible for the cat's **cat low-light vision** abilities, while **cones** are responsible for color perception. The higher concentration of **rods** in the cat's retina allows them to see more effectively in low-light conditions.

## Visual Acuity in Cats
The estimated **visual acuity** of domestic cats is around 20/100, according to a study published in the [Journal of Comparative Physiology A](https://link.springer.com/journal/354), 2017. This means that cats can detect movement and recognize objects at varying distances, but their visual acuity is not as sharp as that of humans. However, their **cat low-light vision** abilities make up for this, allowing them to navigate and hunt effectively in low-light conditions.
