## Introduction to Raw Diets for Cats
The **raw cat diet risks** have been a topic of discussion among veterinarians and pet owners for years. According to a study conducted by the University of California, Davis (2018), the concept of raw feeding in cats dates back to the early 20th century. The study found that 60% of cat owners who fed their pets raw diets did so due to perceived health benefits.

## History of Raw Diets in Cats
The origins of raw feeding in cats can be traced back to the work of **Francis Pottenger**, who in the 1930s and 1940s conducted a series of experiments on the effects of raw vs. cooked diets on cats. A study published in the [Journal of Nutrition](https://academic.oup.com/jn) (1946) found that cats fed a raw diet had improved health outcomes compared to those fed a cooked diet.

## Nutritional Risks of Raw Diets
The nutritional risks associated with raw feeding are a major concern. A study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association](https://avmajournals.avma.org) (2015) found that 85% of raw diets were deficient in one or more essential nutrients. The most common deficiencies were in **vitamin D** and **calcium**.

1. **Vitamin D deficiency**: can lead to weakened bones and increased risk of fractures
2. **Calcium deficiency**: can lead to weakened bones and increased risk of osteoporosis

## Vet Consensus on Raw Diets
The current veterinary consensus on raw feeding is that it is not recommended due to the risks of nutritional deficiencies and **bacterial contamination**. According to a statement from the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org) (2019), raw diets can pose a significant risk to human health, particularly for **immunocompromised individuals**.

## Preparation and Safety Risks
To safely prepare and handle raw foods for cats, it is essential to follow proper food handling and storage procedures. A study published in the [Journal of Food Protection](https://jfoodprotection.org) (2017) found that 75% of raw meat samples were contaminated with **Salmonella** or **E. coli**.

1. **Handle raw meat separately**: to prevent cross-contamination
2. **Store raw meat properly**: in a sealed container at a temperature below 40°F (4°C)

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What are the risks of raw diets for cats?
The risks of raw diets for cats include nutritional deficiencies, bacterial contamination, and increased risk of **gastrointestinal disease**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com) (2018), 50% of cats fed a raw diet developed gastrointestinal disease.

### How can I safely handle raw meat for my cat?
To safely handle raw meat for your cat, it is essential to follow proper food handling and storage procedures. This includes handling raw meat separately, storing it in a sealed container, and washing your hands thoroughly after handling.

### What are the benefits of a cooked diet for cats?
The benefits of a cooked diet for cats include a balanced mix of nutrients, low risk of contamination, and improved digestibility. According to a study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) (2020), cooked diets can be just as nutritious as raw diets.

### Can I feed my cat a combination of raw and cooked diets?
Yes, you can feed your cat a combination of raw and cooked diets. However, it is essential to ensure that the diets are balanced and meet the nutritional needs of your cat. According to a study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Nutrition](https://www.sciencedirect.com) (2019), a combination diet can provide the benefits of both raw and cooked diets.

### What are the signs of nutritional deficiencies in cats?
The signs of nutritional deficiencies in cats include **weight loss**, **poor coat condition**, and **increased risk of disease**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Nutrition](https://academic.oup.com/jn) (2017), cats with nutritional deficiencies are more likely to develop chronic diseases.

### How can I ensure my cat is getting a balanced diet?
To ensure your cat is getting a balanced diet, it is essential to consult with a veterinarian or a qualified animal nutritionist. They can help you develop a diet plan that meets the nutritional needs of your cat.
