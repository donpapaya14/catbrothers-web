## Introduction to Feline Stress Caused by Music
**Cat stress music trigger** is a significant concern for cat owners, as it can lead to anxiety and other behavioral issues. According to a University of Wisconsin-Madison (2018) study on feline auditory perception, certain music genres and styles can cause stress in cats. 

## What types of music can stress out cats?
The University of Wisconsin-Madison (2018) study found that **loud music** and **music with high-frequency sounds** can be stressful for cats. Specifically, genres like **heavy metal** and **electronic dance music (EDM)** can cause stress due to their loud and high-frequency nature. 

| Music Genre | Stress Level |
| --- | --- |
| Heavy Metal | High |
| Electronic Dance Music (EDM) | High |
| Classical Music | Low |

## Why do cats find certain music types stressful?
Cats' stress response to specific music types can be explained by their evolutionary history. According to Cornell University's College of Veterinary Medicine (2020) research on feline behavior and evolution, cats are naturally inclined to respond to **high-frequency sounds**, which are often associated with prey or potential threats. 

## Practical tips for creating a calming environment with music
To create a calming environment for your cat, follow these steps:
1. Choose **calming music** with low frequencies and volumes.
2. Play **classical music** or **nature sounds** to create a soothing atmosphere.
3. Keep the **volume low**, around 50-60 decibels.

## Case study: cats' reactions to music in a real-world setting
A University of California, Davis (2015) study on feline behavior in the home environment found that cats exhibited **reduced stress** when exposed to calming music. In contrast, **loud music** caused increased anxiety and stress.
