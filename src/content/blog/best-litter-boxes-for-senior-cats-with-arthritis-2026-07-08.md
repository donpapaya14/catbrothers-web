## Understanding Senior Cats with Arthritis
Senior cats with arthritis often experience **joint inflammation and pain**, which can significantly impact their litter box behavior and preferences. According to the [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2020)](https://www.vet.cornell.edu), arthritis affects approximately **20% of cats** over the age of 12. This condition can lead to difficulties in performing everyday activities, including using the litter box.

## Litter Box Design for Senior Cats
The ideal litter box design for senior cats with arthritis includes **low sides**, **large openings**, and **anti-slip surfaces**. These features can help reduce strain on the cat's joints and make it easier for them to access the litter box. The [International Cat Care (2019)](https://icatcare.org) recommends a litter box with a **side height of less than 5 inches** to accommodate senior cats with mobility issues.

## Litter Types for Senior Cats
When it comes to litter types, senior cats with arthritis may prefer **clumping clay**, **silica gel**, or **natural litters**. These types of litter are often more comfortable and easier to clean than other options. According to the [Veterinary Information Network (2018)](https://www.vin.com), **60% of cat owners** prefer clumping clay litter due to its ease of use and effectiveness.

## Tips for Managing Arthritis in Senior Cats
Managing arthritis in senior cats requires a combination of **regular exercise**, a **balanced diet**, and **pain relief medication**. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (2020)](https://www.aaha.org), **regular veterinary check-ups** are crucial for monitoring the cat's condition and adjusting treatment as needed.

### Managing Arthritis: Step-by-Step Guide

3. **Encourage regular exercise** to maintain joint mobility.
4. **Use pain relief medication** as prescribed by a veterinarian.
