## Introduction to Pheromones

Pheromones are chemical signals cats use to communicate with each other, playing a critical role in marking territory, reducing stress, and signaling safety. According to [International Cat Care (2019)](https://icatcare.org), cats produce pheromones through facial glands, paws, and urine, which other cats detect via the vomeronasal organ. These signals help create a sense of familiarity and security in their environment.

**Key fact:** A 2019 study found that 78% of cats exposed to synthetic facial pheromones showed reduced stress behaviors within 7 days. This forms the basis of **feline pheromone therapy**, a scientifically backed method to manage **cat anxiety** and behavioral issues.

## How Pheromone Therapy Works

Feline pheromone therapy uses synthetic versions of natural cat pheromones to mimic calming signals. The most studied is the **F3 facial pheromone**, which cats deposit when rubbing their faces on objects to mark safe spaces. Research from the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)](https://journals.sagepub.com) confirms these synthetic pheromones bind to receptors in the vomeronasal organ, triggering a relaxation response.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does feline pheromone therapy take to work?
Most cats show reduced anxiety within **24–48 hours**, but full effects take 7 days. Continuous use for 1 month is ideal for lasting results.

### Are pheromone diffusers safe for kittens?
Yes. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org) confirms synthetic pheromones are non-toxic and safe for cats of all ages, including kittens over 8 weeks.

### Can pheromones stop cats from scratching furniture?
Yes. A 2021 study found **Feliway Spray** reduced scratching by 82% when applied to targeted areas every 2 days.

### Do pheromones work for aggressive cats?
**Partially.** They reduce tension in 60% of cases (AAHA, 2020), but severe aggression may require additional behavior modification.

### How often should I replace a pheromone diffuser?
Replace the vial every **30 days** and the diffuser every 6 months for optimal performance.
