## Introduction to High-Protein Cat Food
When it comes to **best high-protein cat food**, understanding feline nutritional needs is crucial. As **obligate carnivores**, cats require a diet rich in protein to maintain their overall health. According to the [AAFCO Cat Food Nutrient Profiles (2023)](https://www.aafco.org), cats need at least **40% protein** on a dry matter basis. Lower protein levels can lead to **muscle loss** and **obesity**.

## Why High-Protein Matters for Cats
Cats have specific dietary requirements due to their evolution as predators. Their bodies are designed to process high amounts of protein efficiently. A diet lacking sufficient protein can lead to health issues. For instance, a study by the [Journal of Animal Science (2018)](https://www.asas.org) found that cats fed high-protein diets had better **muscle mass** and **urinary health** compared to those on low-protein diets.

## Ash Content: The Hidden Factor in Cat Food
**Ash content**, which refers to the mineral residue left after burning food, is another critical factor in cat food. High ash levels can affect **urinary health**. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2022)](https://www.jfms.com), safe ash thresholds are **less than 3% for wet food** and **less than 8% for dry food**. Some brands have unusually high ash levels, which can be detrimental to feline health.

## Red Flags in Ingredient Lists
When choosing cat food, it's essential to spot **misleading claims** and **unnamed meat meals**. The [FDA Pet Food Labeling Guidelines (2023)](https://www.fda.gov) advise looking for specific ingredient names and avoiding generic terms.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best high-protein cat food for indoor cats?
The best high-protein cat food for indoor cats is one that is rich in protein and low in carbohydrates, such as [Wellness Core Grain-Free Wet Cat Food](AMAZON:Wellness Core Grain-Free Wet Cat Food).
### How much protein does my cat need per day?
The amount of protein your cat needs per day depends on their age, size, and activity level. According to the [National Research Council (2006)](https://www.nap.edu), the average adult cat requires about **30 grams of protein per day**.
### Can I mix wet and dry cat food?
Yes, you can mix wet and dry cat food, but it's essential to choose foods with similar protein levels and ingredient quality to avoid digestive issues.
### What are the benefits of high-protein cat food?
High-protein cat food can help with **weight management**, **muscle maintenance**, and **urinary health**.
### How often should I feed my cat?
The frequency of feeding depends on your cat's age, size, and activity level. Generally, adult cats should be fed **2-3 times a day**.
### What is the difference between grain-free and gluten-free cat food?
Grain-free cat food does not contain grains like wheat, corn, or rice, while gluten-free cat food does not contain gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye.
