## Introduction to Cat Senses
When comparing **cat senses vs human senses**, it's clear that felines have a unique set of abilities that allow them to navigate and interact with their environment in distinct ways. The **cat senses vs human senses comparison** reveals significant differences, particularly in hearing, vision, and smell. According to a study by [National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders](https://www.nidcd.nih.gov/), cats can hear sounds at frequencies as high as **64,000 Hz**, far beyond the **20,000 Hz** limit of human hearing.

## The Acute Hearing of Cats: A 6-Times Better Range
Cats can detect ultrasound frequencies, thanks to their **20 Hz to 64,000 Hz** hearing range, as evidenced by the study by [Lloyd and Fay, 1970](https://www.sciencedirect.com/). This ability allows them to pinpoint the source of high-frequency sounds with greater accuracy than humans. For example, a cat can hear a mouse scurrying across the floor from a much greater distance than a human can.

## Feline Vision: The Wide Angle of Peripheral Vision
Cats have a **200° field of view**, compared to the **180°** of humans, giving them a wider range of peripheral vision. According to [Bartnik et al., 2011](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/), cats also have a higher concentration of rod cells in their retinas, allowing them to see better in low light conditions. However, their visual acuity is lower than that of humans, with a **20/100** visual acuity compared to the **20/20** of humans.

## The Power of Cat Smell: 14 Times More Sensitive
Cats have a highly developed sense of smell, with **14 times more** olfactory receptors than humans. As [Doty, 2010](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/) notes, this allows them to detect pheromones and other scents that are undetectable to humans. For example, a cat can smell the scent of a potential mate from a great distance, allowing them to locate a suitable partner.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How many hours of sleep do cats need?
Cats need **16-18 hours** of sleep per day, according to [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/).
### What is the average lifespan of a cat?
The average lifespan of a cat is **12-15 years**, as noted by [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.catvets.com/).
### Can cats see colors?
Cats can see **blues and violets**, but not **reds and greens**, according to [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/).
### How often should I feed my cat?
Cats should be fed **2-3 times** per day, depending on their age and activity level, as recommended by [World Small Animal Veterinary Association](https://www.wsava.org/).
### What is the best way to train a cat?
The best way to train a cat is through **positive reinforcement**, using rewards and praise to encourage good behavior, according to [International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants](https://www.iaabc.org/).
