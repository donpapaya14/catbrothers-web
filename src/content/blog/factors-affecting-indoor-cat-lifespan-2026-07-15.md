## Introduction to Indoor Cat Lifespan Factors
The average **indoor cat lifespan** is around 12-15 years, but with proper care and attention to **cat health**, many cats can live well into their late teens. Factors affecting **indoor cat lifespan** include environmental enrichment, access to vitamin D, social interaction, noise levels, and nutritional factors.

## 1. Environmental Enrichment and Mortality Rate
A study by Overall et al. (1997) published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association](https://avma.org) found that cats with more toys and scratching posts had a lower mortality rate. This highlights the importance of providing **environmental enrichment** for indoor cats.

## 2. Window Perches and Vitamin D
Research by Ferrell et al. (2016) published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19391355) found that cats with access to **window perches** had higher levels of **vitamin D**, which is essential for bone health. According to the [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int), vitamin D deficiency can lead to a range of health problems.

## 3. Social Interaction and Cognitive Decline
The study by Bennett and Rohlf (2007) published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/jfm) demonstrated that cats with more **social interaction** had a slower rate of **cognitive decline**. This emphasizes the need for cat owners to spend quality time with their pets.

## 4. Noise Level and Stress
Research by Siivola et al. (2016) found that cats exposed to high **noise levels** experienced increased **stress**, which can negatively impact their lifespan. The [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org) recommends creating a quiet and comfortable environment for pets.

## 5. Nutritional Factors and Mortality Rate
The study by Crowell-Davis et al. (2014) published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19391355) showed that cats fed a high-quality, **nutrient-rich diet** had a lower mortality rate compared to those fed a lower-quality diet. ## Practical Advice for Cat Owners
To support the health and wellbeing of your indoor cat, follow these steps:
1. Provide a variety of toys and scratching posts to promote **environmental enrichment**.
2. Ensure access to **window perches** to support **vitamin D** production.
3. Spend quality time with your cat to promote **social interaction**.
4. Create a quiet and comfortable environment to reduce **stress**.
5. Feed a high-quality, **nutrient-rich diet** to support overall health.
