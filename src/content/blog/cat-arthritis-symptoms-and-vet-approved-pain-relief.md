## Introduction to Cat Arthritis Symptoms and Relief
Cat arthritis symptoms can be subtle, making it crucial for owners to recognize **feline joint pain signs**. According to the International Journal of Applied Research in Veterinary Medicine (2022), reduced grooming, hesitation before jumping, or changes in litter box habits can indicate joint pain. These signs correlate with joint pain as they often result from the cat's attempt to avoid exacerbating the discomfort.

## Subtle Behavioral Changes that Signal Pain
Less obvious signs of pain include:
1. **Changes in appetite**: A decrease or increase in food consumption.
2. **Altered sleep patterns**: Changes in the amount of time spent sleeping or resting.
3. **Reduced social interaction**: Withdrawal from social activities or avoiding interaction with family members.
These changes, as noted by the International Journal of Applied Research in Veterinary Medicine (2022), are critical indicators of potential joint pain in cats.

## The 'Stiff Cat' Test Vets Use at Home
The American Association of Feline Practitioners (2023) recommends a gentle assessment of joint flexibility and pain response. This can be done by:
1. **Gently flexing the cat's joints**: Observe for signs of discomfort or resistance.
2. **Monitoring for vocalizations**: Cats in pain may vocalize when their joints are manipulated.
3. **Observing gait and mobility**: Notice any changes in the cat's ability to move or walk.

## Most Overlooked Pain Triggers in Homes
Common household setups can worsen arthritis pain, as highlighted by the Cornell Feline Health Center (2021). These include:
| Setup | Modification Tip |
| --- | --- |
| High-sided litter boxes | Use lower-sided litter boxes or ramps for easier access |
| Slippery floors | Place non-slip mats or rugs to prevent slipping |
