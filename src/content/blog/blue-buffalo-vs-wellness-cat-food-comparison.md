## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best cat food for indoor cats?
The best cat food for indoor cats is one that is **high in protein** and **low in carbohydrates**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://www.journalofanimalscience.org), indoor cats require around **20-25% protein** on a dry matter basis.
### How often should I feed my cat?
The frequency of feeding your cat depends on their age, size, and activity level. Generally, adult cats require **2-3 meals per day**, while kittens require **3-4 meals per day**.
### Can cats eat raw meat?
While some cat owners choose to feed their cats raw meat, it's essential to note that raw meat can pose a risk of **bacterial contamination**. According to the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)](https://www.cdc.gov), raw meat can contain bacteria like **Salmonella** and **E. coli**.
### What is the difference between grain-free and gluten-free cat food?
Grain-free cat food does not contain grains like **wheat**, **corn**, and **soy**, while gluten-free cat food does not contain gluten, a protein found in **wheat**, **barley**, and **rye**.
### Can cats be allergic to food?
Yes, cats can be allergic to food, with common allergens including **beef**, **dairy**, and **fish**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Dermatology](https://www.jvdjournal.org), food allergies can cause symptoms like **itching**, **scratching**, and **digestive issues**.
