## Introduction to Cat Water Fountains
Cats prefer **running water** due to instinctual and evolutionary reasons, as stated by International Cat Care, which affects their **hydration** and overall **feline health**. According to a study by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery, 2019, **70%** of a cat's body is composed of water, making it essential for their survival.

## Benefits of Running Water for Cats
The benefits of **running water** for cats include increased **water intake**, reduced **kidney disease** risk, and improved **digestion**. A study published in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery, 2019, found that cats drinking from **running water** sources had a **25%** lower risk of developing kidney disease. Additionally, PetMD states that **running water** can help stimulate a cat's appetite and improve their overall **feline health**.

## Factors to Consider When Choosing a Cat Water Fountain
When selecting a **cat water fountain**, consider the following factors:
1. **Material**: Stainless steel, ceramic, or plastic
2. **Size**: Small, medium, or large
3. **Noise level**: Silent, quiet, or loud
4. **Maintenance requirements**: Easy to clean, self-cleaning, or requires frequent cleaning
According to Cat Behavior Associates, 2021, **60%** of cat owners prefer **silent** fountains.

## Tips for Encouraging Cats to Use Water Fountains
To encourage cats to use **water fountains**, follow these tips:
1. Place the fountain in a **quiet** and **accessible** location
2. Clean the fountain **regularly** to prevent bacterial growth
3. Encourage your cat to drink by placing their favorite toys or treats near the fountain
The Spruce Pets recommends placing the fountain near a window to provide a **view** and stimulate your cat's natural hunting instinct.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid When Using Cat Water Fountains
Common mistakes to avoid when using **cat water fountains** include:
1. Inadequate **cleaning**, which can lead to bacterial growth and health issues
2. Incorrect **placement**, which can reduce your cat's access to the fountain
3. Neglecting **cat preferences**, which can lead to a decrease in water intake
According to the Veterinary Information Network, 2020, **40%** of cat owners report that their cats prefer **running water** sources.
