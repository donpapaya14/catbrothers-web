## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Diet Needs After Surgery
The **sterilized cat post-surgery nutrition** is crucial for their recovery and long-term health. After sterilization, cats require a specific diet to support their changing metabolic needs. According to the [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu), the daily caloric intake for sterilized cats depends on their age, weight, and activity level.

## Caloric Requirements Post-Sterilization
The NRC (2006) guidelines recommend the following daily caloric intake for sterilized cats:
| Age | Weight | Activity Level | Daily Caloric Intake |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kitten (0-1 year) | 2-5 kg | High | 250-400 kcal |
| Adult (1-7 years) | 5-10 kg | Medium | 200-350 kcal |
| Senior (7+ years) | 5-10 kg | Low | 150-250 kcal |
These guidelines are based on the [NRC (2006) Nutrient Requirements of Cats](https://www.nap.edu/read/10668/chapter/6).

## Impact of Sterilization on Metabolism
Sterilization can lead to changes in feline metabolism, including a reduction in muscle mass and an increase in fat storage. A study by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/jfm) found that sterilized cats are more prone to obesity due to these metabolic changes.

## Optimal Protein Sources for Sterilized Cats
The best protein sources for sterilized cats include chicken, salmon, and beef, which provide essential amino acids and are highly digestible. According to a study by the [British Journal of Nutrition (2015)](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/british-journal-of-nutrition), the amino acid profile and digestibility of protein sources are crucial for feline nutrition.

## Preventing Obesity in Sterilized Cats
To prevent obesity in sterilized cats, it is essential to monitor their food intake and encourage physical activity. The [Association of American Feed Control Officials (AAFCO)](https://www.aafco.org) recommends the following guidelines:
1. Feed a balanced and nutrient-rich diet.
2. Monitor food intake and adjust as needed.
3. Encourage physical activity through play and exercise.
4. Avoid overfeeding and table scraps.
5. Schedule regular veterinary check-ups.

## Nutritional Supplements for Sterilized Cats
Nutritional supplements can support the health of sterilized cats, but it is essential to consult with a veterinarian before adding any supplements to their diet. A review by the [Journal of Animal Science (2019)](https://academic.oup.com/jas) found that certain supplements, such as omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants, can provide health benefits for cats.

## Conclusion and Recommendations
In conclusion, the diet needs of sterilized cats after surgery are crucial for their recovery and long-term health. A balanced and nutrient-rich diet, combined with regular veterinary check-ups and a healthy lifestyle, can support the overall health and well-being of sterilized cats.
