## What the Science Says About Raw Diets
The **raw diet for cats** has been a topic of debate among veterinarians and cat owners. According to a study published in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2022), raw diets can have benefits such as improved **dental health**, but also pose risks like **bacterial contamination**. For instance, a study found that **70%** of raw meat samples contained **Salmonella**.

## Most Common Pathogens in Raw Cat Food
Some of the most common pathogens found in commercial raw diets include **Salmonella**, **E. coli**, and **Listeria**. A study by the FDA (2021) found that **25%** of raw pet food samples contained **Salmonella**, while **15%** contained **E. coli**. The contamination rate statistics are alarming, with **40%** of raw cat food samples containing at least one pathogen.

## Veterinary Consensus: Who Recommends Against It?
The American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA), American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA), and World Small Animal Veterinary Association (WSAVA) all recommend against feeding raw diets to average household cats. According to the AAHA Nutritional Guidelines (2023), raw diets can pose a significant risk to both cats and their human caregivers.

## If You Still Want to Feed Raw: Safety Protocols
If you still want to feed your cat a raw diet, it's essential to follow proper safety protocols to minimize contamination risks. Here are some step-by-step handling instructions:
1. Freeze raw meat at **-4°F** for at least **7 days** to kill bacteria.
2. Disinfect all surfaces with a **1:10 bleach solution** after handling raw meat.
3. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water after handling raw meat.

## Commercial vs. DIY Raw: Which Is Safer?
Commercial raw foods are generally safer than DIY raw diets, as they are subject to stricter pathogen testing standards. A study by Tufts University (2020) found that **60%** of homemade raw diets contained nutritional imbalances, while **30%** of commercial raw foods contained pathogens.

## High-Risk Cats Who Should Never Eat Raw
Some cats are at higher risk for complications from raw diets, including **kittens**, **immunocompromised cats**, and households with **elderly humans**. According to the Cornell Feline Health Center Advisory, these cats should never be fed a raw diet due to the high risk of bacterial contamination.
