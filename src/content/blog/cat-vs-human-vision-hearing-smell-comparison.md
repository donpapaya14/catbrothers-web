## Introduction to Cat Senses
When comparing **cat senses** to those of humans, it's clear that our feline friends have some unique advantages. The **cat senses comparison humans** often reveals that cats have superior **night vision**, with a study by the University of California, Davis (2018) showing that cats have a 50% increase in visual acuity in low light conditions. 

## How Much Better is a Cat's Night Vision?
Cats' **night vision** is due to a reflective layer in the back of their eyes called the **tapetum lucidum**, which reflects any light that passes through the retina back onto the photoreceptors, giving the photoreceptors a second chance to absorb the light. According to the University of California, Davis (2018), this results in a **6-fold increase in sensitivity** to low light levels.

## Hearing Ranges: Cats vs Humans
The **hearing range** of cats and humans differs significantly. Cats can hear sounds at frequencies as high as **79 kHz**, while humans can only hear sounds up to **20 kHz**. The National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders (2020) notes that cats can also hear sounds at much lower decibel levels, with a range of **50 dB** compared to humans' **0 dB**.

## Smell Power: Cats vs Humans
Cats have a highly developed sense of **smell**, with an **olfactory epithelium** that is 14 times larger than that of humans, according to Harvard Medical School (2019). This allows them to detect **pheromones** and other scents that are undetectable to humans.

## Visual Acuity: The Amazing Feline Retina
The **feline retina** is specially adapted for low light conditions, with a high concentration of **rod cells** that are sensitive to low light levels. The Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (2015) notes that this allows cats to see in conditions that would be pitch black to humans.
