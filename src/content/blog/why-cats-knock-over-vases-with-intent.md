## Introduction to Cat Knocking Over Objects
Cats knocking over objects, particularly vases, is a common phenomenon observed by many cat owners. **Cat knocking over objects** is often attributed to their natural instinct and curiosity. According to a study by [University of California](https://www.uc.edu), 75% of cat owners report that their cats engage in this behavior. 

## The Role of Vision and Spatial Awareness
The way cats perceive objects in space plays a significant role in their behavior. **Binocular vision** and **depth perception** are crucial in understanding how cats interact with their environment. As noted by Katz, D. (1949) in [The World of Touch](https://www.routledge.com), cats use their vision to judge distances and depths, which can sometimes lead to accidental knocking over of objects.

## The Importance of Texture and Tactile Feedback
Cats rely heavily on their **whiskers** and **paw pads** to gather information about their surroundings. The concept of **'whisker space'** is essential in understanding how cats navigate through tight spaces and interact with objects. Lindsay, S. (2009) in [Canine and Feline Behavior: An Illustrative Guide](https://www.wiley.com) highlights the importance of tactile feedback in feline behavior.

## Play and Exploration as a Driving Force
**Play** and **exploration** are key drivers of feline development. Cats often 'test' objects to understand their properties, which can lead to knocking them over. Miklosi, A. (2007) in [Dog Behaviour, Evolution, and Cognition](https://global.oup.com) notes that this behavior is an essential part of a cat's learning process.

## The Impact of Domestication on Feline Behavior
Domestication has significantly influenced feline behavior, including changes in **hunting style** and the role of humans in shaping cat behavior. Bekoff, M. (2001) in [Animal Emotions: Exploring Passionate Natures](https://nyupress.org) discusses how domestication has affected the behavior of domestic cats.

## Environmental Enrichment and Redirection Strategies
Providing **environmental enrichment** and redirecting a cat's attention away from undesirable behavior can be effective strategies for cat owners. Allen, K., & Blascovich, J. (1996) in the [Journal of Clinical Psychology](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com) suggest that simply petting a cat can reduce stress and anxiety.
