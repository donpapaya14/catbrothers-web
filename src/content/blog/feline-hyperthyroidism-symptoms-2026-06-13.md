## Introduction to Feline Hyperthyroidism
Feline hyperthyroidism is the most common endocrine disorder in cats, affecting **1 in 300 cats over age 10** according to the [American Animal Hospital Association (2020)](https://www.aaha.org). It occurs when the thyroid gland produces excess hormones, accelerating metabolism. Early diagnosis is critical—untreated cases can lead to heart disease, kidney failure, or death within months.

## Causes and Risk Factors of Feline Hyperthyroidism
The exact cause remains unclear, but the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019)](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/jfm) identifies key risk factors:
- **Environmental toxins**: Flame retardants in household dust increase risk by 3.5×
- **Diet**: Cats fed exclusively canned food show 2.1× higher incidence (University of Edinburgh, 2018)
- **Age**: 95% of cases occur in cats over 10 years old

## Symptoms of Feline Hyperthyroidism
Watch for these **cat hyperthyroidism symptoms** (Veterinary Information Network, 2022):
1. **Weight loss despite increased appetite** (87% of cases)
2. **Hyperactivity or restlessness** (76%)
3. **Vomiting/diarrhea** (49%)
4. **Unkempt coat** (42%)
5. **Increased thirst/urination** (38%)

## Diagnosing Feline Hyperthyroidism
Vets use:
1. **T4 blood test** (90% accurate for initial screening)
2. **Free T4 by equilibrium dialysis** (detects 98% of cases per [International Journal of Endocrinology (2018)](https://www.hindawi.com/journals/ije/))
3. **Thyroid ultrasound** (identifies tumors >3mm)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the first signs of hyperthyroidism in cats?
The earliest **cat hyperthyroidism symptoms** are sudden weight loss despite eating more (87% of cases) and increased vocalization. These typically appear 3-6 months before full diagnosis.

### Can hyperthyroidism in cats be cured?
Yes, **radioactive iodine therapy** cures 95% of feline thyroid disease cases with one treatment. Medication manages but doesn't cure the condition.

### How long can a cat live with hyperthyroidism?
With proper **feline hyperthyroidism treatment**, cats live 3-5+ years post-diagnosis. Untreated, survival drops to <6 months due to heart/kidney damage.

### Is hyperthyroidism painful for cats?
No, but uncontrolled **hyperthyroidism in cats** causes discomfort from constant hunger, muscle wasting, and hypertension. Pain occurs only with secondary conditions like heart failure.

### What foods should hyperthyroid cats avoid?
Avoid high-iodine foods like fish, dairy, and egg yolks. The [American College of Veterinary Nutrition](https://www.acvn.org) recommends <0.3mg iodine/kg in therapeutic diets.
