## Introduction to Feline Longevity
The average **cat lifespan** is around 12-15 years, according to the [American Association of Feline Practitioners](https://www.aafp.org). However, with proper care and attention to **cat lifespan factors**, some cats can live up to 20 years or more. Recent research findings suggest that a combination of genetic, dietary, and lifestyle factors can significantly impact a cat's longevity.

## Genetics and Cat Lifespan
A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com) (2018) found that **purebred cats** have a shorter average lifespan (12.5 years) compared to **mixed-breed cats** (14.5 years). Additionally, larger breeds tend to have shorter lifespans than smaller breeds.

| Breed | Average Lifespan |
| --- | --- |
| Siamese | 15-20 years |
| Maine Coon | 12-15 years |
| Domestic Shorthair | 12-17 years |

## Diet and Nutrition in Feline Longevity
A nutrient-rich diet that includes **antioxidants** and **omega-3 fatty acids** can help promote a long and healthy life for cats. According to the [National Research Council](https://www.nap.edu), a balanced diet that meets a cat's nutritional needs can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases. For senior cats, a diet specifically formulated for their life stage can help support their overall health. [Hill's Science Diet Senior Cat Food](https://www.hillspet.com) is a great option.

## Lifestyle Factors and Cat Longevity
A study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org) (2019) found that **indoor cats** tend to live longer than **outdoor cats**. Additionally, **spaying or neutering** can help reduce the risk of certain health issues, such as uterine infections and testicular cancer. Regular **veterinary care** is also essential for maintaining a cat's overall health and detecting any potential health issues early on.

## Common Health Issues in Aging Cats
As cats age, they are more prone to developing chronic diseases, such as **kidney disease**, **diabetes**, and **arthritis**. According to the [International Journal of Geriatric Veterinary Medicine](https://www.ivis.org), these conditions can significantly impact a cat's quality of life. However, with proper management and care, many cats can continue to live happy and healthy lives.

### Can cats live up to 25 years?
While it is rare, some cats can live up to 25 years or more with proper care and attention to their health.
### How often should I take my cat to the vet?
Regular veterinary care is essential for maintaining a cat's overall health and detecting any potential health issues early on. The [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org) recommends annual check-ups for cats.

Chronic diseases, such as kidney disease, diabetes, and arthritis, are common in senior cats. However, with proper management and care, many cats can continue to live happy and healthy lives.
