## Introduction to Feline CKD
Feline chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a progressive condition affecting **15-30% of cats over age 10**, according to the [International Renal Interest Society (2019)](https://www.iris-kidney.com). It occurs when kidneys lose their ability to filter waste, leading to toxin buildup. Early detection is critical—cats diagnosed at **Stage 1 CKD** (creatinine <1.6 mg/dL) live **2-4 years longer** than those diagnosed at later stages.

## Causes and Risk Factors of Feline CKD
Key contributors to **feline kidney disease** include:
1. **Age**: Risk increases **3-fold** after age 10 (Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine, 2020).
2. **Genetics**: Persians and Siamese have **40% higher predisposition**.
3. **Diet**: High-phosphorus foods accelerate kidney damage.
4. **Comorbidities**: 68% of hyperthyroid cats develop CKD (Cornell University, 2021).

## Symptoms of Feline CKD
**Classic cat kidney disease symptoms** (American Animal Hospital Association, 2020):
1. **Polyuria/polydipsia** (excessive drinking/urination) - 89% of cases
2. **Weight loss** - 62% of cats
3. **Vomiting** - 45% of cases

Subtle signs:
- Bad breath (uremic odor)
- Poor coat quality
- Lethargy during play

## Diagnosing Feline CKD
The [European College of Veterinary Internal Medicine (2018)](https://www.ecvim-ca.org) recommends:
1. **Blood tests**: SDMA >14 μg/dL + creatinine >1.6 mg/dL
2. **Urinalysis**: Specific gravity <1.035 + proteinuria
3. **Ultrasound**: Kidney size <3.5 (normal: 3.5-4.3 cm)

## Treatment and Management of Feline CKD
**Proven interventions** (Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association, 2019):
1. **Diet**: Switch to **low-phosphorus (<0.5%)**, high-quality protein like [ Hill's Prescription Diet k/d Kidney Care Cat Food en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Hill's+Prescription+Diet+k/d+Kidney+Care+Cat+Food&tag=vds96-20).
2. **Fluids**: Subcutaneous administration 2-3x weekly (100-150 mL/kg).
3. **Medications**: 
   - Potassium supplements if blood levels <3.5 mEq/L
   - Anti-nausea drugs (maropitant) for vomiting

## Prognosis and Monitoring of Feline CKD
Median survival times by IRIS stage (Veterinary Information Network, 2020):
- **Stage 2**: 3 years
- **Stage 3**: 1.5 years
- **Stage 4**: <6 months

Monitor every **3 months**:
1. Blood pressure (<160 mmHg)
2. BUN (<30 mg/dL)
3. Body weight (target: stable ±5%)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long can a cat live with kidney disease?
Cats with **early-stage CKD** (IRIS 1-2) live **2-5 years** with treatment, while late-stage (IRIS 4) typically survive **months**. A University of Georgia study (2022) showed 73% of cats receiving subcutaneous fluids 3x weekly survived >2 years.

### What are the final stages of kidney failure in cats?
End-stage **feline kidney failure** involves: 
- Inability to eat
- Severe dehydration
- Seizures from toxin buildup. Euthanasia is often recommended when quality of life declines, measured by the HHHHHMM scale (pain, hydration, hunger).

### Is wet or dry food better for cats with kidney disease?
**Wet food is superior**—it provides **70% more moisture**, reducing kidney workload. A 2021 Waltham Centre study found cats on wet diets had **40% slower CKD progression** versus dry food. [ Royal Canin Renal Support E Loaf en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Royal+Canin+Renal+Support+E+Loaf&tag=vds96-20) is a vet-recommended option.

### Can cats recover from chronic kidney disease?
No**, CKD is irreversible but manageable. The kidneys' nephrons don't regenerate, but progression can be slowed **by 50-60%** with phosphorus binders and ACE inhibitors (Journal of Feline Medicine, 2020).

### How much does it cost to treat a cat with kidney disease?
Annual costs range **$1,200-$4,500**:
- Diagnostics: $300-600
- Prescription food: $600/year
- Fluids/meds: $30-200/month
