## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Diet
The best diet for sterilized cats after 1 year is crucial for maintaining their overall health and preventing obesity-related diseases. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/), sterilized cats have different caloric needs compared to intact cats. 

## Caloric Needs of Sterilized Cats
The research by the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/) in 2019 shows that sterilized cats require **20-30% fewer calories** than intact cats. The following table compares the caloric needs of intact vs sterilized cats:
| Cat Type | Caloric Needs |
| --- | --- |
| Intact Male | 250-300 kcal/day |
| Intact Female | 200-250 kcal/day |
| Sterilized Male | 200-230 kcal/day |
| Sterilized Female | 150-200 kcal/day |

## Factors Affecting Caloric Needs
The [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu/) in 2006 found that factors such as age, sex, and weight affect the caloric needs of sterilized cats. For example, a **10-year-old sterilized cat** may require **10-15% fewer calories** than a 5-year-old sterilized cat. To calculate an individual cat's caloric needs, follow these steps:
1. Determine the cat's ideal weight
2. Calculate the cat's daily caloric needs based on its ideal weight
3. Adjust the caloric needs based on the cat's age, sex, and activity level

The [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/) in 2020 found that a balanced and nutrient-rich diet is essential for maintaining the health of sterilized cats. A diet that is high in **protein** and **fiber** and low in **carbohydrates** is recommended. ## Homemade Diets for Sterilized Cats
The [Academy of Veterinary Nutrition (AVN)](https://www.academyofveterinarynutrition.org/) in 2018 provided a recipe for a homemade diet for sterilized cats. The recipe includes **1 pound of chicken breast**, **1/2 cup of cooked brown rice**, and **1/4 cup of canned tuna**. However, it is essential to consult with a veterinarian before making any changes to a cat's diet.

## Monitoring Weight and Health
The [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/) in 2019 emphasized the importance of regular monitoring of a sterilized cat's weight and health. Follow these steps to monitor a cat's weight and health:
1. Weigh the cat regularly
2. Monitor the cat's body condition score
3. Schedule regular veterinary visits

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the best diet for sterilized cats?
The best diet for sterilized cats is a balanced and nutrient-rich diet that is high in protein and fiber and low in carbohydrates. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/), a diet that meets these requirements can help maintain the health of sterilized cats.
### How often should I feed my sterilized cat?
The frequency of feeding a sterilized cat depends on its age, sex, and activity level. According to the [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu/), adult sterilized cats can be fed **2-3 times a day**.
### Can I give my sterilized cat treats?
Yes, but in moderation. According to the [Pet Food Institute](https://www.petfoodinstitute.org/), treats should not exceed **10% of the cat's daily caloric intake**.
### How can I prevent weight gain in my sterilized cat?
To prevent weight gain in a sterilized cat, monitor its food intake and ensure it gets regular exercise. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org/), **10-15 minutes of playtime per day** can help maintain a cat's physical health.
### What are the health risks associated with sterilized cats?
Sterilized cats are at risk of obesity-related diseases, such as **diabetes** and **arthritis**. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/), regular monitoring of a cat's weight and health can help prevent these diseases.
### How can I ensure my sterilized cat gets enough nutrients?
To ensure a sterilized cat gets enough nutrients, feed a balanced and nutrient-rich diet. According to the [Academy of Veterinary Nutrition (AVN)](https://www.academyofveterinarynutrition.org/), a diet that includes **essential vitamins and minerals** can help maintain a cat's overall health.
