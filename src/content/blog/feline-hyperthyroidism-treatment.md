## Introduction to Feline Hyperthyroidism
Feline hyperthyroidism is a common endocrine disorder affecting **30-40% of cats** over the age of 10, according to the American Association of Feline Practitioners (AAFP) (2020). This condition occurs when the thyroid gland produces excessive amounts of thyroid hormones, leading to various health issues. The AAFP reports that **70% of cats** with hyperthyroidism are over 13 years old.

## Symptoms of Feline Hyperthyroidism
Common **feline hyperthyroidism symptoms** include weight loss, increased appetite, and hyperactivity. Less common symptoms are vomiting and diarrhea, as reported by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019). A study published in this journal found that **60% of cats** with hyperthyroidism exhibit weight loss despite increased appetite.

## Causes and Risk Factors of Feline Hyperthyroidism
The causes of feline hyperthyroidism are not fully understood, but **genetics**, age, and environmental factors are thought to play a role. The Veterinary Journal (2018) suggests a potential link between iodine intake and the development of hyperthyroidism in cats. **90% of cases** are caused by a benign tumor on the thyroid gland.

## Diagnosing Feline Hyperthyroidism
Diagnosing feline hyperthyroidism involves blood work, thyroid scanning, and ultrasound. The Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine (2020) states that **80% of cats** with hyperthyroidism have elevated thyroid hormone levels in their blood.
