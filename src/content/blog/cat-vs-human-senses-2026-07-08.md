## Visual Acuity Comparison

Cats' **visual acuity** is significantly different from humans'. While humans typically have **20/20 vision**, cats range between **20/100 to 20/200**, meaning they see at 20 feet what humans can see clearly at 100-200 feet ([American Academy of Ophthalmology, 2020](https://www.aao.org)). However, cats excel in low-light conditions due to the **tapetum lucidum**, a reflective layer in their eyes that amplifies available light.

### Key Differences:
- **Night Vision**: Cats need **1/6th the light** humans do to see clearly.
- **Color Perception**: Cats see blues and yellows but struggle with reds and greens.
- **Peripheral Vision**: Cats have a 200-degree field of view vs. humans' 180 degrees.

## Hearing Frequency Range

Cats' **feline senses** include extraordinary hearing capabilities. They detect sounds up to **79 kHz**, far beyond the human limit of **20 kHz** ([Journal of Comparative Physiology A, 2018](https://link.springer.com/journal/359)). Their ears can rotate **180 degrees** to pinpoint prey or threats with precision.

## Smell Sensitivity Comparison

Cats possess **200 million olfactory receptors**, dwarfing humans' **6 million** ([Scientific American, 2019](https://www.scientificamerican.com)). This allows them to detect **pheromones** and subtle environmental changes, explaining behaviors like scent-marking with cheeks or urine.

## Senses Interaction and Priority

Cats' senses don’t work in isolation. Research shows their **sense of smell can override vision** when identifying familiar humans or prey ([Animal Behaviour, 2017](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/animal-behaviour)). This explains why cats may sniff intently at objects they’ve already seen.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can cats see in complete darkness?
No, cats need some light to see. Their tapetum lucidum reflects **available light**, but total darkness renders them as blind as humans.

### Why do cats twitch their ears so much?
Cats adjust their **pinnae (ear flaps)** to locate sounds. Each ear moves independently to triangulate noises with precision.

### Do cats use smell more than sight?
Yes, for close-range identification. A 2017 study found cats rely on smell **within 1.5 feet**, switching to vision for distant objects.
### How to protect cats from loud noises?
Use **white noise machines** during fireworks (volume < 70 dB) and provide hiding spots like covered beds.

### Why do cats hate citrus smells?
Citrus oils contain **limonene**, which cats’ olfactory receptors detect as toxic. Avoid cleaners with citrus extracts.
