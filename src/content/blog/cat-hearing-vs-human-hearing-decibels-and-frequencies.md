## Introduction to Cat Hearing vs Human Hearing
When it comes to **cat hearing vs human hearing**, cats have a significant advantage, particularly in hearing higher frequencies. According to the [National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders (NIDCD)](https://www.nidcd.nih.gov/), cats can hear sounds in the range of **50-79 kHz**, while humans are limited to **20 Hz - 20 kHz**.

## How Cats Hear Higher Frequencies
This unique ability allows cats to detect even the slightest movements, making them skilled hunters. ## Why Cats Can Localize Sounds Better
Cats' ears are designed to rotate independently, allowing them to pinpoint the source of sounds more accurately than humans. According to a study published in the [Journal of the Acoustical Society of America](https://asa.scitation.org/) in **2015**, this unique ear anatomy enables cats to detect the slightest differences in sound waves, making them expert hunters.

## Practical Implications for Cat Owners
Cat owners can take advantage of their cat's acute hearing by using **high-pitched toys** or **whistling** to engage their cat's attention. Contrary to popular belief, cats cannot hear underwater, and their sense of smell is not more sensitive than their hearing. According to [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com/), cats have a unique sense of hearing that is adapted to their environment, but it is not superior to their sense of smell.

Cats' acute hearing has evolved to help them survive and thrive in their natural environment. As reported by [Science Daily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/) in **2017**, cats use their hearing to detect prey, avoid predators, and navigate their surroundings.
