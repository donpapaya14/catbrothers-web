## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Diet Plan
A **sterilized cat diet plan** is crucial to prevent obesity, as sterilization can lead to metabolic changes that increase appetite and fat storage. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14635241), 2018, neutered cats are more prone to weight gain due to hormonal changes.

## Why Sterilized Cats Gain Weight
The metabolic changes post-sterilization can lead to an increase in appetite and a decrease in energy expenditure. As stated by the [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int), maintaining a healthy weight is essential for overall health. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14635241), 2018, found that **63% of neutered cats** are overweight or obese.

## Exact Daily Calorie Requirements
To determine the exact daily calorie requirements, a calorie calculator based on weight, age, and activity level can be used. According to the [WSAVA Nutritional Guidelines](https://www.wsava.org), 2021, the daily calorie requirements for neutered cats are:
| Weight (kg) | Age | Activity Level | Daily Calorie Requirements |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2-4 | Adult | Sedentary | 150-200 kcal |
| 4-6 | Adult | Average | 200-250 kcal |
| 6-8 | Adult | High | 250-300 kcal |

## Best Protein-to-Fat Ratios
The ideal macronutrient breakdown to maintain muscle mass while limiting fat storage is crucial. A study published in the [Tufts University Veterinary Nutrition Study](https://vet.tufts.edu), 2020, found that a **protein-to-fat ratio of 2:1** is ideal for neutered cats.

## Portion Control Strategies that Work
Practical feeding schedules and measuring techniques can help prevent overfeeding. According to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu), 2022, feeding **2-3% of body weight** per day can help maintain a healthy weight.

## When to Switch to Weight Management Food
Clear signs that a cat needs specialized diet include:
* Weight gain
* Decreased activity level
* Increased appetite
According to the [AAFP Feline Nutrition Guidelines](https://www.aafp.org), 2021, switching to a weight management food can help prevent obesity.
