## What is Kidney Disease in Cats?
Kidney disease in cats, also known as **feline chronic kidney disease (CKD)**, is a common health issue affecting **30-50% of cats over 15 years old** (American Animal Hospital Association, 2020). It is caused by the gradual loss of kidney function, leading to a buildup of toxins in the blood. Symptoms include increased thirst and urination, weight loss, and **decreased appetite**.

## Nutritional Requirements for Cats with Kidney Disease
Cats with kidney disease require a **restricted protein diet** to reduce the strain on their kidneys (World Small Animal Veterinary Association, 2019). The ideal diet should contain **20-30% protein** on a dry matter basis, with a **phosphorus content of less than 1.5%**. Additionally, cats with kidney disease require a **balanced fatty acid profile** to support skin and coat health.

### Key Nutritional Requirements
| Nutrient | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Protein | 20-30% on dry matter basis |
| Phosphorus | Less than 1.5% |
| Fatty Acids | Balanced profile |

## What to Look for in a Cat Food for Kidney Disease
When choosing a cat food for kidney disease, look for the following:
1. **Restricted protein content**: Less than 30% on a dry matter basis
2. **Low phosphorus content**: Less than 1.5%
3. **Balanced fatty acid profile**
4. **No added salt or sugars**

### Reading Cat Food Labels
When reading cat food labels, look for the following:
* **Guaranteed analysis**: Lists the minimum and maximum values for protein, fat, and other nutrients
* **Ingredient list**: Lists the ingredients used in the food, in order of proportion

## Raw Diet vs. Cooked Diet for Cats with Kidney Disease
A raw diet may not be suitable for cats with kidney disease, as it can be **high in protein and phosphorus** (Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery, 2018). A cooked diet, on the other hand, can be formulated to meet the nutritional requirements of cats with kidney disease.
