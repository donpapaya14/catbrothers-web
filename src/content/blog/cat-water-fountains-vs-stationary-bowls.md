When it comes to **cat water fountain vs stationary bowl**, the choice can significantly impact your cat's hydration and overall health. ## Why Cats Prefer Running Water
The science behind feline preference for running water includes the role of **vision**, **sound**, and **smell**. Cats are visually oriented and can see the movement of water, which stimulates their desire to drink. The sound of running water also plays a crucial role, as it alerts them to the presence of water. Furthermore, the smell of fresh water is more appealing to cats than stagnant water. A study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery) (2018) found that **71% of cats** preferred to drink from a flowing water source.

## Types of Cat Water Fountains
There are several types of **cat water fountains** available, including **bubblers**, **splashes**, and **circulating systems**. Bubblers release air into the water, creating a gentle flow, while splashes use a small pump to circulate the water. Circulating systems use a combination of pumps and filters to keep the water fresh and clean.
