## Indoor vs Outdoor: Average Lifespan Comparison
The **indoor outdoor cat lifespan difference** is significant, with indoor cats living **12-20 years** on average, compared to **2-5 years** for outdoor cats, according to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com/) (2015). The primary causes of death disparity include vehicle accidents, predation, and disease transmission.

## Top 3 Risks That Shorten Outdoor Cat Lifespan
Outdoor cats face numerous risks, with **40% of deaths** attributed to vehicle accidents, followed by predation and disease transmission, as reported by the [American Veterinary Medical Association](https://www.avma.org/) (AVMA 2020). Here are the top 3 risks:
1. **Vehicle accidents**: 40% of outdoor cat deaths
2. **Predation**: 25% of outdoor cat deaths
3. **Disease transmission**: 15% of outdoor cat deaths

## The Indoor Cat Longevity Paradox
Indoor cats may develop **obesity** and **diabetes** more often, but they still outlive outdoor cats, according to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) (2022). To mitigate this, indoor cats require **at least 30 minutes of playtime** per day, divided into **2-3 sessions**.

## Hybrid Approach: Safe Outdoor Time Solutions

Some cat breeds, such as **Bengals**, are more adapted to outdoor life, while **brachycephalic breeds** face increased risks, according to a [PLOS ONE Genetics Study](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/) (2019). Here is a comparison of breed-specific lifespan differences:
