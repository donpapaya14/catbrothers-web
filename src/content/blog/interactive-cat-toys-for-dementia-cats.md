## Understanding Feline Cognitive Decline
Feline cognitive decline affects **40% of cats** over the age of 12, according to a study by the Certification in Animal Behavior and Training (CABT) 2020. The 7 stages of feline cognitive decline include common signs and symptoms such as confusion, disorientation, and forgetfulness.

## The Importance of Environmental Enrichment
Environmental enrichment plays a crucial role in slowing down dementia in cats. The Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery 2018 suggests that creating a stimulating home environment with vertical space, hiding places, and scent work can help. Here are some tips:
1. Provide **multiple levels** of vertical space for your cat to climb and explore.
2. Create **hiding places** using cardboard boxes or tunnels.
3. Engage your cat's sense of smell with **scent work** activities.

## Sensory Stimulation for Feline Cognition
Sensory stimulation is essential for maintaining cognitive function in cats. Animal Behaviour and Cognition 2019 found that **touch**, **smell**, and **hearing** are crucial senses for feline cognition. Examples of sensory toys and activities include:
1. **Brushing** your cat's fur to stimulate their sense of touch.
2. **Hiding treats** with a strong scent to encourage your cat to use their sense of smell.
3. **Playing calming music** to reduce stress and stimulate their sense of hearing.

## Creating a Dementia-Friendly Home Environment
Creating a dementia-friendly home environment can help your cat feel more comfortable and secure. PetMD 2020 suggests making modifications to daily routines, feeding, and exercise. Here are some practical tips:
1. Establish a **regular routine** for feeding, playtime, and sleep.
2. Provide a **quiet and comfortable** space for your cat to rest.
3. Encourage **gentle exercise** such as short walks or playtime with a favorite toy.

## When to Consult a Veterinary Behaviorist
Consulting a veterinary behaviorist is essential for personalized advice on managing dementia in cats. The American College of Veterinary Behavior 2020 recommends consulting a veterinary behaviorist if you notice any of the following red flags:
* **Increased aggression** or fearfulness
* **Changes in appetite** or eating habits
* **Increased vocalization** or meowing
