## Introduction to Cat Lifespan Factors Over 12 Years
The **cat lifespan factors over 12 years** are complex and multifaceted. According to a study by the Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2015), the average lifespan of a domestic cat is around 12-15 years. However, with proper care and attention to **longevity factors in cats**, some cats can live up to 20 years or more.

## Section 1: Genetics and Heredity
The role of genetics in determining a cat's lifespan is significant. A study by the Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2015) found that **breed-specific longevity** varies greatly. For example, the **Maine Coon** breed has an average lifespan of 12-15 years, while the **Sphynx** breed has an average lifespan of 15-20 years.

| Breed | Average Lifespan |
| --- | --- |
| Maine Coon | 12-15 years |
| Sphynx | 15-20 years |
| British Shorthair | 12-17 years |

## Section 2: Nutrition and Diet
A cat's diet plays a crucial role in determining its lifespan. A study by Royal Canin (2018) found that **omega-3 fatty acids** can help reduce inflammation and improve overall health. On the other hand, **excessive protein intake** can lead to kidney disease and other health problems.

Here are some tips for providing a balanced diet for your cat:
1. Feed a high-quality commercial cat food that meets your cat's nutritional needs.
2. Avoid overfeeding, as this can lead to obesity and other health problems.
3. Consider adding supplements, such as **omega-3 fatty acids**, to your cat's diet.

## Section 3: Lifestyle and Environment
A cat's lifestyle and environment can also impact its lifespan. A study by the University of Wisconsin-Madison (2012) found that **cognitive stimulation** can help improve a cat's mental and physical health. Here are some tips for providing a stimulating environment for your cat:
1. Provide plenty of toys and scratching posts to keep your cat active and engaged.
2. Create a **vertical space** for your cat to climb and explore.
3. Consider adding a **catio** or outdoor enclosure to provide your cat with fresh air and exercise.

## Section 4: Health Conditions and Age-Related Diseases
As cats age, they are more likely to develop certain health conditions. A study by the American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020) found that **kidney disease**, **arthritis**, and **cancer** are among the most common age-related diseases in cats. Here are some tips for detecting and managing these conditions:
1. Schedule regular veterinary check-ups to monitor your cat's health.
2. Watch for signs of illness, such as **weight loss**, **vomiting**, or **diarrhea**.
3. Consider adding supplements, such as **joint support**, to your cat's diet.

## Section 5: Veterinary Care and Wellness
Regular veterinary care is essential for maintaining a cat's overall health and longevity. A study by the American Veterinary Medical Association (2019) found that **regular vaccinations** and **preventative care** can help prevent certain diseases. Here are some tips for providing optimal veterinary care for your cat:
1. Schedule regular veterinary check-ups, at least once a year.
2. Stay up-to-date on your cat's vaccinations and preventative care.
3. Consider adding a **microchip** to your cat's identification.

## Section 6: Optimizing a Cat's Lifespan with Nutrition and Supplements
Providing a balanced diet and adding supplements can help optimize a cat's lifespan. A study by Nestle Purina PetCare (2020) found that **antioxidant supplements** can help reduce inflammation and improve overall health. Here are some tips for selecting the best food and supplements for your cat:
