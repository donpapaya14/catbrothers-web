## The Surprising Range of Cat Hearing
The upper limit of a cat's hearing range is **79 kHz**, compared to humans at **20 kHz**, according to the [National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders](https://www.nidcd.nih.gov/). This means cats can hear sounds that are too high for humans to detect.

## Human vs Cat Hearing Loss Thresholds
Humans typically experience hearing loss at **85 decibels (dB)**, while cats can suffer hearing loss at **50 dB**, as stated by the [American Academy of Audiology](https://www.audiology.org/). This highlights the importance of protecting your cat's hearing.

| --- | --- |

| Cat | 50 dB |

## How Loud is a Cat's Meow?
A cat's meow can reach **25 dB**, which is relatively quiet compared to other household noises, according to the [Journal of the Acoustical Society of America](https://asa.scitation.org/). For comparison, a vacuum cleaner can reach **90 dB**.

## The Impact of Age on Cat Hearing
As cats age, their hearing range and sensitivity decline, with **50% of cats** experiencing hearing loss by age 10, as reported by the [Journal of Gerontology](https://academic.oup.com/geronj). Regular check-ups with your veterinarian can help monitor your cat's hearing health.
