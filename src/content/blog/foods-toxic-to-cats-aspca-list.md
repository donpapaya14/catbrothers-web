## Introduction to Foods Toxic to Cats
The **foods toxic to cats ASPCA** list is a crucial resource for cat owners, highlighting the dangers of common household foods. According to the [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org), **chocolate**, **grapes**, and **onions** are among the top toxic foods for cats. A study by the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19391369) (2018) found that grape and raisin toxicity in cats can cause **kidney failure**.

## Top 10 Most Toxic Foods to Cats
The ASPCA's toxicity level ranking includes:
1. **Chocolate**: contains **theobromine**, which can cause **vomiting**, **diarrhea**, and **increased heart rate**.
2. **Grapes**: can cause **kidney failure** in cats, with even small amounts being toxic.
3. **Onions**: contain **N-propyl disulfide**, which can damage a cat's **red blood cells**.
4. **Macadamia nuts**: can cause **weakness**, **depression**, and **vomiting** in cats.
5. **Yeast dough**: can expand in a cat's stomach, causing **pain** and **discomfort**.
6. **Xylitol**: a sugar substitute that can cause **low blood sugar** and **liver failure** in cats.
7. **Caffeine**: can cause **vomiting**, **diarrhea**, and **increased heart rate** in cats.
8. **Raw or undercooked meat**: can contain **bacteria** like **Salmonella** and **E. coli**.
9. **Bones**: can cause **obstruction** or **splintering** in a cat's digestive tract.
10. **Avocado**: can cause **vomiting** and **diarrhea** in cats, although the **pit** and **skin** are more toxic than the flesh.

## Why Grapes and Raisins are Toxic to Cats
The exact mechanism of grape and raisin toxicity in cats is unknown, but it's believed to be related to the **tartaric acid** content. A study by the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19391369) (2018) found that even small amounts of grapes or raisins can cause **kidney failure** in cats.

## How to Identify Signs of Food Poisoning in Cats
Common signs of food poisoning in cats include:
* **Vomiting**
* **Diarrhea**
* **Lethargy**
* **Loss of appetite**
* **Abdominal pain**
If you suspect your cat has ingested a toxic food, contact your vet immediately.

## Treatment and Prevention of Food Toxicity in Cats
Treatment options for food toxicity in cats include:
1. **Activated charcoal**: to absorb the toxin
2. **Supportive care**: such as **fluid therapy** and **medication** to manage symptoms
3. **Dietary changes**: to prevent future incidents
Prevention strategies include keeping toxic foods out of reach and monitoring your cat's food intake.

## Common Household Foods That are Toxic to Cats
In addition to the top 10 list, other common household foods that are toxic to cats include:
* **Baked goods**: containing **chocolate** or **xylitol**
* **Candy**: containing **xylitol** or **caffeine**
* **Spices**: such as **onions** or **garlic**

## What to Do in Case of a Food Poisoning Emergency
If you suspect your cat has ingested a toxic food, follow these steps:
1. **Contact your vet**: immediately
2. **Provide information**: about the suspected toxic food and the amount ingested
3. **Follow instructions**: from your vet, such as administering **activated charcoal** or seeking **immediate veterinary care**
