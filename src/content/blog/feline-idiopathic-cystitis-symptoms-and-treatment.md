## What is Feline Idiopathic Cystitis? 
Feline idiopathic cystitis (FIC) is a common condition affecting **40-60% of cats** with feline lower urinary tract disease (FLUTD), according to the American Association of Feline Practitioners (2019). The condition is characterized by **painful urination**, **straining**, and **bloody urine**. 

## Causes and Risk Factors 
Potential triggers and underlying conditions contributing to FIC include **stress**, **genetics**, and **diet**, as reported by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018). A study by the Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine (2015) found that **30% of cats** with FIC had a history of urinary tract infections. 

## Symptoms and Diagnosis 
Recognizing FIC symptoms such as **straining**, **accidents outside the litter box**, and **bloody urine** is crucial for early diagnosis. According to Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2020), a urinalysis and urine culture can help diagnose the condition. 

## Treatment and Management Options 
Effective treatment strategies for FIC include **medication**, **dietary changes**, and **environmental modifications**, as recommended by Veterinary Clinics of North America: Small Animal Practice (2017). A study by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018) found that **80% of cats** with FIC responded to treatment with a combination of medication and dietary changes.

## Medications and Supplements 
Prescription and over-the-counter medications, as well as natural supplements, can help manage FIC symptoms and prevent future episodes. According to Merck Veterinary Manual (2022), **pain relief medication** and **anti-anxiety medication** can be effective in reducing symptoms.
