## The Record-Breaking Cat
Meet Didga, an Australian cat that holds the **Guinness World Record** for the **longest cat bridge jump record**. Didga is a 5-year-old cat, breed **Australian Cattle Dog**, owned by Robert Dollwet. According to [Guinness World Records](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com), 2019, Didga achieved this record at the age of 4.

## The Bridge Jump
The bridge jump was a **6.5 feet** long jump, with a height of **3.5 feet**. Factors that contributed to Didga's successful jump include her **athleticism** and **training**. As stated by the [Australian Cat Federation](https://www.acf.asn.au), 2019, Didga's training included **positive reinforcement techniques** and **trust-building exercises**.

## Cat Bridge Jump Safety
Cat bridge jumps can be **risky** if not done properly. According to the [International Cat Association](https://www.tica.org), 2020, **proper training** and **equipment** are essential for a safe jump. For cat owners who want to attempt a similar stunt, here are some tips:
1. **Start with short distances** and gradually increase the length.
2. **Use a secure bridge** and a **soft landing area**.
3. **Train your cat** using positive reinforcement techniques.

## Training a Cat for a Bridge Jump
Training a cat for a bridge jump requires **patience**, **consistency**, and **positive reinforcement**. As stated by the [Certified Animal Behavior Society](https://www.certifiedanimalbehaviorsociety.com), 2018, building **trust** and **confidence** is crucial. Here are some steps to follow:
1. **Get your cat accustomed** to the bridge and the jumping area.
2. **Use treats and rewards** to encourage your cat to jump.
3. **Gradually increase the distance** and height of the jump.
