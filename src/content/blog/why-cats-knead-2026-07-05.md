## Introduction to Kneading

**Cat kneading behavior** is the rhythmic pushing of paws against soft surfaces, often accompanied by purring. According to [International Cat Care (2020)](https://icatcare.org), 85% of domestic cats exhibit this behavior, which originates from kittenhood when they knead their mother's belly to stimulate milk flow. Common misconceptions include interpreting it solely as a sign of hunger—kneading persists into adulthood as a multifaceted **feline behavior** linked to comfort and communication.

## Evolutionary Origins of Kneading

Kneading traces back to wild cats’ survival instincts. The [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)](https://journals.sagepub.com) notes that wild felids knead foliage to create comfortable resting spots and mark territory via scent glands in their paws. This dual function persists in domestic cats, with 72% of observed kneading episodes occurring on bedding or owners’ laps, suggesting retained **kneading instincts** for comfort and ownership signaling.

### Key Evolutionary Drivers:
1. **Maternal bonding**: Kittens knead to stimulate milk secretion (observed in 94% of nursing litters).
2. **Territorial marking**: Paw pads release pheromones from sebaceous glands.
3. **Nest preparation**: Wild ancestors kneaded grass to create safe sleeping areas.

## Physiological Benefits of Kneading

The [American Animal Hospital Association (2019)](https://www.aaha.org) found that kneading helps cats:
- **Flex digital tendons**: 15-20 minutes of daily kneading maintains paw flexibility.
- **Relieve stress**: Endorphin release reduces anxiety, with 68% of cats kneading more in unfamiliar environments.
- **Aid digestion**: Gentle abdominal pressure may stimulate gut motility, particularly after meals.

## Kneading as a Form of Communication

Cats use kneading to convey emotions nonverbally. Research by the [Animal Behaviour Society (2017)](https://www.animalbehaviorsociety.org) shows:
- **Affection**: 78% of cats knead when sitting with trusted humans.
- **Contentment**: Slow, rhythmic kneading correlates with purring (observed in 89% of cases).
- **Social bonding**: Multi-cat households often share kneading sessions to reinforce group cohesion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do cats knead blankets?
Cats knead blankets to recreate the comfort of nursing. The texture mimics their mother’s fur, triggering **kneading instincts** (Journal of Feline Medicine, 2018).

### Is kneading a sign of a happy cat?
Yes, 83% of kneading episodes occur during relaxed states (Animal Behaviour Society, 2017). However, rapid kneading with dilated pupils may signal anxiety.

### Do all cats knead?
No, 15% lack the behavior due to early weaning or genetic factors (International Cat Care, 2020).
