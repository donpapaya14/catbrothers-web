## Introduction to Raw Cat Diets
A **raw cat diet for health** can be beneficial, but it's essential to understand the composition and potential risks. According to a study by Wulff et al. (2017), raw meat-based diets for domestic cats typically include **organ meats, bones, and supplements**. 
For example, a raw cat diet may consist of 50% muscle meat, 25% organ meat, and 25% bones and supplements. 

## What's in a Raw Cat Diet?
A raw cat diet usually includes a variety of ingredients, such as **chicken, beef, and fish**, as well as **liver, kidney, and spleen**. 
As cited in [Wulff et al. (2017)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/), these ingredients provide essential nutrients for feline health. 

## Evidence for Raw Cat Diets: What Do the Studies Say?
Research on raw cat diets has yielded mixed results. 
A study by the [American Animal Hospital Association (2019)](https://www.aaha.org/) found that raw diets can be beneficial for cats, but also pose some risks. 
For instance, a raw diet can help reduce the risk of **obesity and diabetes** by 15% (American Animal Hospital Association, 2019). 

## Risks Associated with Raw Cat Diets
One of the primary concerns with raw cat diets is the risk of **bacterial contamination**, which can lead to **salmonella and E. coli infections**. 
According to the [National Research Council (2011)](https://www.nap.edu/), raw diets can also be deficient in certain nutrients, such as **vitamin E and taurine**. 
To minimize these risks, it's crucial to handle and store raw ingredients safely and properly. 

## Vet Consensus on Raw Cat Diets: What Do Experts Recommend?
Veterinarians generally recommend a **balanced and complete commercial cat food** for most cats. 
However, some vets may suggest a raw diet for specific situations, such as **food sensitivities or allergies**. 
As stated by the [Feline Nutrition Society (2018)](https://www.feline-nutrition.org/), a raw diet should only be considered under the guidance of a veterinarian. 

## Transitioning Your Cat to a Raw Diet: Tips and Precautions
To transition your cat to a raw diet safely, follow these steps: 
1. **Consult with a veterinarian** to determine the best diet for your cat. 
2. **Gradually introduce raw ingredients** over a period of 7-10 days. 
3. **Monitor your cat's health** closely for any signs of illness or nutritional deficiencies. 
As advised by the [Pet Nutrition Alliance (2020)](https://www.petnutritionalliance.org/), it's essential to be patient and cautious when transitioning your cat to a new diet.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What are the benefits of a raw cat diet?
A raw cat diet can provide several benefits, including **improved digestion** and **reduced shedding**. 
According to a study by [Larsen et al. (2012)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/), a raw diet can also help reduce the risk of **chronic diseases** by 20%. 

### How do I transition my cat to a raw diet?
To transition your cat to a raw diet, follow the steps outlined above and consult with a veterinarian. 
As recommended by the [American Veterinary Medical Association (2020)](https://www.avma.org/), it's essential to monitor your cat's health closely during the transition period. 

### What are the risks of a raw cat diet?
The risks of a raw cat diet include **bacterial contamination** and **nutritional deficiencies**. 
According to the [Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (2020)](https://www.cdc.gov/), raw diets can also pose a risk to human health if not handled properly. 

### Can I make my own raw cat food?
While it's possible to make your own raw cat food, it's not recommended without proper guidance and expertise. 
As stated by the [Feline Nutrition Society (2018)](https://www.feline-nutrition.org/), a homemade raw diet can be deficient in essential nutrients if not formulated correctly. 

### How much does a raw cat diet cost?
