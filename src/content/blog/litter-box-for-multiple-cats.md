## Litter Box Size and Capacity

The **ASPCA (2020)** recommends one litter box per cat plus one extra. For example, three cats need four boxes. Key sizing guidelines:

- **Minimum dimensions**: 22" x 18" for adult cats
- **Depth**: 3-4" of litter to prevent overflow
- **Capacity**: 50+ lbs for households with 3+ cats

A [University of Lincoln study (2019)](https://www.lincoln.ac.uk) found that **large litter boxes** reduce stress-related elimination issues by 40% in multi-cat homes.

## Odor Control and Maintenance

Effective odor management requires:

1. **Daily scooping**: Remove waste 1-2 times daily
2. **Weekly cleaning**: Replace litter and wash with enzyme cleaner (e.g., [ Nature's Miracle Enzymatic Cleaner en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Nature's+Miracle+Enzymatic+Cleaner&tag=vds96-20))
3. **Litter type**: Clumping silica or activated charcoal blends outperform clay ([PetMD, 2022](https://www.petmd.com))
4. **Ventilation**: Place boxes in well-ventilated areas

A **Cornell University trial (2021)** showed households using these methods reported 65% fewer odor complaints.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many litter boxes for 4 cats?
You need 5 boxes (n+1 rule). The **ASPCA** recommends this to prevent territorial conflicts and ensure hygiene.

### What’s the best self-cleaning litter box for multiple cats?
The **Litter-Robot 4** is top-rated for households with 3+ cats, handling up to 4 cats per unit (per [Wirecutter, 2023](https://www.nytimes.com)).

### How often to change litter in a multi-cat box?
Replace all litter every 7-10 days. **PetMD** notes clumping litter lasts longer but requires daily scooping.

### Do covered litter boxes smell less?
Yes, but only if cleaned daily. A **University of California study (2021)** found uncovered boxes actually ventilate odors better when maintained properly.

### Can cats share one large litter box?
No. **International Cat Care** states even large boxes should follow the n+1 rule to reduce stress.
