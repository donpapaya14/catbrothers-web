## What is Taurine and Why is it Essential for Cats?
Taurine is an **amino acid** that plays a crucial role in feline metabolism, heart health, and vision. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA)](https://www.aaha.org), 2020 guidelines, taurine is essential for cats, and its deficiency can lead to severe health issues. 

## Taurine Deficiency in Cats: Symptoms and Consequences
A [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu) study in 2015 found that taurine deficiency can cause **dilated cardiomyopathy**, a condition that can lead to heart failure. Other symptoms include **retinal degeneration**, which can lead to blindness. 

## How to Choose Cat Food with Optimal Taurine Levels
The [Association of American Feed Control Officials (AAFCO)](https://www.aafco.org) 2022 standards recommend a minimum of **0.1% taurine** in dry cat food and **0.2% in wet cat food**. When choosing cat food, look for brands that meet or exceed these standards.

## Taurine-Rich Foods for Cats: Human Foods to Supplement Your Cat's Diet
According to [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com), 2020 article, taurine-rich human foods include **shellfish**, **organ meats**, and **dark leafy greens**. However, it's essential to feed these foods in **moderation** and as part of a **balanced diet**.

## Interactions between Taurine and Other Feline Nutrients
The [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu) 2006 guidelines state that taurine interacts with other essential nutrients, such as **vitamin E** and **omega-3 fatty acids**. A balanced diet that includes these nutrients can help prevent deficiencies.

The recommended daily intake of taurine for cats is **50-100mg per kilogram of body weight**. According to the [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int), a cat's diet should provide all the necessary nutrients, including taurine.
