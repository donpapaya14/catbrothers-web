## Cats hear 1.6x higher frequencies than humans
Cats detect up to **64 kHz** vs humans' **20 kHz**, with exact frequency range infographic, as reported by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com) (2021). This means cats can hear sounds that are too high for humans to detect.

## Decibel sensitivity: Why vacuum cleaners hurt cat ears
Cats hear sounds at **-15 dB** (humans need **0 dB**) with examples of common household noises, according to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) (2022). For instance, a vacuum cleaner can produce sounds up to **90 dB**, which can be painful for cats.

## Ultrasonic pest repellers: Harmful to cats?
Analysis of **22-55 kHz** devices and safe distance recommendations, as per the [AVMA](https://www.avma.org) ultrasound safety guidelines (2020), suggest that these devices can be harmful to cats if they are too close. It is recommended to keep these devices at least **10 feet** away from your cat.

## The 'twitch test': How cats locate prey 3x faster
Time comparison: Cats pinpoint sounds in **0.05 sec** vs human **0.15 sec**, as found in the [University of Lincoln](https://www.lincoln.ac.uk) feline cognition study (2019). This means cats can react faster to sounds than humans.
