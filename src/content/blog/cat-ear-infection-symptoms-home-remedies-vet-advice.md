## Subtle signs your cat has an ear infection
If you suspect your cat has an ear infection, look for **excessive blinking**, **head tilt without discharge**, or **avoiding cheek rubs**, as noted by the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) in 2022. These less obvious symptoms can indicate an underlying issue.

## When home treatment is (and isn't) appropriate
Home care can be attempted in **3 scenarios**: no visible trauma but mild odor, slight redness without discharge, or after a vet has diagnosed a mild infection. However, **immediate vet visits** are required for severe symptoms, discharge, or if your cat is in pain, as per the [AAFP Feline Guidelines](https://www.catvets.com/) 2023.

## Step-by-step ear cleaning with household items
To clean your cat's ears, mix a **saline solution** with a 1:10 dilution ratio of salt to water. Use a **cotton ball**, not a Q-tip, to gently wipe the outer ear canal. For restraint, **wrap your cat in a towel** to minimize movement, as recommended by the [Veterinary Medical Association of NYC](https://www.vmanyc.org/) in 2021.

## Vet-recommended natural remedies
For natural remedies, try **chilled chamomile tea bags**. Soak a tea bag in cold water, then place it in the refrigerator for 30 minutes. Apply the cooled tea bag to the affected ear for 5-7 minutes, 2-3 times a day. According to [Tufts University Veterinary Medicine](https://vet.tufts.edu/), this method can help soothe the ear canal.

## Products that make infections worse
Avoid using **hydrogen peroxide** or **alcohol wipes**, as they can damage the feline ear canal, notes the [Journal of Feline Medicine](https://www.jfms.com/) in 2023. These products can push debris further into the ear, exacerbating the infection.

## How to monitor progress effectively
Monitor your cat's progress at **24**, **48**, and **72-hour checkpoints**. If you notice improvement, continue with the prescribed treatment. However, if symptoms worsen, seek immediate veterinary attention, advises [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/) in 2024.
