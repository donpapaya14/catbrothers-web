## What Age is Considered Old for Cats?
The definition of old age for cats differs from human aging. According to a study by the [American Association of Feline Practitioners (AAFP)](https://www.aafp.org/), 2018, cats are considered **senior** at the age of **11-14 years** and **geriatric** at **15 years** or older. This classification is based on the average **cat lifespan factors research study**, which shows that cats in this age group are more prone to age-related health issues.

## Genetic Factors Affecting Cat Lifespan
Research on genetic factors that influence cat lifespan has shed light on the role of **telomere length**. A study by the [National Institutes of Health (NIH)](https://www.nih.gov/), 2015, found that shorter telomeres are associated with **premature aging** in cats. This information can be used to identify cats at higher risk of age-related health issues.

## Lifestyle Factors That Increase Cat Lifespan
Practical advice on how to increase your cat's lifespan includes making lifestyle changes such as:
1. Providing a **balanced diet** with essential nutrients
2. Encouraging **regular exercise** through play
3. Offering **mental stimulation** through interactive toys and activities
A study by the [University of California, Davis](https://www.ucdavis.edu/), 2019, found that cats that receive regular exercise and mental stimulation have a **lower risk of obesity** and related health issues.

## The Role of Nutrition in Cat Lifespan
The impact of diet on cat lifespan cannot be overstated. A **balanced and nutrient-rich diet** is essential for maintaining overall health. Research by the [Waltham Centre for Pet Nutrition](https://www.waltham.com/), 2020, found that **overfeeding and obesity** are significant risk factors for age-related health issues in cats. Feeding your cat a high-quality diet such as [Purina Pro Plan Senior Cat Food](https://www.amazon.com/Purina-Pro-Plan-Senior-Cat-Food) can help support their overall health and wellbeing.

## How to Identify and Manage Age-Related Health Issues
Recognizing the signs of age-related health issues in cats is crucial for providing effective care. Common health issues in older cats include:
* **Kidney disease**
* **Arthritis**
* **Cognitive decline**
Guidelines from the [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/) organization, 2020, provide practical tips on how to manage these conditions, including:
1. **Regular veterinary check-ups**
2. **Monitoring for signs of illness**
3. **Providing a comfortable and supportive environment**
