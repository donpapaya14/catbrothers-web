## Introduction to Cat Senses
The **cat senses comparison** is a fascinating topic that highlights the unique abilities of felines. When it comes to **cat senses**, research by the [American College of Veterinary Ophthalmologists](https://www.acvo.org) (2020) shows that cats have a visual acuity of 20/100, compared to humans who have a visual acuity of 20/20.

## Vision Comparison
Cats have a **field of vision** of almost 200 degrees, whereas humans have a field of vision of around 180 degrees. Additionally, cats can see colors in the blue and violet spectrum, but not in the red spectrum, according to the [American College of Veterinary Ophthalmologists](https://www.acvo.org) (2020).

## Hearing Range
Cats can hear sounds at frequencies as high as **79 kHz**, while humans can only hear sounds up to 20 kHz, as reported in the [Journal of Comparative Physiology A](https://link.springer.com/journal/365) (2019). This allows cats to detect **ultrasonic sounds** that are beyond human range.

## Smell Sensitivity
Cats have **200 million olfactory receptors**, compared to only 6 million in humans, according to [Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com) (2018). This makes them highly sensitive to smells, including **pheromones**.

## Comparison of Sensory Brain Areas
The brain of a cat dedicates **40%** of its area to processing sensory information, compared to only **10%** in humans, as stated by the [National Institute of Mental Health](https://www.nimh.nih.gov) (2017).
