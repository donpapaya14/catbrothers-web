## Introduction to Feline Nutrition

Cats require a diet rich in **animal-based proteins**, moderate fats, and minimal carbohydrates. According to the [National Research Council (2006)](https://www.nap.edu), felines need **at least 26% protein** in their diet for maintenance, with higher requirements for growing kittens (30-40%). Unlike dogs, cats lack specific enzymes to process plant-based proteins efficiently, making **wet food vs dry food cats** a critical discussion for optimal **cat nutrition**.

Key nutritional differences:
- **Wet food**: Typically contains **70-80% moisture**, mimicking natural prey.
- **Dry food**: Only **5-10% moisture**, often higher in carbohydrates (up to 50% in some brands).

## Moisture Content Comparison

A [Journal of Animal Science (2018)](https://www.journalofanimalscience.org) study found cats fed exclusively dry food consumed **50% less water** than those on wet diets, increasing risks of urinary tract diseases. Key data:

| Metric          | Wet Food | Dry Food |
|-----------------|----------|----------|
| Moisture Content | 70-80%   | 5-10%    |
| Water Intake    | 30-50ml/kg/day | 10-20ml/kg/day |

## Digestive Health

Wet food promotes **better gastrointestinal motility** due to higher moisture content. A 2015 [American Journal of Veterinary Research](https://avmajournals.avma.org) study showed cats on wet diets had **28% faster gastric emptying times** and more balanced gut microbiota. Dry food may cause constipation in sedentary cats.

## Dental Health Considerations

Contrary to popular belief, dry food doesn’t clean teeth effectively. The [Journal of Veterinary Dentistry (2019)](https://www.avdc.org) found **no significant reduction in plaque** between cats fed dry vs wet food. Dental health requires:
1. Annual veterinary cleanings
2. Daily brushing with feline toothpaste

## Veterinary Recommendations

The [American Animal Hospital Association (2020)](https://www.aaha.org) recommends:
1. **Kittens**: Wet food for easier chewing and hydration
2. **Adults**: 50/50 mix of wet and dry
3. **Seniors**: Predominantly wet food to support kidney function

## Making an Informed Decision

Follow this 3-step decision guide from the [International Journal of Animal Nutrition (2022)](https://www.sciencedirect.com):
1. Assess your cat’s **hydration status** (skin tent test)
2. Check for **dental issues** (bad breath, red gums)
3. Consider **activity level** (indoor cats benefit from wet food)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is wet or dry food better for overweight cats?
Wet food is superior for weight management. A 2020 University of Illinois study found cats on wet diets had **18% lower body fat** due to higher protein and lower carb content.

### Can dry food replace wet food completely?
No. The WSAVA states cats need **at least 20% of their diet as wet food** to prevent chronic dehydration and kidney disease.

### How often should I feed my cat wet food?
Veterinarians recommend **2-3 wet meals daily** (1/4 cup per meal) with free-access dry food for grazing.

### Does wet food cause diarrhea in cats?
Only if introduced abruptly. Transition over **7 days**: Day 1-2: 25% wet, Day 3-4: 50%, Day 5-7: 75-100%.

### Is grain-free dry food healthier?
Not necessarily. The FDA (2021) linked some grain-free diets to **dilated cardiomyopathy** in cats due to pea protein overload.
