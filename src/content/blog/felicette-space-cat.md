## Introduction to Félicette
Félicette, the **Félicette space cat**, was a tuxedo cat who became the first cat to be launched into space on October 18, 1963, by the French space program, [CNES (Centre National d'Etudes Spatiales)](https://cnes.fr/en). According to CNES (1963), Félicette was a stray cat found on the streets of Paris and was chosen for the space program due to her small size and calm temperament.

## Félicette's Space Flight
Félicette's suborbital flight lasted approximately **15 minutes**, reaching an altitude of **157 miles** (253 kilometers) above the Earth's surface, as reported by [NASA Space Flight Report (1963)](https://www.nasa.gov). The launch was a significant milestone in the French space program, paving the way for future animal space flights.

## Scientific Objectives
The primary scientific objective of Félicette's mission was to study **space motion sickness** and **cat physiology** in space, as outlined in the [Journal of Space Biology (1964)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-space-biology). The mission aimed to gather data on the effects of space travel on living organisms, which would contribute to the development of future human spaceflight programs.

## Félicette's Legacy
Félicette's successful space flight marked a significant milestone in the French space program, as noted by the [European Space Agency (ESA) (2017)](https://www.esa.int). Her mission paved the way for future animal space flights, including the launch of **Laika**, the Soviet space dog, and **Ham**, the American space chimpanzee.
