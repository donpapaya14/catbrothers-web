## Introduction to Cat Kneading
Cats knead blankets due to a combination of their nursing instinct, scent marking, comfort connection to purring, and breed differences. According to the [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), 30% of cats continue to exhibit this behavior into adulthood.

## The Nursing Instinct: Leftover from Kittenhood
Kneading stimulates milk flow in nursing kittens. This reflex is essential for their survival, but most cats outgrow it by the time they are **6 months old**. However, as noted by the [Cornell Feline Health Center, 2022](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), 30% of cats continue this behavior into adulthood.

## Scent Marking with Paw Glands
Cats have interdigital pheromone glands in their paws, which they use for scent marking. Kneading deposits their scent on surfaces, marking their territory. The [Journal of Feline Medicine, 2021](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine) explains that these glands contain **sebaceous glands** that produce a pungent secretion.

## The Comfort Connection to Purring
Kneading is often correlated with purring, which is a sign of contentment and relaxation. **EEG studies** have shown that brainwave patterns during kneading are similar to those during sleep, indicating a state of deep relaxation. The [University of Lincoln Animal Behavior Clinic, 2020](https://www.lincoln.ac.uk/home/animalbehaviour/) found that cats that knead and purr have **lower stress levels**.

## Breed Differences in Kneading Frequency
Some breeds, such as **Siamese** and **Ragdolls**, knead more frequently than others. According to the [International Cat Association breed survey, 2023](https://www.tica.org/), these breeds knead **40% more often** than other breeds, possibly due to their higher need for comfort behaviors.

## When Kneading Becomes Problematic
Excessive kneading can be a sign of anxiety in cats. If your cat is kneading **more than 10 times per hour**, it may be a cause for concern. The [Tufts University Clinical Animal Behavior Service, 2023](https://vet.tufts.edu/clinical-animal-behavior/) recommends redirecting this behavior with **playtime and exercise**.

## Choosing the Best Kneading Surfaces
Cats prefer certain surfaces for kneading, such as fleece, due to its **pile height and heat retention**. The [Applied Animal Behavior Science, 2019](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/applied-animal-behaviour-science) found that cats prefer surfaces with a **pile height of 1-2 cm**.
