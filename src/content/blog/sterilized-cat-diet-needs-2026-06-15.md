## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Nutrition

Sterilization alters a cat's metabolism and appetite, requiring a tailored sterilized cat diet plan to prevent weight gain and maintain health. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association (AAHA, 2020)](https://www.aaha.org), spayed/neutered cats experience a **20-25% reduction in metabolic rate** while their appetite increases by up to 30%. This imbalance makes them prone to obesity, which affects **60% of sterilized cats** (AAHA, 2020).

Key adjustments include:
1. **Reduced calories**: 20-30% fewer calories than pre-sterilization
2. **Higher protein**: At least 30% of daily intake to preserve muscle mass
3. **Controlled fats**: Limit to 10-15% of diet to prevent weight gain

## Calculating Daily Caloric Needs

Use this formula from the [National Research Council (NRC, 2019)](https://www.nationalacademies.org) to calculate your cat's Resting Energy Requirement (RER):

```
RER (kcal/day) = 70 × (body weight in kg)^0.75
```

For sterilized cats, multiply RER by:
- **1.0** for inactive cats
- **1.2** for moderately active
- **1.4** for very active

```
70 × (4)^0.75 = 198 kcal/day × 1.0 = 198 kcal
```

## Carbohydrate and Fiber Needs

The [Veterinary Clinics of North America (2017)](https://www.vetsmall.theclinics.com) recommends:
- **<20% carbs**: Prevents blood sugar spikes
- **3-8% fiber**: Aids digestion and satiety

Top fiber sources:
1. Pumpkin (3.4g fiber per 100g)
2. Peas (5.5g fiber per 100g)
3. Beet pulp (18g fiber per 100g)

## Monitoring and Adjusting the Diet

Per [International Journal of Veterinary Medicine (2019)](https://www.ijarvm.com):

1. **Weigh monthly**: Ideal sterilized cat weight gain should be <1% body weight/month
2. **Body Condition Score (BCS)**: Aim for 4-5/9 (ribs palpable with slight fat cover)
3. **Adjust portions**: Reduce by 5-10% if gaining >50g/month

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should a sterilized cat eat per day?
A 4kg sterilized cat needs **160-200 kcal/day** (NRC, 2019). Divide into 2-3 meals to prevent overeating. Use the RER formula above for precise calculations.

### What's the best protein source for neutered cats?
Chicken and turkey are ideal, providing **30-34% protein** with lean fat content. Fish like salmon offer omega-3s but should be limited to 2x/week due to mercury risks (FDA, 2020).

### Can sterilized cats eat regular cat food?
No. Regular food contains **25% more calories** than sterilized formulas. A [Petfood Industry (2020)](https://www.petfoodindustry.com) study found cats fed standard diets gained 18% more weight in 6 months post-sterilization.

### How often should I weigh my spayed cat?
Monthly weigh-ins are critical. Use a baby scale (accurate to 10g) and track trends. A **5% weight gain in 3 months** warrants dietary adjustment (IJARVM, 2019).

### Are wet or dry foods better for sterilized cats?
Wet food is preferable (70% water content increases satiety), but dry food like [ Hill's Science Diet Sterilized Cat Food en Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=+Hill's+Science+Diet+Sterilized+Cat+Food&tag=vds96-20) works if portion-controlled. The AAHA recommends **at least 25% of diet as wet food**.
