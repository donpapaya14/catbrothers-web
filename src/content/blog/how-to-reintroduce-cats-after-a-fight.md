## Assess the Severity of the Fight
When **reintroducing cats after a fight**, it's crucial to first assess the severity of the altercation. According to [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/), 2022, **70% of cat fights** are minor and do not require veterinary attention. However, signs of stress or injury, such as **hissing**, **growling**, or **visible wounds**, should be monitored closely.

## Immediate Separation Protocol
After a fight, it's essential to separate the cats immediately to prevent further aggression. The [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center), 2021, recommends keeping them apart for at least **24-48 hours**. To separate them safely, use **blankets** or **towels** to block their line of sight, and place them in **separate rooms**.

## Scent Reintroduction Techniques
Before visual reintroduction, **scent reintroduction** can help rebuild familiarity between the cats. The [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/jfm), 2020, suggests rubbing **towels** on each cat's face and then swapping them, or using **shared bedding**. This process should be repeated for **3-5 days** before visual reintroduction.

## Controlled Visual Reintroduction
When reintroducing the cats visually, use **baby gates** or **screen doors** to allow supervised face-to-face meetings. The [American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA)](https://www.aspca.org/), 2023, recommends starting with **short sessions** of **5-10 minutes**, gradually increasing the duration over **7-10 days**.

## Positive Reinforcement Strategies
To associate each other's presence with positive experiences, use **treats**, **toys**, and **feeding routines**. The [Applied Animal Behaviour Science](https://www.journals.elsevier.com/applied-animal-behaviour-science/), 2019, suggests rewarding calm behavior with **high-value treats**, such as **tuna** or **chicken**.

## When to Seek Professional Help
If the cats exhibit **prolonged aggression** or **refusal to eat**, it's essential to seek professional help from a certified animal behaviorist. The [International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants](https://iaabc.org/), 2021, provides a directory of certified experts.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How long does it take to reintroduce cats after a fight?
The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/) recommends a **minimum of 7-10 days** for reintroduction, depending on the severity of the fight and the individual cats' personalities.
### What are the signs of stress or injury in cats?
According to [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org/), signs of stress or injury include **hissing**, **growling**, **visible wounds**, and **changes in appetite or behavior**.

### How often should I feed my cats during reintroduction?
The [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) recommends feeding your cats **2-3 times a day** during reintroduction, to maintain a consistent routine and reduce competition.
### What if my cats refuse to eat during reintroduction?
If your cats refuse to eat, it's essential to seek professional help from a certified animal behaviorist or a veterinarian, as **refusal to eat** can be a sign of **severe stress or anxiety**.
### Can I reintroduce my cats without professional help?
While it's possible to reintroduce your cats without professional help, it's highly recommended to seek guidance from a certified animal behaviorist to ensure a **safe and successful reintroduction**.
