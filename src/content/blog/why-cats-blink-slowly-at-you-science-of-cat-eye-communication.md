## The 'Cat I Love You' Blink: What It Really Means
When cats **slowly blink** at you, it's a sign of trust and affection. According to a 2020 study by the **University of Sussex**, this behavior is distinct from regular blinking. Cats use slow blinking to communicate comfort and security, similar to a human hug.

## Science Behind the Slow Blink: Oxytocin Connection
The **science behind slow blinking** reveals a connection to oxytocin, often referred to as the 'love hormone.' A 2022 study published in the **Animal Cognition journal** found that mutual slow blinking between cats and humans increases oxytocin levels, similar to the bonding experience between a mother and infant. This oxytocin connection strengthens the bond between cats and their owners.

## How to Properly 'Blink Back' at Your Cat
To initiate and respond to slow blinks, follow these steps:
1. **Make eye contact**: Look directly at your cat without staring aggressively.
2. **Slowly blink**: Close your eyes for about 2-3 seconds, then open them again.
3. **Repeat the process**: Continue blinking slowly to show your cat you're engaged and trusting.
As advised by the **International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants**, this technique helps build trust and encourages your cat to blink back.

## When Slow Blinking Doesn't Work: Reading Other Signals
Not all cats are receptive to blinking communication. According to the **Cornell Feline Health Center** (2021), signs that your cat isn't interested include:
- **Pupil dilation**: If your cat's pupils are dilated, it may indicate fear or aggression.
- **Ear position**: Ears laid back or flattened can signify discomfort or stress.
Understanding these signals helps you adjust your approach to better communicate with your cat.

## Wild Cats vs. Domestic Cats: Evolutionary Origins
The blinking behavior of domestic cats has evolutionary roots in their wild ancestors. The **Smithsonian Conservation Biology Institute** notes that big cats also display slow blinking, although its function may differ in the wild. Domestication has likely shaped this behavior in domestic cats, making it a unique form of communication with humans.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What does it mean when a cat blinks slowly at you?
When a cat blinks slowly at you, it's a sign of trust and affection. This behavior is a form of non-verbal communication that cats use to show comfort and security around their human companions.
### How do I know if my cat is blinking slowly at me?
To recognize a slow blink, look for a blink that is noticeably slower than a regular blink. It's almost as if your cat is saying, 'I trust you, and I'm comfortable around you.'
### Can I use slow blinking to calm my cat?
Yes, slow blinking can be used to calm anxious cats. By creating a calm environment, using products like the [Feliway Classic Cat Calming Diffuser], and engaging in slow blinking, you can help reduce your cat's stress levels.
### What if my cat doesn't blink back at me?
If your cat doesn't blink back, it may not be receptive to blinking communication at that moment. Look for other signs of comfort or stress, such as ear position and pupil dilation, to understand your cat's mood better.
### How often should I blink at my cat?
There's no specific frequency for blinking at your cat. The key is to be genuine and responsive. When you catch your cat's eye, slowly blink back to show you're engaged and trusting.
### Can slow blinking help with cat training?
While slow blinking itself isn't a training method, it can help establish a trusting relationship between you and your cat, making training easier and more effective.
