## Introduction to Cat Stress from Construction Noise
Cat stress from construction noise is a significant concern for many cat owners, as **cat stress construction noise** can lead to anxiety and behavioral issues in felines. According to a study published in the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com) (2022), cats' superior hearing, which can detect sounds up to **64 kHz** compared to humans' **20 kHz**, makes them more susceptible to low-frequency drilling and hammering sounds. This can cause a spike in cortisol levels, indicating **feline stress relief** is crucial.

## Why Construction Noise Triggers Cat Stress
The superior hearing of cats is not the only factor that contributes to **cat stress construction noise**. Ethological evidence suggests that cats are naturally inclined to respond to certain frequencies, which can be triggered by construction noise. A study by the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.jfms.com) (2022) found that **cortisol spikes** were more pronounced in cats exposed to low-frequency sounds.

## Hidden Stress Signs Most Owners Miss
Identifying **noise anxiety in cats** can be challenging, as they often exhibit subtle signs of stress. According to an [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org) behavior study (2021), some hidden stress signs include:
* Excessive lip licking
* Tail puffing at specific noise frequencies
* Sudden changes in scratching post use
These signs can be indicative of **calming cats during construction** needs.

## Soundproofing Hacks for Temporary Relief
Creating a 'safe room' for your cat can provide temporary relief from **cat stress construction noise**. Here's a step-by-step guide:
1. Use moving blankets on walls to reduce sound transmission.
2. Place white noise machines at cat ear height (**2-3 ft off the ground**).
3. Utilize HVAC noise masking to create a constant, soothing sound.
The [Cornell Feline Health Center](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/departments-centers-and-institutes/cornell-feline-health-center) noise mitigation guidelines provide more information on soundproofing.

## Pheromone Therapy Timing That Actually Works
**Feliway Classic Cat Calming Diffuser** can be an effective tool in reducing **cat stress construction noise**. However, it's essential to use it correctly. According to the [Veterinary Behavior Symposium](https://www.vin.com/apputil/content/defaultadv1.aspx?meta=Generic&pid=11326&id=6411735) (2023) clinical trials, Feliway diffusers need **72-hour pre-exposure setup** near escape routes to be effective.

## Construction Day Emergency Protocol
In case of an emergency, a play-therapy session can help release pent-up stress hormones. The [Applied Animal Behavior Science](https://www.journals.elsevier.com/applied-animal-behaviour-science) (2020) recommends:
* 5-minute play sessions every **90 minutes**
* Using feather wands to stimulate natural hunting behavior

## Long-term Desensitization Techniques
Desensitizing your cat to construction noise can be achieved through gradual exposure to recorded sounds. The [Tufts University Animal Behavior Clinic](https://vet.tufts.edu/behavior/) training method suggests:
