## Introduction to Feline Kneading
When cats knead after a long absence, it's a sign of comfort and security. **Feline kneading after separation** is a common behavior observed in cats, and it's linked to their memory and comfort. According to a study by the [University of Bristol](https://www.bristol.ac.uk) (2013) on the development of feline behavior, kneading is a leftover behavior from kittenhood, where they used to knead their mother's belly to stimulate milk production.

## The Science of Kneading
Kneading is a natural behavior for cats, and it's not just limited to their mothers. A study by the [University of Wisconsin-Madison](https://www.wisc.edu) (2017) found that **pheromones play a significant role in feline behavior**, including kneading. When cats knead, they release calming hormones, which help them relax and feel secure.

## Kneading as a Sign of Trust
Cats associate kneading with feelings of safety and security, which is why they often knead on their owner's lap or on a familiar surface. Research by [Dr. Bonnie Beaver](https://www.vetmed.tamu.edu) (2004) on cat behavior and bonding found that cats use kneading as a way to self-soothe and mark their territory. This behavior is a sign of trust and affection towards their owners.

## Recognizing the 'Return Ritual'
When a cat kneads after a long absence, it's often part of a 'return ritual' that includes other behaviors like rubbing, purring, and vocalization. According to an article by the [International Cat Care](https://icatcare.org) organization (2018), recognizing these behaviors can help owners understand their cat's emotional state and respond accordingly.
