## Introduction to Feline Longevity
The average **cat lifespan** is around 12-15 years, according to the American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020). However, with proper care and attention to **cat lifespan factors research**, some cats can live up to 20 years or more. Understanding the factors that influence **feline longevity** is crucial for cat owners who want to provide the best possible life for their pets.

## Genetics and Breed-Specific Lifespan
Different breeds have varying lifespans, with some breeds living longer than others. For example, the **Siamese** breed has an average lifespan of 15-20 years, while the **Maine Coon** breed has an average lifespan of 12-15 years, as reported by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019). Genetic factors also play a significant role in determining a cat's lifespan, with some breeds being more prone to certain health issues.

## Nutrition and Diet Impact on Cat Lifespan
**Nutrition** is a critical factor in determining a cat's lifespan. A study by the National Research Council (2016) found that cats fed a **high-protein diet** had a longer lifespan than those fed a low-protein diet. The type of food also matters, with **wet food** being generally better for cats than **dry food**. A comparison of the two is as follows:

## Environmental Factors and Cat Longevity
The environment in which a cat lives can also impact its lifespan. Indoor cats tend to live longer than outdoor cats, as they are less exposed to **toxins** and **stress**, according to Environmental Science & Technology (2018). A study found that indoor cats had a 2.5 times lower risk of death than outdoor cats.

## Health Conditions Affecting Cat Lifespan
Common health issues in aging cats include **kidney disease**, **diabetes**, and **arthritis**, as reported by the International Journal of Veterinary Science (2020). Managing these conditions through regular veterinary check-ups and proper care can help extend a cat's lifespan.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What is the average lifespan of a cat?
The average lifespan of a cat is around 12-15 years, according to the American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020).
### How often should I feed my cat?
The frequency of feeding depends on the age and health of your cat. Generally, adult cats should be fed **2-3 times a day**, while senior cats may require **more frequent, smaller meals**.
### Can cats be vegetarian?
While it is possible to feed cats a **vegetarian diet**, it is not recommended, as cats require a diet high in protein and fat, which is typically found in animal-based ingredients.
### How much exercise does my cat need?
Cats require **regular exercise** to stay healthy, which can include playtime with toys or interactive games. Aim for at least **30 minutes of playtime per day**.
### What are the signs of aging in cats?
Common signs of aging in cats include **weight loss**, **decreased appetite**, and **increased sleeping**. If you notice any of these signs, consult with your veterinarian.
