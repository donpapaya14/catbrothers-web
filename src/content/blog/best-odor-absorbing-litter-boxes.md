## Understanding Odor-Producing Bacteria in Cat Litter
The science behind **ammonia-producing bacteria** in cat litter is complex. According to the [American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA)](https://www.aspca.org), these bacteria thrive in warm, moist environments, producing **ammonia** as a byproduct. This process is accelerated by **temperature** and **moisture**, making it essential to manage these factors to reduce odor.

## The Importance of Litter Box Size and Placement
Choosing the right litter box size and placement is crucial for **odor control**. According to [International Cat Care 2019](https://icatcare.org), a litter box should be at least **1.5 times** the length of your cat. Placement in a **well-ventilated area** can also minimize odor.

## DIY Odor-Absorbing Solutions for Cat Litter Boxes
Creating your own odor-absorbing solutions is easy. Mix **1 cup of baking soda** with **1 cup of activated charcoal** and sprinkle it in the litter box. As suggested by [Cat World 2018](https://www.catworld.co.uk), this natural solution can help reduce odor.

## Tips for Maintaining a Fresh and Odor-Free Cat Litter Box
To maintain a fresh and odor-free cat litter box:
1. **Scoop the litter box daily**.
2. **Change the litter completely every 7-10 days**.
3. **Clean the litter box with mild soap and water**.
As advised by [PetMD 2020](https://www.petmd.com), regular maintenance is key to reducing odor.
