## Introduction to Cat Water Fountains
When it comes to **cat hydration**, cats prefer **running water** over still water due to their evolutionary history. According to Dr. Ian Dunbar's work on feline behavior and instinct (2019), cats in the wild often drink from streams or rivers, which has led to a natural preference for moving water.

## Why Cats Prefer Running Water
This preference is rooted in the cat's natural instinct to hunt and survive. A study by the [University of California, Davis](https://www.ucdavis.edu) found that cats are more likely to drink from a source that mimics the sound and movement of running water. This is because, in the wild, running water is often a sign of freshness and purity.

## Benefits of Cat Water Fountains
Using **cat water fountains** can have numerous health benefits for your cat, including reduced stress and improved hydration. A study by the [University of California, Davis](https://www.ucdavis.edu) (2015) found that cats that drank from running water sources had lower levels of stress and anxiety. Additionally, the [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org) recommends providing cats with access to fresh, running water to promote overall health and wellbeing.

## Cat Water Fountain Maintenance Tips
To keep your **cat water fountain** clean and functioning properly, follow these steps:
1. Change the water daily to prevent bacterial growth.
2. Clean the fountain every week with a mixture of water and white vinegar.
3. Replace the filter every 1-2 months, depending on usage.

## Choosing the Right Cat Water Fountain Size
When choosing a **cat water fountain**, consider the number of cats you have and the available space. According to Dr. Karen Becker, a cat behavior expert, a good rule of thumb is to provide at least 1 gallon of water per cat. For example, if you have two cats, a 2-gallon fountain would be a good choice.

## Cat Water Fountain Safety Precautions
While **cat water fountains** can be a great way to promote hydration and reduce stress, there are some potential safety hazards to be aware of. According to the [American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA)](https://www.aspca.org), electrical shock and drowning are two potential risks associated with cat water fountains. To prevent these hazards, make sure to:
1. Place the fountain in a safe location, away from electrical outlets and cords.
2. Supervise your cat when they are using the fountain.
3. Make sure the fountain is stable and secure to prevent it from tipping over.
