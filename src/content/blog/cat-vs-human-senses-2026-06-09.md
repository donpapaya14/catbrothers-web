## Visual Acuity Comparison

Cats and humans perceive the world differently due to distinct visual capabilities. While humans have **20/20 vision** as the standard for normal acuity, cats have approximately **20/100 to 20/200 vision** (American Academy of Ophthalmology, 2020). This means what a human sees clearly at 100 feet, a cat would only see at 20 feet.

Key differences:
- **Color perception**: Humans see a full spectrum, while cats primarily detect blues and yellows.
- **Peripheral vision**: Cats have a 200-degree field vs. humans' 180 degrees.
- **Motion detection**: Cats spot movement at 1/6th the speed humans require.

## Olfactory Sensitivity Comparison

A cat's sense of smell is **14 times stronger** than humans' (Scientific American, 2018). They possess:
- **200 million olfactory receptors** (vs. 5-6 million in humans)
- **Jacobson's organ** for analyzing pheromones

Practical implications:
1. Cats detect food spoilage 3-4 days before humans
2. They identify individual humans by scent within 30 seconds of meeting
3. Stress responses occur at 1/10th the odor concentration humans require

## Low Light Vision Capabilities

Cats need just **1/6th the light** humans do for navigation (National Geographic, 2020). Their **tapetum lucidum** reflects light through retinas twice, explaining eye shine in darkness. However, they sacrifice:
- Color accuracy in dim conditions
- Close-up focus (can't see within 1 foot clearly)

## Hearing Thresholds

At 1 kHz frequency, cats hear sounds at **-15 dB** versus humans' **0 dB** threshold (Acoustics Today, 2017). For perspective:
- A cat hears a mouse's heartbeat at 3 feet
- Human whispering (30 dB) sounds like normal speech to cats

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can cats see in complete darkness?
No, cats require some light. They need just **15% of the light humans do** thanks to rod-dominated retinas and tapetum lucidum (Journal of Feline Medicine, 2021). Total darkness renders them as blind as humans.

### Why do cats smell everything?
Cats gather more information through scent than vision. Their vomeronasal organ detects pheromones at concentrations **100x lower** than human smell thresholds (Cornell Feline Health Center, 2020).

### How far can cats hear?
Cats detect sounds up to **1.5 miles away** in ideal conditions. Their pinnae rotate 180 degrees to pinpoint sources within 3 inches accuracy (University of Lincoln, 2018).

### Do cats see TV differently?
Yes. While humans perceive smooth motion at 60Hz, cats need **100+ Hz refresh rates** to see TV as continuous action. This explains why some cats ignore screens while others react.

### Can cats smell human emotions?
Indirectly. Cats detect **adrenaline and cortisol** in sweat at concentrations as low as 0.001 ppm (PLOS ONE, 2020). They associate these with stress but don't comprehend emotions conceptually.
