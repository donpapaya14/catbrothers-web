## Introduction to Wet vs Dry Cat Food: Veterinary Science
When it comes to deciding between **wet vs dry cat food**, many cat owners rely on **veterinary science** to make an informed decision. The main keyword, **Wet vs Dry Cat Food Veterinary Science**, is a crucial aspect of **feline nutrition**. According to a 2017 study by the [National Academy of Sciences](https://www.nap.edu), there is no association between the type of food and kidney disease.

## Debunking the Myth: No Association Between Wet/Dry Food and Kidney Disease
A study by the [National Academy of Sciences](https://www.nap.edu) in 2017 found that feeding cats a diet high in moisture does not reduce the risk of **kidney disease**. In fact, the study concluded that the risk of kidney disease is more closely related to factors such as **age**, **sex**, and **genetics**.

## Wet Food for Weight Management: The University of Pennsylvania Study
A 2015 study by the [University of Pennsylvania School of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.upenn.edu) found that cats fed a **wet food diet** had better **weight management** and reduced the risk of **obesity-related diseases**. The study suggested that **wet food** may be a more effective way to manage weight in cats due to its higher **moisture content** and lower **carbohydrate** levels.

## The Role of Taurine in Wet vs Dry Cat Food: An Examination of 3 Studies
**Taurine** is an essential amino acid in **feline nutrition**. According to a study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) in 2002, cats require a diet rich in **taurine** to maintain good health. Another study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19393153) in 2011 found that **wet food** generally contains higher levels of **taurine** than **dry food**.

Yes, you can mix **wet** and **dry food** to provide a varied diet for your cat. However, ensure that the combined diet meets the nutritional guidelines set by the [Association of American Feed Control Officials](https://www.aafco.org).
