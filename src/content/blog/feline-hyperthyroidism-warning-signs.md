## Introduction to Feline Hyperthyroidism
Feline hyperthyroidism symptoms can be subtle, but early detection is crucial. According to the American Association of Feline Practitioners (2020), **hyperthyroidism affects approximately 10% of cats over 10 years old**. Regular check-ups with a veterinarian can help identify the condition early on, improving treatment outcomes.

## Subtle Weight Loss Symptoms
Identifying subtle weight loss in cats can be challenging. A study by the Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine (2018) found that **regular weight monitoring is essential**, as it can help detect weight loss associated with hyperthyroidism. Here are some steps to monitor your cat's weight:
1. Weigh your cat every 2-3 months
2. Use a digital scale for accuracy
3. Record the weight and track changes over time

## Behavioral Changes in Cats with Hyperthyroidism
Cats with hyperthyroidism often exhibit behavioral changes, such as **increased vocalization**, restlessness, and aggression. A study by the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2019) reported that these changes can be subtle and may not always be immediately noticeable.

## Grooming and Coat Changes
Hyperthyroidism can affect a cat's grooming habits and coat condition. According to a study by the Journal of Veterinary Dermatology (2017), **cats with hyperthyroidism may exhibit poor coat condition**, thinning of the hair, and increased shedding.

## Diagnostic Tests and Interpretation
Diagnostic tests, such as T4 and free T4 tests, can help diagnose hyperthyroidism in cats. The International Cat Care organization (2022) recommends the following steps for interpreting test results:
1. Consult with a veterinarian to determine the best course of action
2. Review the test results and medical history
3. Discuss treatment options and develop a plan
