## Introduction to Sterilized Cat Nutrition
The best diet for **sterilized cats** after spaying or neutering is crucial for maintaining their overall health and wellbeing. According to the [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu/), a cat's metabolism and energy expenditure are significantly affected by sterilization, resulting in a **reduction in caloric needs** of up to **20-30%**.

## Caloric Needs After Sterilization
Research by the [National Research Council (NRC)](https://www.nap.edu/) in 2006 found that sterilized cats require fewer calories due to a decrease in their metabolic rate. This reduction in caloric needs is essential to prevent obesity and related health issues. For example, a study published in the [Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association](https://avmajournals.avma.org/) in 2018 reported that **57% of spayed or neutered cats** are overweight or obese.

## Impact of Sterilization on Protein Requirements
The [American College of Veterinary Nutrition (ACVN)](https://www.acvn.org/) states that sterilization can affect a cat's protein requirements, potentially leading to reduced muscle mass. It is essential to maintain adequate protein intake to prevent muscle loss and support overall health. A study by [Tufts University](https://www.tufts.edu/) in 2019 found that **high-protein diets** can help mitigate muscle loss in sterilized cats.

## Key Nutrient Adjustments for Sterilized Cats
The [Feline Nutrition Society (FNS)](https://www.feline-nutrition.org/) recommends adjusting the nutrient content of a sterilized cat's diet to include increased **vitamin E** and **omega-3 fatty acid** content. These nutrients play a crucial role in maintaining skin and coat health, as well as supporting immune function. For example, a study published in the [Journal of Animal Science](https://academic.oup.com/jas) in 2020 found that **omega-3 fatty acids** can help reduce inflammation in cats.

## Feeding Guidelines for Sterilized Cats
The [Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/) provides practical feeding guidelines for sterilized cats, including recommendations for **meal frequency**, **portion sizes**, and **feeding schedules**. For example, a study published in the [Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/19390987) in 2015 found that **feeding smaller, more frequent meals** can help regulate blood sugar levels in sterilized cats.
