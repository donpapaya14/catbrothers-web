## Introduction to Foods Toxic to Cats
According to the **ASPCA's Animal Poison Control Center (2022)**, there are several **foods toxic to cats** that can cause serious health issues. As a responsible cat owner, it's essential to be aware of these foods to ensure your cat's safety. The **main keyword** here is **foods toxic to cats**, and we will also discuss **ASPCA cat food safety** and **cat nutrition**.

## The Top 10 Most Toxic Foods to Cats
The top 10 most toxic foods to cats include:
1. **Chocolate**: contains theobromine, which can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and increased heart rate.
2. **Onions**: contain a compound that can damage a cat's red blood cells, leading to anemia.
3. **Grapes**: can cause kidney failure in cats.
4. **Macadamia nuts**: can cause weakness, depression, and tremors.
5. **Avocado**: the pit, skin, and leaves contain a toxic compound called persin.
6. **Caffeine**: can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and increased heart rate.
7. **Raw or undercooked meat, eggs, and fish**: can contain bacteria like Salmonella and E. coli.
8. **Xylitol**: a sugar substitute commonly used in sugar-free gum and candy.
9. **Yeast dough**: can expand in a cat's stomach, causing pain and potentially leading to a life-threatening emergency.
10. **Milk and dairy products**: can cause digestive issues in cats, as they are lactose intolerant.

These foods are considered toxic to cats, and it's crucial to keep them out of reach. For more information, visit the [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org) website.

## The Dangers of Overfeeding Cats with Table Scraps
Overfeeding cats with **table scraps** can lead to obesity, pancreatitis, and other health issues. According to the **Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018)**, a study found that 25% of cats are overweight or obese, which can lead to a range of health problems. To prevent this, it's essential to:
1. Feed your cat a balanced and nutritious diet.
2. Avoid giving table scraps.
3. Monitor your cat's weight and adjust their food intake accordingly.
