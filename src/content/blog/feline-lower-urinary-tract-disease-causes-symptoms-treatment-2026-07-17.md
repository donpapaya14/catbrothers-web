## What is FLUTD and its Common Symptoms
Feline lower urinary tract disease (FLUTD) is a common condition affecting **70% of cats** over their lifetime, according to the American Animal Hospital Association (2020). Symptoms include straining, accidents outside the litter box, **blood in urine**, and frequency of urination. 
Statistics show that **3% of cats** develop FLUTD each year, with **males more prone** to the condition.

## Causes of FLUTD: Nutrition, Environment, and Genetics
The role of diet, environmental factors, and genetics in the development of FLUTD is crucial. A **moisture-rich diet** can help prevent FLUTD, as stated in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery (2018). 
1. Feed a balanced diet
2. Provide a clean environment
3. Monitor genetic predisposition

## Diagnosing FLUTD: Tests and Procedures
Diagnostic tests for FLUTD include **urinalysis**, **imaging studies**, and **blood tests**, as explained by the International Society of Feline Medicine (2019). 
| Test | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Urinalysis | Identify infection or inflammation |
| Imaging studies | Detect stones or tumors |
| Blood tests | Evaluate kidney function |

## Effective Treatment Options for FLUTD
Treatment options for FLUTD include **dietary changes**, **medical therapy**, and **surgery**, as discussed in Veterinary Clinics of North America: Small Animal Practice (2017). 
Prompt treatment is essential to prevent long-term damage. 
1. Dietary changes: feed a balanced, moisture-rich diet
2. Medical therapy: administer antibiotics or pain relief medication
3. Surgery: remove stones or tumors

## Preventing FLUTD: Tips for Cat Owners
To prevent FLUTD, cat owners can take the following steps:
1. Feed a **balanced diet**
2. Provide **adequate water**
3. Maintain a **clean litter box**, as recommended by Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine (2020)
