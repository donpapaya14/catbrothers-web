## How to Choose the Right Size and Type
When selecting an automatic litter box, consider the number of cats, litter type, and space constraints. According to the [American Animal Hospital Association](https://www.aaha.org/), 2019, **cats prefer large, uncovered litter boxes** with low sides and non-slip surfaces. Here are some guidelines to follow:
1. **Measure the space**: Ensure the litter box fits comfortably in the designated area.
2. **Consider the litter type**: Choose a litter box compatible with your preferred litter type.
3. **Think about the number of cats**: Select a litter box that can accommodate multiple cats, if necessary.

## Maintenance and Troubleshooting Tips
To maintain and troubleshoot common issues with automatic litter boxes, follow these tips:
1. **Regularly clean the litter box**: Scoop out solid waste daily and change the litter completely every 7-10 days.
2. **Check for jammed motors**: Regularly inspect the litter box for any blockages or jammed motors.
3. **Control odor**: Use odor-absorbing filters or sprays to minimize unpleasant smells.
As per [Manufacturer instructions](https://www.litterrobot.com/) and [user forums](https://www.reddit.com/r/catlitter/), 2022, **regular maintenance** can prevent common issues.

## Health Benefits and Hygiene Considerations
Using an automatic litter box can have several health benefits for cats, including reduced **odor and bacteria growth**. According to the [Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-feline-medicine-and-surgery), 2018, **cats are prone to urinary tract infections** if their litter boxes are not kept clean. To maintain hygiene:
1. **Wash your hands**: After handling the litter box or cat waste.
2. **Disinfect the litter box**: Regularly disinfect the litter box to prevent bacterial growth.
